14. what is the connection of research variable and reasearch problem? ​


Answer:

There are many kinds of variables, but the most common distinction is between independent variables (the presumed causes) and dependent variables (the presumed effects). A research problem is a question about the relation of two or more variables, which frames a quantitative study.

Answer:

A good question as variables are driven by the problem, gap, purpose, and research questions/hypotheses for quantitative research. Variables come in several forms, but generally are identified as the independent (predictor) (IV) and dependent (outcome) (DV) variable. In terms of the connection between these two variable types and the problem, I teach mentees and clients that the DV is the problem to be addressed, which is the outcome of the study, and that the IV is the gap that exists in the literature to be researched as a possible predictor of the DV, in that, as the IV changes, so would the DV. Once established in the problem statement, these variables would persist through the purpose, research questions, hypotheses, and design elements for quantitative research.

21. how is quantitative reasearch differ from qualitative research?​


Answer:

Quantitative research deals with numbers and statistics, while qualitative research deals with words and meanings. Quantitative methods allow you to test a hypothesis by systematically collecting and analyzing data, while qualitative methods allow you to explore ideas and experiences in depth.
