25. the mass of a golf club is 0.6 kg.suppose the club is traveling 2.0 m/s.What's the club's kinetic energy?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for KE

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{0.6 kg})(\text{2.0 m/s})^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{KE = \text{1.2 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the kinetic energy of the golf ball is 1.2 J.

26. explanation about swinging a golf club​


Answer:

Swinging the Club. Start your backswing. The backswing is where you lift the club back from its starting position and bring it above your head. Try to rotate the torso on the backswing by shifting weight from the ball of your front foot to the ball of your back foot.

At the top, the hips are turned only half as far as the shoulders. Your left arm should remain straight, but not rigid, and your right elbow should point to the ground. The hands should swing back to 11 o'clock, with the hands and arms under the club, supporting its weight
