1. Public Administration vs Private Administration


Public Administration versus Private Administration

The difference between public administration and private administration is that while public administration is a political activity that is run by the government with a service motive, private administration is a business activity that is run by a group of individuals not related to government in any way whatsoever with the sole motive of profit.

Main differences between Public Administration and Private Administration

Public administration is set up by the government. While Private administration is not set up by the government instead it is set up by a group of private individuals.

Public administration follows a bureaucratic approach while private administration follows an egalitarian approach.

Public administration follows a pluralistic approach of decision making while private administration follows a monopolistic approach to decision making.

Public administration has a service and welfare motive. Private administration is profit-oriented i.e the sole motive here is profit maximization.

Public administration is accountable to the general public while private administration is accountable to its employees.

Public Administration earns revenue through taxes, fines, fees, etc. while private administration earns revenue through profit.

Administrators working in a public administration are paid salaries while administrators working in a private administration earn their remuneration through profits.

Public administration is political activity while private administration is a business activity.

Universal truth is something about all the facts about it, rather than things that are imagined. An example of a universal truth is science — laws that apply through out the entire universe is universal. Relative truth is truth about mere phenomena.  An example of a relative truth is something that only applies to you — Chinese food causes stomach aches.

Public truth is requiring the truth to be revealed to those who hold the right to be informed of it, with due regard for matters that are confidential or best not revealed for a good reason. An example of Public Truth: In victory, we accept the bestowal of meaning and elevated purpose. Private truth uses its theoretical argument to illuminate an array of puzzling social phenomena. An example of Private Truth: In defeat, we console ourselves that public loss is ultimately meaningless and impermanent.

Empirical truth states that experience and observation should be the means of gaining knowledge. On the other hand, Rational truth believes that opinions and actions should be based on reason rather than on religious beliefs or emotions. An example of empirical truth: Scientists formed a hypothesis to compare population growth of a species of bats vs the weather changes. Example of rational truth: A consumer with a goal to buy the most healthy muffin must know all the ingredients of the muffin, how each ingredient was cultivated and processed and the health impacts of all of this and be able to logically formulate this into an option of healthiness

28. create a short script about 2 friends talking about comparing the public school vs private school​


Answer:

Title: The Debate

Setting: A park with two friends sitting on a bench.

Characters:

JON - a student from a private school

JESS - a student from a public school

(Jon and Jess are sitting on a bench in a park, enjoying the warm weather.)

Jon: So, Jess, how's school been treating you?

Jess: It's been tough, but I manage.

Jon: Yeah, I understand. I'm from a private school, and it's not exactly a walk in the park either.

Jess: (laughs) You guys have it easy. Private schools have fewer students, more resources, and better teachers.

Jon: I don't think that's entirely true, Jess. Private schools do have more resources, but that's because tuition fees are high. Public schools have more students, and that's not always a bad thing. It teaches us how to interact with people from different backgrounds.

Jess: (nodding) That's true, but in private schools, the teachers can give us more attention because there are fewer students.

Jon: I understand, but at the end of the day, it's about the quality of education. Public schools may have larger class sizes, but they still produce great students who are competitive in the job market.

Jess: (smiling) You have a point there, Jon. Public schools may not have the same resources as private schools, but we're still making it work.

Jon: (smiling) And that's what matters. As long as we're getting a good education, it doesn't matter which school we're in.

Jess: (nodding) Absolutely. It's not about the school; it's about the student.

(They both laugh and continue to enjoy the rest of their day in the park.)
