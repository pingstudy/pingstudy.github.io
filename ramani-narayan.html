Answer:

Explanation:

Krishnan is a modern-day hero of India. He gave up his career as a chef after he witnessed the hardships being experienced by his countrymen. He worked for the Taj Hotels in Bangalore where he became an award-winning chef. He was also shortlisted for a job in Switzerland.

It was in 2002 when he decided to leave his job. A year after, in 2003, he founded the Ashkaya Trust organization which fed the homeless and mentally-disabled in Madurai.

For his selfless works, he was named as one of CNN Heroes 2010 list.

4. bakit dapat isaalang-alang ang mga narayan sa pag-iibak ng pagkain​


Ang imbakan ng pagkain ay isa sa mahalagang bagay na dapat isaalang-alang natin upang mapanatili ang kaligtasan at kalinisan ng ating pagkain. Ang tamang imbakan ng pagkain ay protektado sa karumihan at kasiraan ng pagkain. Ang pag-imbak ng pagkain ay nakakatulong rin sa pagiwas ng pagkakalason ng pagkain at nakakatulong rin ito mapanatili ang atin kalusugan. Kapag hindi natin nalagay ang ating pagkain sa tamang lugar, mas madaragdag ang posibilidad na umakit ang mga mikroorganismo sa ating pagkain at masira ito. Samakatwid, pilit natin itapon ang nasira na pagkain at sayangin ito. May mga simpleng bagay tayo maisagawa upang maiwasan natin sayangin ang mga pagkain at gastos at mapanatili ang kaligtasan ng pagkain. Sa maayos at malinis na paraan ng pag-iimbak ng pagkain, nananatili ng lasa, amoy, ang pagkahabi at iba pa. Kinakailangan ito isaalang-alang ng bawat tao upang mas manatili ang kaligtasan ng pagkain at ang kaligtasan n gating kalusugan.

11. Sino ang naging pinuno ng prithvi narayan shah​


Answer:

Prithvi Nārāyaṇ Shah, (born 1723?—died 1775), member of the ruling Shah family of the Gurkha (Gorkha) principality, Nepal, who conquered the three Malla kingdoms of Kāthmāndu, Pātan, and Bhādgaon in 1769 and consolidated them to found the modern state of Nepal. He also established the capital of Nepal at Kāthmāndu.

Maharajadhiraja Prithivi Narayan Shah (1723–1775;[4] Nepali: पृथ्वीनारायण शाह) was the last ruler of the Gorkha Kingdom and first monarch of Kingdom of Nepal (also called Kingdom of Gorkha). Prithvi Narayan Shah is credited for starting the campaign for the unification of Nepal.[5]

Shah proclaimed the newly unified Kingdom of Nepal as Asal Hindustan ("Real Land of Hindus") due to North India being ruled by the Islamic Mughal rulers. He also referred to the rest of Northern India as Mughlan (Country of Mughals).[6] Prithvi Narayan Shah is commonly, though not formally, considered the Father of the Nation in Nepal.
