1. who is Guido of Arezzo?​


Answer:

Guido of Arezzo was an Italian music theorist of the Medieval era. He is regarded as the inventor of modern staff notation that replaced neumatic notation. His text, the Micrologus, was the second most widely distributed treatise on music in the Middle Ages.

29. what is Guido arezzo's contribution to musical notation


Answer:

The first of Guido's major developments in the realm of music theory and education was his codification of the hexachord system. In his system, the notes of the musical gamut were described in interlocking six-note segments, known as hexachords, all of which shared the same interval pattern.
