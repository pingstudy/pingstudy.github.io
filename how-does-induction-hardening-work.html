4. how does charging by friction conduction and induction happen?​


Answer:

Charging by friction, conduction, and induction are all methods of transferring electric charge between objects.

Charging by friction occurs when two objects are rubbed together, and the friction between them causes electrons to be transferred from one object to the other. One object becomes positively charged because it has lost electrons, while the other becomes negatively charged because it has gained electrons.

Charging by conduction occurs when a charged object is brought into contact with a neutral object. Electrons flow from the charged object to the neutral object, equalizing the charge. If the charged object is positively charged, it will transfer some of its excess protons to the neutral object, making it positively charged as well. If the charged object is negatively charged, it will transfer some of its excess electrons to the neutral object, making it negatively charged as well.

Charging by induction occurs when a charged object is brought close to a neutral object. The charged object polarizes the neutral object, causing electrons to shift within it. If the charged object is negatively charged, it will repel electrons in the neutral object, causing them to accumulate on the side closest to the charged object. This creates a region of negative charge on the neutral object closest to the charged object, and a region of positive charge on the far side of the neutral object. If the charged object is positively charged, the opposite will occur, with a region of positive charge closest to the charged object and a region of negative charge on the far side of the neutral object.

In summary, charging by friction involves the transfer of electrons between objects due to friction, charging by conduction involves the transfer of charge between objects that are in direct contact, and charging by induction involves the polarization of a neutral object by a charged object.

27. how molten rock formed and what type of rock does it create when it hardens​


Answer:

Explanation:

1: Answer: Extrusive igneous: Molten lava cools and hardens to form rock.

Intrusive igneous: Molten magma cools and hardens to form rock.

Metamorphic: Physical structure and chemical composition of rock are changed by immense heat and pressure.

Sedimentary : Compaction and cementation bind particles together at the bottom of a water basin to form rock.

Extrusive igneous rocks formed on the earth surface when the lava eruption on the earth surface and then cools and hardens to form rock. Intrusive igneous rock formed below the earth surface as subsurface emplacements of magma when this magma cools and formed harden rocks. Sedimentary rocks formed through the process of weathering of the preexisting rock, transportation of weathering products, deposition of the weathering products in basin, then followed by the compaction and cementation of the sediments within the basin. Metamorphic rock formed due to the physical and chemical alteration by the immense temperature and pressure effects of the preexisting igneous and sedimentary rock.

2: Answer: Extrusive igneous : Obsidian

Intrusive igneous : Granite

Metamorphic : Marble.

Sedimentary: Sandstone

Extrusive igneous rocks formed on the earth surface when the lava eruption on the earth surface and cools vary rapidly so formed fine grain rocks. Obsidian is a fine grained rock and formed in earth surface so it is extrusive igneous rock. Intrusive igneous rock formed below the earth surface as subsurface emplacements of magma when this magma cools very slowly and formed larger grains. Granite has a large crystal so it forms in slow cooling and below the subsurface so it's a intrusive rock. Marble formed when the limestone sedimentary rock suffer high temperature and pressure and limestone metamorphosed to marble. Sandstone is a sedimentary rocks which contain sand size grains of mineral, rock and the organic materials and also contain the cementing materials which bind the sand grains togethers and it also contain the matrix of clay and silt size particles.

3: Answer: how they are formed.

The rock types of Earth's crust are classified on the basis of their geological formation i.e., the process they are formed. three types of rock on the Earth's crust are sedimentary formed by the erosion, deposition, compaction and cementation of the sediments; igneous rocks formed from the cooling of the magma/lava; metamorphic rocks formed from the changes in the physical and chemical alteration of the preexisting igneous and sedimentary rock by the immense heat and pressure. So the three rock types of Earth's crust does not classified on the basis of mineral composition and relative age.
