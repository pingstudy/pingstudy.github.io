6. Suppose that in a population of psychology students of 1600, 500 are 1st years, 400 are 2nd years, 380 are 3rd years, 270 are 4th years, and 50 are irregular students. What is the probability of getting a sample that is a 2nd year student or irregular student?


Answer:

The total number of students in the population is 1600, and the number of 2nd year and irregular students is 400 + 50 = 450.

Therefore, the probability of randomly selecting a 2nd year or irregular student from the population is:

P(2nd year or irregular) = (number of 2nd year or irregular students) / (total number of students)

P(2nd year or irregular) = 450 / 1600

P(2nd year or irregular) = 0.28125

So the probability of getting a sample that is a 2nd year student or irregular student is 0.28125, or 28.125%.

9. Find the amount of the following payments at the end of 5th year, 3000p at the end of 1st year, 4500p at the end of 2nd year and 6000p at the end of 4th year if money worth 12% per annum.


Answer:

To find the amount of the payments at the end of the 5th year, you will need to compound the interest on the payments for the first four years. Here's how you can do that:

Calculate the interest on the payment for the first year: 3000 * 12% = 360

Add the interest to the payment to find the total amount at the end of the first year: 3000 + 360 = 3360

Calculate the interest on the total amount at the end of the first year for the second year: 3360 * 12% = 403.2

Add the interest to the total amount at the end of the first year to find the total amount at the end of the second year: 3360 + 403.2 = 3763.2

Calculate the interest on the total amount at the end of the second year for the third year: 3763.2 * 12% = 4515.84

Calculate the interest on the total amount at the end of the third year for the fourth year: 4515.84 * 12% = 5419.008

Add the interest to the total amount at the end of the third year to find the total amount at the end of the fourth year: 4515.84 + 5419.008 = 9934.848

Add the payment for the fourth year to the total amount at the end of the fourth year: 9934.848 + 6000 = 15934.848

Calculate the interest on the total amount at the end of the fourth year for the fifth year: 15934.848 * 12% = 1915.181

Add the interest to the total amount at the end of the fourth year to find the total amount at the end of the fifth year: 15934.848 + 1915.181 = 17849.028

So, the total amount at the end of the fifth year would be 17849.028. This is the amount of the payments at the end of the fifth year.

26. What is the present worth of Php 250 annuity starting at the end of 3rd year and continuing in the end of 4th year, if the annual rate is 5 %?


Explanation:

To compute for the present worth of the annuity, we can use the formula for the present value of an annuity:

P = A x [(1 - (1 + r)^(-n)) / r]

where:

P = present worth or present value

A = amount of annuity payment

r = interest rate per period

n = number of periods

In this case, the amount of annuity payment is Php 250, the interest rate is 5%, and the number of periods is 2 (since the payments start at the end of the 3rd year and continue in the end of the 4th year).

Using these values, we can compute for the present worth as follows:

P = Php 250 x [(1 - (1 + 0.05)^(-2)) / 0.05]

P = Php 250 x [(1 - 0.90573) / 0.05]

P = Php 250 x (0.09427 / 0.05)

P = Php 469.38

Therefore, the present worth of Php 250 annuity starting at the end of 3rd year and continuing in the end of 4th year, if the annual rate is 5%, is Php 469.38.
