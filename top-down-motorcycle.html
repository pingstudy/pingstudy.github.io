3. what is the energy of a motorcycle moving slowly at the top of a hill


Answer:

It has gravitational potential energy and kinetic energy

Explanation:

One of the energies shown in this example is the gravitational potential energy.

Gravitational potential energy is the stored energy from the  Earth's gravitational pull on the object.  

Formula

GPE = mgh

where

GPE     is the gravitational potential energy, the unit is in Joules (J)

m         is the mass, the unit is in kg

h          is the height of the object, the unit is in meter

The gravitational potential energy is directly proportional to the mass, gravitational pull of the Earth and the mass of the object. This means that there is energy due to the vertical position of the object.

Another form of energy is kinetic energy. This is the energy of an object when it is moving. This means that there is no kinetic energy when there is no  motion.

Formula

KE = ¹/₂mv²

where

KE     is the kinetic energy, the unit is in Joules (J)

m      is also the mass of the object, the unit is in kg

v      is the speed or velocity of the moving object, the unit is in m/s

The formula for kinetic energy states that there is kinetic energy when the mass of the object is in motion.

Now, since the motorcycle is moving slowly at the top of a hill, this means that the motorcycle has kinetic energy because it is moving at a slow speed. It also has gravitational potential energy due to its height or  position above the ground.

9. After your motorcycle breaks down, you start pushing it to the nearest repair shop. How will you compare the force you exerted on the motorcycle to the force exerted by the motorcycle on you?


Answer:

In order for the motorcycle to reach the nearest repair shop by pushing, we need to take a look at Newton's three laws of motion to understand the force exerted on the motorcycle and the force exerted by the motorcycle.

First Law (Inertia). Depending on the terrain, the amount of friction on the motorcycle wheels against the ground may require more force to push the vehicle. The greater the friction, the more force is needed to push the motorcycle as it will also exert greater force against the person (this is the third law of motion).

Second Law (Law of Force and Acceleration). The motorcycle wheels decreases the overall resistance of the motorcycle against the ground. When force is acted on the vehicle, the force will come to a point when it it greater that the total friction the wheel is exerting against the ground. This unequal distribution of forces moves the vehicle forward. Largely dependent on the size of the vehicle, more force would be needed to make it accelerate towards the shop. Greater force is needed to accelerate an Ninja than a Mio 125.

Third Law (Law of Action and Reaction). The net force exerted by the motorcycle against the force of the person pushing it depends on the mass the motorcycle, the friction on the wheels against the ground and even the lubrication of the wheels. The direction of this force is directly opposite to the push exerted by the person when pushing the vehicle.

15. what is the energy of a motorcycle going fast midway down a hill?


Answer:

The motorcycle going fast midway down a hill contains both potential and kinetic energy.

Explanation:

An object contains energy whether it is at rest or it is already moving.

The energy of an object when it is at rest on a certain elevated place is called potential energy. Potential energy is affected by the height of elevation where the object is resting, the mass of the object, and the gravitational acceleration.

Here is the mathematical expression of potential energy:

PE = mgh    where

PE stands for potential energy, m is mass, g is gravitational acceleration, and h is the height.

The energy of an object that is already moving is called kinetic energy. Kinetic energy is affected by the object's velocity, and object's mass.

Here's the mathematical expression of potential energy:

KE = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] mv²  where

KE stands for kinetic energy, m is mass, and v is velocity.

As the object move from rest, its potential energy is converted to kinetic energy.

From the definition above, kinetic energy is the energy of a moving object, and as the object moves, its potential energy is constantly converted into kinetic energy until it stops.

For the case of the motorcycle going fast down a hill, the motorcycle contains both potential and kinetic energy. This is because it is moving (kinetic energy) midway down the hill (potential energy still being converted to kinetic energy). Since the motorcycle is still moving and hasn't stopped yet, hence, there is still potential energy in the motorcycle but it's being converted.

18. what is the energy of a motorcycle moving slowly at the top of a hill


Answer:

A motorcycle moving slowly at the top of a hill has kinetic energy.

Explanation:

For this question, we can choose from kinetic energy or potential energy. Let us first define these energies so that we can analyze which energy is more appropriate for this situation.

Kinetic energy - the energy of an object because of its motion

Mathematical expression: KE = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] m[tex]v^2[/tex] wherem = mass of objectv = velocity of object

Potential energy - the energy of an object due to its position, arrangement or state.

Mathematical expression: PE = mgh where

m = mass of objectg = gravitational accelerationh = height

Potential energy can be transformed into kinetic energy. For example, a ball is placed on the top of a table. As the ball stays on the table, it posses potential energy. The amount of potential energy of a ball depends on the ball's mass, the height of the table, and the gravitational acceleration. If the ball drops from the table down to the ground, then its potential energy is converted into kinetic energy. As the ball moves downward, potential energy decreases, and the kinetic energy increases.

For our situation, the motorcycle is already moving. Even if it is moving at a slow speed, it is still moving. In addition, it is not stated that the motorcycle is moving down from the top of the hill; it only said that it is moving slowly at the top of the hill, therefore, no height is involved. Hence, the energy of this object is kinetic energy.
