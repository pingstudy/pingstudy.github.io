19. For Fluid pipe Formula An insulated steam pipe located where the ambient temperature is 32°C, has an inside diameter of 50 mm with 10 mm thick wall. The outside diameter of the corrugated asbestos insulation is 125 mm and the surface coefficient of still air, h0 = 12 W/m2 -K. Inside the pipe is steam having a temperature of 150 °C with film coefficient h=6000W/m2k thermal conductivity of pipe and asbestos insulation are 45 and 0.12w/mk respectively. So If the heat loss per unit becomes 100w/m determine the required thickness of the pipe?


Answer:

We can use the formula for heat transfer through a cylindrical wall:

Q/t = 2πkL(T1-T2) / [ln(r2/r1) / h1 + (r2-r1) / k + ln(r3/r2) / h2]

where Q/t is the heat loss per unit length, k is the thermal conductivity, L is the length of the pipe, T1 and T2 are the temperatures of the inside and outside surfaces of the wall, r1 and r3 are the inner and outer radii of the pipe, and r2 is the outer radius of the insulation.

We can rearrange this formula to solve for the required thickness of the pipe:

r1 = (r2^2 - L / (2πkL(T1-T2)) * [ln(r2/r3) / h2 + (r2-r3) / k + ln(r2/r1) / h1]

Plugging in the given values:

r1 = (0.0625^2 - 1 / (2π * 45 * 0.1 * (150-32))) * [ln(0.125/0.145) / 12 + (0.125-0.145) / 0.12 + ln(0.125/0.05) / 6000]

r1 = 0.00598 m

Therefore, the required thickness of the pipe is:

t = r2 - r1 - d/2 = 0.125 - 0.00598 - 0.05 / 2 = 0.034 m

So the required thickness of the pipe is approximately 34 mm.
