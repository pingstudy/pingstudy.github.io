5. PA HELP PO!Ben claims that the average midi-chlorians a jedi had in his time was 15,000 with a standard deviation of 1500. Luke doesn’t believe it and claims that it is lesser than that so tested his new set of Jedi students and found out that with 12 Jedi’s they had an average of 16,500 midi-chlorians. Test if the average midi-chlorians is less than 15,000. Use 1% level of significance​


Answer:

The null hypothesis is that the average midi-chlorians of a Jedi is 15,000 and the alternate hypothesis is that the average midi-chlorians of a Jedi is less than 15,000.

The sample size is 12 and the average midi-chlorians of the sample is 16,500.

We can use the Z-test to test the hypothesis.

The Z-score is calculated as:

Z = (x - mu)/(sigma/sqrt(n))

Where x is the sample mean, mu is the population mean, sigma is the population standard deviation and n is the sample size.

Substituting the values, we get:

Z = (16,500 - 15,000)/(1500/sqrt(12)) = 1.11

The critical Z-score for 1% level of significance is -2.33.

Since 1.11 > -2.33, we cannot reject the null hypothesis.

Therefore, we can conclude that the average midi-chlorians of a Jedi is not less than 15,000 with 1% level of significance.

26. sino Ang lumikha ng songbook?​


Answer:

The creation of songbooks can be attributed to the efforts of many individuals and groups over time. Songbooks are collections of songs that are compiled and arranged in a particular format, often for use in singing or music education. The earliest known songbooks date back to the medieval period, and were used primarily in religious contexts. Over time, songbooks have been created for a variety of purposes and genres, including popular music, folk songs, and hymns.

It is difficult to pinpoint a single individual or group as the creator of the songbook, as the development of this musical format has been an ongoing process of evolution and adaptation over many centuries. However, it is clear that songbooks have played an important role in the preservation and dissemination of music throughout history, and continue to do so in the present day.
