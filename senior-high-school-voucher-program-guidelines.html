7. What is the impact of computer technology and website creation on the academic performance of senior high school students in programming courses?​


Answer:

Studies have shown that computer technology and website creation can have a positive impact on the academic performance of senior high school students in programming courses.

The use of computer technology in programming courses allows students to explore programming languages and concepts through hands-on learning experiences. Students benefit from improved problem-solving skills, logical thinking, and enhanced critical thinking skills. Computer technology provides an interactive and engaging platform for learning that encourages students to take an active approach to their education.

Website creation, in particular, allows students to apply their programming skills to real-world scenarios. It provides an opportunity for students to develop their abilities in web design, programming, and problem-solving. Students can also develop valuable teamwork and communication skills as they collaborate on website development projects.

Ultimately, the impact of computer technology and website creation on academic performance is significant. These tools help students to engage with programming concepts and acquire new skills that are applicable in everyday life. As such, computer technology and website creation are valuable educational resources that can help to improve student outcomes in programming courses.

25. Please give me guidelines for making an introduction for research.it's about "The Struggles and Adjustments of Senior High School Students during Face-to-Face classes"​


Answer

The transition from junior high school to senior high school can be a challenging time for students, as they are faced with new academic demands, social pressures, and the possibility of moving on to higher education or entering the workforce. One factor that can contribute to the struggles and adjustments of senior high school students is the mode of instruction they receive, with some students transitioning from online to face-to-face classes or vice versa.

The COVID-19 pandemic has disrupted the education of millions of students around the world, with many schools transitioning to online or hybrid learning models to mitigate the spread of the virus. While these measures have been necessary to protect the health and safety of students and staff, they have also posed significant challenges for students, teachers, and parents.

One of the most significant challenges of online learning is the lack of face-to-face interaction with teachers and peers. Research has shown that students who receive face-to-face instruction tend to perform better academically and have higher levels of engagement and motivation compared to those who receive online instruction. Additionally, face-to-face classes provide opportunities for students to participate in hands-on activities and experiential learning, which can be difficult to replicate in an online setting.

The purpose of this study is to explore the experiences of senior high school students during face-to-face classes, including the struggles and adjustments they encounter. Through this research, we aim to gain a better understanding of the challenges faced by senior high school students in this context and identify potential strategies for supporting their academic and social development. This information may be useful for educators, policymakers, and other stakeholders in promoting the success and well-being of senior high school students during this critical stage of their education.

To conduct this study, we will use a mixed-methods approach, including both quantitative and qualitative data collection. We will survey a sample of senior high school students to gather data on their experiences during face-to-face classes, including their levels of engagement, motivation, and academic performance. We will also conduct in-depth interviews with a subset of students to explore their experiences in greater detail and gather more nuanced insights into their struggles and adjustments.

One of the key questions we will seek to answer in this study is how senior high school students adjust to face-to-face classes after experiencing online or hybrid learning. We will examine the differences in academic performance, engagement, and motivation between students who received face-to-face instruction prior to the pandemic and those who received online or hybrid instruction. We will also explore the role of support systems, such as teachers, peers, and parents, in helping students adjust to face-to-face classes.

In addition to examining the academic and social experiences of senior high school students during face-to-face classes, we will also consider the impact of other factors such as socio-economic status, gender, and cultural background. Previous research has shown that these factors can influence students' academic outcomes and experiences in school, and it is important to consider their role in the struggles and adjustments of senior high school students.

Our goal is to provide a comprehensive understanding of the challenges faced by senior high school students during face-to-face classes and to identify strategies for supporting their academic and social development. This information may be useful for educators and policymakers as they work to promote the success of all students, regardless of the mode of instruction they receive.
