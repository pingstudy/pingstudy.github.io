All of the given expressions are the Difference of Two Squares

To identify if an expression is the Difference Of Two Squares, you must check:

1. Is the first and second term can be square rooted which the result is a whole number?

2. Is the operation in between the two terms subtraction (-)?

If both conditions are met, then we can say the expression is a Difference of Two Squares

The formula to factor the Difference of Two Squares is:

[tex]x^2-y^2 = (x-y)(x+y)\\or\\x^2 - y^2 = (x+y)(x-y)[/tex]

Now since we have the formula, we can now solve this.

1. [tex]x^2 - 81\\x^2 = x\\81 = 9\\x^2-81=(x-9)(x+9)[/tex]
