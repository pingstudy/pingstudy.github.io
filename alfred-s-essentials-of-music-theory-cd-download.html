5. how the theory of alfred wegener is presented


German climatologist and geophysicist who, in 1915, published as expanded version of his 1912 book The Origin of Continents and Oceans. This work was one of the first to suggest continental drift and plate tectonics. He suggested that a supercontinent he called Pangaea had existed in the past, broke up starting 200 million years ago, and that the pieces "drifted" to their present positions. He cited the fit of South America and Africa, ancient climate similarities, fossil evidence (such as the fern Glossopteris and mesosaurus), and similarity of rock structures. The American Frank Taylor had published a rather speculative paper suggesting continental drift in 1910 which, however, had attracted relatively little attention, as had previous such suggestions by Humboldt and Osmond Fisher. The book was translated to English in 1924, when in aroused hostile criticism

23. what is Alfred Marshal weaknesses about his theory?


Title : Marshall's Theory Weaknesses

Answer

Alfred Marshall's theory of economics, known as "neoclassical economics," has been criticized for several reasons. Some of its weaknesses include:

Assumptions of perfect competition: Marshall's theory assumes that markets are perfectly competitive, which is not always the case in the real world.

Static nature: Marshall's theory is based on a static view of the economy, which does not take into account dynamic changes such as technological progress and economic growth.

Limited scope: Marshall's theory is primarily focused on microeconomics, and does not address macroeconomic issues such as inflation and unemployment.

Lack of emphasis on distribution of wealth: Marshall's theory primarily focuses on efficiency and growth, and does not address issues related to the distribution of wealth and income inequality.

Lack of attention to role of government: Marshall's theory does not take into account the role of government in regulating markets and addressing.
