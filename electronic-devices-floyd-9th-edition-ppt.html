11. Study about lesson 5 - literary devices. answer the application 1 to 5 items in the ppt. write your answer on your english notebook.


LITERARY DEVICESALLEGORY: An allegory is a narrative that serves to illustrate a broader point on actual (historical) problems and/or occurrences. Usually, it's a whole book, novel, drama, and others.FORESHADOWING: Events, words, or phrases that provide the reader a hint or a suggestion about what will happen next in the tale.SATIRE: exposing human immorality or foolishness with comedy, wit, or satire.SYMBOLISM: using things or actions to convey a deeper meaning than what is immediately apparent.METAPHOR: a technique that claims that one thing is another, giving the initial topic new significance for a new understanding.HYPERBOLE: exaggerating a claim or concept to highlight a point or feeling.OXYMORON: a strategy that combines two seemingly incompatible concepts to convey a deep meaning.COLLOQUIALISM: The usage of slang and casual language is known as colloquialism. It's frequently employed by writers to give their characters and conversations a feeling of reality.FLASHBACK: An interruption in a story that portrays past events, either before the present or before the time the narration takes place, is known as a flashback. The reader is frequently provided with additional background information and specifics about certain individuals, events, narrative elements, and so forth using this approach.
