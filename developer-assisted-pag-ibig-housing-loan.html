1. Possible research question: What factors influence the low repayment rate of PAG-IBIG housing loans among its members?

2. Problem situation: PAG-IBIG housing loans have a consistently low repayment rate, with a delinquency rate of about 30% and an average arrears of Php 80,000. Many properties have been foreclosed as documented by notices of foreclosure.

3. The ideal situation is for PAG-IBIG members to pay their housing loans on time and in full to avoid delinquency and foreclosure.

4. Possible reasons for the discrepancy between what is and what should be include lack of awareness among borrowers of the consequences of non-payment, negative attitudes towards government loans, shortage of cash due to other financial needs, and social and economic factors that influence repayment.

5. Related studies on loan payment behaviors of credit cooperative members showed that social and economic factors influence repayment, but did not touch on knowledge and attitudes towards loan repayment. Studies on housing loans are limited.

6. Unanswered questions may include how to increase borrowers' awareness of loan repayment consequences, how to improve attitudes towards government loans, and how to address social and economic factors that affect repayment.

"SIMPLE INTEREST FORMULA :"

A = P(1+rt)

P = initial principal balance

A = final Amount

r = annual interest rate

t = time ( in years)

SOLUTION :

t = 15

r = 6.5%/100

= 0.065 ( decimal)

P = 1,500,000.00

A = P(1+rt)

= 1,500,000.00(1+0.065(15))

= 2,962,500.00

"COMPOUND INTEREST FORMULA :"

A = P(1+r/n)^(nt)

A = final Amount

P = initial principal balance

r = interest rate

n = number of times interest applied per time period

t = number of time periods elapsed

SOLUTION :

P = 1,500,000.00

r = 6.5%/100

= 0.065 ( decimal)

n = 12( monthly/yrs.

t = 15 ( in years)

A = P(1+r/n)^(nt)

A = 1,500,000.00 (1+ 0.065/12 )^(12(15))

= 3,966,301.2292565
