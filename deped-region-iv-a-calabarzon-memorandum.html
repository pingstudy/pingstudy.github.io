Answer:

3.38 x 10^24 molecules in 25.90 g of C₂H5O.

Explanation:

To determine the number of molecules in a given mass of a substance, you would need to use the substance's molar mass and the number of moles. The molar mass of C2H5OH (ethyl alcohol) is 46.07 g/mol. To find the number of moles, you would divide the mass of the substance (25.90 g) by its molar mass (46.07 g/mol).

25.90 g C₂H5O / 46.07 g/mol = 0.564 mol

Therefore, there are approximately 0.564 moles of C2H5OH in 25.90 g. To convert moles to number of molecules, you would multiply the number of moles by Avogadro's number, which is 6.022 x 10^23.

0.564 mol * (6.022 x 10^23 molecules/mol) = 3.38 x 10^24 molecules

So, there are approximately 3.38 x 10^24 molecules in 25.90 g of C₂H5O.
