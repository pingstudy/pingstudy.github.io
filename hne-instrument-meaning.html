6. What is Hne, ka si, and SaungGauk?


Answer:

Hne, ka si, and saung gauk are traditional musical instruments from Myanmar (formerly known as Burma).

Hne is a type of bamboo xylophone, with a series of bamboo slats of varying lengths mounted on a frame. It is played by striking the slats with mallets, and it's sound is similar to that of a marimba. It is often used in traditional Myanmar music as a solo instrument or in ensembles.

Ka si is a type of harp, with a wooden frame and bamboo strings stretched over it. It is played by plucking the strings with the fingers, and it has a soft, melodic sound.

The Ka si is a relatively uncommon instruments in Myanmar, and it's mostly used for court and religious music.

Saung gauk is a two-stringed lute, with a wooden body and a thin neck. It is played by plucking the strings with a plectrum and is often used as a solo instrument, or to accompany vocal performances.

It's considered as a traditional folk instrument and associated with the rural areas of Myanmar.
