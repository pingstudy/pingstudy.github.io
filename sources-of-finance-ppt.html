8. Discuss the functions of financial management, uses and sources of funds, the rules of sound financing and financial record keeping.


Answer:

The functions of financial management include planning, organizing, controlling, and monitoring an organization's financial resources. This involves forecasting future cash flows, setting financial goals and objectives, developing budgets, and making decisions on how to allocate funds to achieve the organization's goals.

Uses of funds in financial management include investing in assets, such as property, plant, and equipment, financing operations, and providing working capital for daily operations. Sources of funds include equity capital from owners, loans from banks and other financial institutions, and revenues from sales.

The rules of sound financing include maintaining a healthy balance between debt and equity, minimizing risk through diversification, and keeping debt at a manageable level. Financial record keeping involves accurately tracking and recording financial transactions and preparing financial statements to provide a clear picture of the organization's financial health.

10. As a future business person why should you have a source of funds in a business? Why is it important properly identify the source of financing?​


Answer:

Explanation:

Corporations often need external financing, or capital, to grow into new markets or regions, to engage in research and development, or to fight off competition.

Before a business can choose the appropriate source of funding, it must first define its financial requirements (i.e. what it needs money for), the time period during which it requires that money, and the amount required. To this end the business should prepare a business plan and a cashflow analysis. Not only will the business plan identify financial needs, it will also aid in acquiring financing, since a strong business plan will persuade the bank and other investors that the venture is worthwhile. The cashflow analysis will determine when and how much cash will be required to cover any deficits.
