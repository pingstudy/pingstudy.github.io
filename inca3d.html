14. what factors are included in an ecosystem that are not included in a community?


Answer:

The factors included in an ecosystem that are not included in a community are abiotic factors.

Explanation:

An ecosystem has both biotic (living) factors such as plants and animals and abiotic (non-living) factors like rocks, temperature, and climate.

A community is only composed of biotic factors as it is defined as an interacting group of several species in a given or common location.

For example, an ecosystem in a desert includes organisms (biotic) such as camels, scorpions, cacti, etc., and non-living (abiotic) factors such as hot temperature, low precipitation, sandy soil, etc. while a community in a desert ecosystem could be composed of a population of cacti, cactus wrens, scorpions, and desert king snakes, all of which are biotic.

The difference between a convergent and a divergent plate boundary is the direction the plates move in relation to each other. At a convergent plate boundary, the two tectonic plates are moving toward each other. This causes them to collide put great pressure on the places where they meet. At a divergent plate boundary, the tectonic plates are moving away from each other. This creates a rift area where magma rises and cools to form new rock in the gap between the plates
