17. what is altering of clothes what are the economic and social values of alteration help


Answer:

Altering of clothes refers to the process of making changes to an existing piece of clothing to better fit the wearer's body or style preferences. This can involve taking in or letting out seams, hemming or shortening sleeves, adjusting the length or width of a garment, or adding embellishments or decorations.

The economic value of alteration lies in its ability to extend the life of a garment, making it more cost-effective and sustainable for the wearer. Instead of buying a new piece of clothing, altering an existing garment can be a more affordable and environmentally friendly option. Alteration can also save money by allowing people to purchase clothing that may not fit perfectly off the rack, knowing that they can have it altered to fit their body.

The social value of alteration is also significant. Clothing that fits properly can enhance a person's confidence and self-esteem, making them feel more comfortable and presentable in social situations. Alteration can also help people to express their personal style, allowing them to customize their clothing to their own preferences and tastes.

Additionally, alteration can provide job opportunities and income for individuals and small businesses specializing in sewing and alterations. By supporting local tailors and seamstresses, communities can help to sustain a valuable craft and keep traditional textile skills alive.
