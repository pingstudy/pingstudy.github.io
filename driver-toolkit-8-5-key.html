5. Definition of terms 1. Bin 2. Computer chassis 3. Diagnostic tools 4. Hazards5. Insulation 6. Nozzle 7. Preventive 8. Static electricity9. Tool 10. Toolkit​​


Answer:

1. A receptacle for storing a specified substance.

2. A chassis (pronounced CHasē) is another word for a computer case, aka tower. The chassis holds most of a PC's components. This doesn't include peripherals like the keyboard, mouse or monitor.

3. Diagnostic equipment, methods, or systems are used for discovering what is wrong with people who are ill or with things that do not work properly.

4. A danger or risk.

5. In general, insulation is any material that helps prevent or stop the passing of electricity, heat, or sound. For example, there are companies who sell acoustic padding that help insulate or dampen the sound from your computer.

6. A nozzle is often a pipe or tube of varying cross sectional area, and it can be used to direct or modify the flow of a fluid (liquid or gas). Nozzles are frequently used to control the rate of flow, speed, direction, mass, shape, and/or the pressure of the stream that emerges from them.

7. The act of a regularly scheduled check of the computer hardware or software to help ensure it continues to operate properly.

8. Static electricity is a familiar electric phenomenon in which charged particles are transferred from one body to another.

9. A program used for software development or system maintenance.

10. A single utility program, a set of software routines or a complete integrated set of software utilities that are used to develop and maintain applications and databases. There are toolkits for developing almost anything. See tool, developer's toolkit, library, class library and CASE.
