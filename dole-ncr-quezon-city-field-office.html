21. an ncr committee consists of 5 representatives from manila, 6 from quezon city and 4 from makati. in how many ways can a subcommittee be chosen consisting of two members from different cities?​


Answer:

There are 900 ways.

Step-by-step explanation:

This is a combination problem, where it asks for a number of ways to make a set wherein the order of the elements do not matter. To solve this problem, we need to know the formula for combination which is:

[tex]C (n, r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!r!}[/tex]

Wherein we look for the number of combinations of [tex]n[/tex] things taken [tex]r[/tex] at a time.

We use this formula to find the number of combinations for each group of city representatives:

1) Combinations of subcommittee from MANILA:

[tex]C(5,2) = \frac{5!}{(5-2)!2!} = 10[/tex]

2) Combinations of subcommittee from QUEZON CITY:

[tex]C (6,2) = \frac{6!}{(6-2)!2!} = 15[/tex]

3) Combinations of subcommittee from MAKATI:

[tex]C (4, 2) = \frac{4!}{(4-2)!2!} = 6[/tex]

Then, following the Fundamental Counting Principle, we multiply all the combinations together:

4) [tex]10[/tex]×[tex]15[/tex]×[tex]6[/tex] = 900 ways

30. An ncr committee consists of 5 representatives from manila, 6 from quezon city and 4 from makati. in how many ways can a subcommittee be chosen consisting of two members from different cities?​


Answer:

74

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us take all the possible city combinations.

Case 1: Manila and Makati

There are 5 representatives from Manila and 4 from Makati, making a total of 5 x 4 = 20 combinations.

Case 2: Manila and Quezon

There are 5 representatives from Manila and 6 from Quezon, making a total of 5 x 6 = 30 combinations.

Case 3: Quezon and Makati

There are 6 representatives from Quezon and 4 from Makati, making a total of 6 x 4 = 24 combinations.

The total number of combinations is the sum of all the numbers of possible combinations, 20+30+24 = 74 total combinations.
