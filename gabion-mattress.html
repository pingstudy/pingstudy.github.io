4. A furniture store pays a wholesale price for a mattress. Then, the store marks up the retail price to 150% of the wholesale price. Later, they put the mattress on sale for 50% off of the retail price. A customer just bought the mattress on sale and paid $1,200. What was the retail price of the mattress, before the discount? What was the wholesale price, before the markup?


Answer:

Let's call the wholesale price "w".

The retail price before discount is 150% of w, or 1.5w.

The sale price is 50% off the retail price, so 1.5w * 50% = 0.75w.

We know the sale price was $1,200, so 1.5w = $1,200.

Solving for w, we have w = $800.

The retail price before discount is 1.5w = 1.5 * $800 = $1,200.

The wholesale price before markup is w = $800.

17. step by step vacuuming a mattress​


1. Remove the Linens

Before you start vacuuming your bed, ensure that you remove the linens from the bed. Wash the mattress pad, sheets, and bedspread and thoroughly dry them. Follow the instructions mentioned and use hot water and dryer heat setting to kill the dust mites.

2. Vacuum the Mattress

The next step is to vacuum the entire mattress surface. Start vacuuming from the top of the mattress and go all the way to the overlapping parts of the mattress. When vacuuming the mattresses, pay special attention to the services and seams as these areas collect a lot of dust, dirt, dead skin and other pollutants. To thoroughly clean the mattress, get a bed and upholstery vacuum cleaner which comes with a UV disinfection tool and is ideal for cleaning flat surfaces.

3. Spot Clean to Remove Stains

Once you are done with vacuuming the mattress, check for any stains on the mattress. If your mattress has any kind of stains because of spills, use the right cleaner to remove the stains from the mattress. Though it is advisable to clean the stains immediately, there may be instances when a spill may go unnoticed. You can use a bed and upholstery cleaner to remove stubborn stains from the mattresses. The simplest way to remove stains is by mixing a teaspoon of detergent in a cup of warm water.

4. Deodorize the Mattress

To deodorize the mattress, sprinkle baking soda over the entire mattress. If possible, leave the baking soda on the mattress for 24 hours to get the best results. Baking soda absorbs any excess liquid from the mattress to give it a fresh smell. Use the bed and upholstery vacuum cleaner to remove the baking soda from the mattress.

5. Cover the Mattress

Ensure that you flip the mattress and repeat the process on both sides of the mattress. After vacuuming, cover up the mattress. The mattress cover is easy to wash, and it also protects the mattress from dirt and stains. You can also add a mattress pad between the bottom sheet and mattress cover in order to absorb the moisture.

Answer:

CONVECTION

Answer:

CONVECTION

Explanation:

Convection is single or multiphase fluid flow that occurs spontaneously due to the combined effects of material property heterogeneity and body forces on a fluid, most commonly density and gravity.

When a fluid, such as air or a liquid, is heated and then travels away from the source, it carries the thermal energy along. This type of heat transfer is called convection. The fluid above a hot surface expands, becomes less dense, and rises.
