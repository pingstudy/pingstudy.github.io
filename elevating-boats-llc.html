8. the angle of elevation from a boat to the top of a 92 meter hill is 12° how far is the boat from the base of the hill with drawing


Answer:

The distance of the boat from the base of the hill is 432.83 meters.

Explanation:

The angle of elevation is the angle formed where the observer's line of sight is above its horizontal axis.

The illustration for this problem is shown below.

Using the illustration, it created a right triangle with the given:

[tex]y=92 m[/tex]       this is the height of the hill

∠A = 12°      this is the angle of elevation from the boat to the      

                   top of the hill

x = ?             this is the distance of the boat from the base of the hill

Solving for the distance, we use TOA or [tex]Tangent=\frac{Opposite}{Hypotenuse}[/tex]

[tex]Tan 12[/tex]°[tex]=\frac{92m}{x}[/tex]

Using cross-multiply, we get:

[tex]x=\frac{92m}{Tan12}[/tex]

[tex]x=432.83m[/tex]

Therefore, the distance of the boat from the base of the hill is 432.83m.

15. the angle of elevation from a boat to the top of a 92 meter hill is 12degree. how far is the boat from the base of the hill?


Answer:

The distance of the boat from the base of the hill is [tex]432.83[/tex] meters.

To better understand the situation in the problem, draw an illustration that describes the situation. Refer to the picture below.

Two types of angle behaviors

1. Angle of elevation

2. Angle of depression

Angle of elevation is the angle from the horizontal upward to a particular sight.

Angle of depression is the angle from the horizontal downward to particular sight

The shape in the illustration is right triangle which has one [tex]90^o\\[/tex] angle at the base of the hill. So, the concepts in Trigonometry can help us to solve the problem. There are six basic trigonometric functions.

Some concepts of right triangle, https://brainly.ph/question/1441042

Six Basic Trigonometric Functions

1. [tex]cos \theta = \frac{\text{adjacent side}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \\[/tex]

2. [tex]sin \theta = \frac{\text{opposite side}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \\[/tex]

3. [tex]tan \theta = \frac{\text{opposite side}}{\text{opposite side}} \\[/tex]

4. [tex]sec\theta = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{adjacent side}} \\[/tex]

5. [tex]csc\theta = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{opposite side}} \\[/tex]

6. [tex]cot\theta = \frac{\text{adjacent side}}{\text{opposite side}} \\[/tex]

More about trigonometric functions, access the link https://brainly.ph/question/740865

Assume that the unknown distance is [tex]x[/tex]. Since the known details are the angle of reference which measures [tex]12^o \\[/tex] and its opposite side is [tex]92\text{meters}[/tex], use the [tex]tan\theta\\[/tex] function.

Use the following steps to solve for the unknown distance

1. Substitute the values of [tex]\theta=12^o[/tex] and [tex]\text{opposite side}=92[/tex] in to the tangent function.

2. Do cross multiplication.

3. Divide both sides by [tex]tan12^o[/tex].

4. Simplify using a scientific calculator.

5. Round to the nearest hundredths.

                                     [tex]\begin{aligned}tan\theta&=\frac{92}{x}\\xtan12^o&=92\\x&=\frac{92}{tan12^o}\\x&=432.8259701\\&\approx{432.83}\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means that the distance of the boat to the base of the hill is [tex]432.83\text{ meters}[/tex].

28. A boat is 600 meters from the base of the cliff,Erika who is sitting in the boat of elevation to the top of the cliff is 32Â°.how high is the cliff?


Given,

Angle of elevation = 32°

Adjacent side to angle = 600m

Opposite Side to angle = height of cliff = ?

To find the height of cliff, we need to use the trigonometric ratio of Tangent,

[tex] \huge{tan \theta \: = \: \frac{opposite}{adjacent}}[/tex]

[tex]tan32 = \frac{opposite}{600} \\ \\ opposite = tan32(600) \\ \\ opposite \: = \: 374.92 \: meters[/tex]

Hence the height of the cliff is 374.92 meters.
