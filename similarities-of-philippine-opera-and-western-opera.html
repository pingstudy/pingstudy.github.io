6. What do you think are the differences and similarities of European Opera to Philippine Opera?


Answer:

European Opera and Philippine Opera have both similarities and differences in terms of their musical style, themes, and performance traditions.

One of the major differences is the language used in the opera. European Opera is often performed in Italian, French, German, or English, while Philippine Opera is usually performed in Tagalog or other local languages. This difference in language also reflects the cultural and historical influences on the development of each opera tradition.

Another difference is the musical style. European Opera is known for its grand and elaborate musical arrangements, often with large orchestras and choirs, while Philippine Opera often features more traditional Filipino musical instruments and melodies.

However, both opera traditions share similarities in their themes, such as love, tragedy, and human emotions. They also both feature strong vocal performances, acting, and stagecraft to convey the story and emotions of the characters.

In terms of performance traditions, both European Opera and Philippine Opera have formal and traditional aspects, with conventions for dress, behavior, and audience participation. However, Philippine Opera also often incorporates elements of folk culture and local customs, while European Opera tends to be more formal and stylized.

Overall, while there are significant differences between European Opera and Philippine Opera, both traditions offer unique and powerful experiences of music, drama, and storytelling
