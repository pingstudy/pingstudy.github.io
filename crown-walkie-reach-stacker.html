16. Why is Chicago referred to as hog butcher for a world tool maker stacker of wheat and player with railroad


Chicago is known as the "World's Hog Butcher" because it was once a major center for the meatpacking industry, with large stockyards and meatpacking plants. It is known as a "tool maker" because it was a major center for the production of industrial tools and equipment. The term "wheat stacker" refers to Chicago's role as a major grain-processing and storage hub. "Railroad player" refers to Chicago's central location and importance as a transportation hub, with many major railroads converging in the city. Carl Sandburg coined the phrase "Chicago" in his poem of the same name.

23. describe the different types of walking the brisk walking power walking chi walking and race walking​


Answer:

Power walking. This workout is done at a quick jogging pace and uses rigorous arm swings to build cardiovascular endurance and total-body strength.

ChiWalking. encourages participants to maintain a neutral spine, move freely, engage their core muscles, and relax their arm and leg muscles. This method of walking is less taxing on the body and lessens the aches and pains brought on by improper walking.

One measure to quantify brisk walking is “steps per minute,” and 100 steps per minute is considered moderate intensity or brisk walking.

Race Walking The sport of swift, continuous foot contact, performed in an upright, rhythmic stride with the arms typically held bent and high and pumped close to the body, requires that the trailing foot not be lifted until the other meets the ground and the knee locks momentarily.
