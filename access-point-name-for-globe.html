1. Name the parts of the globe ​


Answer:

GLOBE – The Different Parts Of The Globe

PARTS OF GLOBE – The Globe And Its Parts

PARTS OF GLOBE – In this topic, we will learn about the globe itself and its several different parts with meaning for each part.

It is the Earth itself or in other terms, a spherical or round object.

The Parts  

1. Latitude and Longitude

The horizontal (latitude) and vertical(longitude) maps or coordinates of the Earth which are used to locate the global address of the specific location.

2. Equator

The central horizontal line that has the value of zero degrees latitude. It is equally distant to the North and South Pole.

3. Prime Meridian

The central 180 degrees vertical line that has the value of zero degrees latitude. It is equally distant to the International Date Line.

4. North Pole

The northernmost point of the world, which is 90 degrees away from the equator and equidistant to the South Pole.

5. South Pole

The southernmost point of theworld, which is -90 degrees away from the equator and equidistant to the North Pole.

6. Northern Hemisphere

The northern half of the world and north to the equator.

7. Southern Hemisphere

The southern half of the world and south to the equator. It contains continents like Australia, 1/3 Africa, 90% South America, and several islands of Asia.

8. Eastern Hemisphere

The east half of the world, east of the prime meridian and west of the Internatonal Date Line or antimeridian.

9. Western Hemisphere

The west half of the world, west of the prime meridian and east of the antimeridian

10. Oceans

Bodies of water that makes up more than 2/3 of the globe. Thee are 5 main oceans in the world: Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, And Arctic.

Answer:

1.longitudinal lines

Imaginary lines, also called meridians, running vertically around the globe. Unlike latitude lines, longitude lines are not parallel. Meridians meet at the poles and are widest apart at the equator.

2.parallels

Horizontal mapping lines on Earth are lines of latitude. They are known as "parallels" of latitude, because they run parallel to the equator.

3.northern hemisphere

This spherical Earth can be divided into equal halves called hemispheres. If we separate Earth halfway between the North and South poles (at the equator), we get the Northern Hemisphere (centered around the North Pole), and the Southern Hemisphere (centered around the South Pole).

4.southern hemisphere

This spherical Earth can be divided into equal halves called hemispheres. If we separate Earth halfway between the North and South poles (at the equator), we get the Northern Hemisphere (centered around the North Pole), and the Southern Hemisphere (centered around the South Pole).

5.The prime meridian

is the line of 0° longitude, the starting point for measuring distance both east and west around the Earth.

6.The equator

is at 0 degrees (0°) latitude, and it divides Earth into its northern and southern hemispheres. It is the starting point for measuring distances in degrees north or south of the equator.

23. how is the philippines located in the globe or in the map?name the landmasses and territorial waters surrounding it.​


Answer:

There are no landmasses surrounding the Philippines but there are plenty of bodies of water as the Philippines is an archipelago. There are other countries, however that are near, including Taiwan to the North and Malaysia and Indonesia to the South. The Philippines is strategically located where access from Asian countries are easy. It also serves as a usual stopover for cross-Pacific flights as well as a port of call for various ships internationally because of our many bodies of water.

North

Land: North of the Philippines we have Taiwan. China and and the Korean Peninsula.  

Water: We have the Luzon Strait running across most of Luzon .

East  

Land: Towards the East is the Pacific Ocean including islands like Guam, Palau, the Micronesia and other small island territories. The Philippine Sea is towards the southern part of our eastern seaboard.

Water: We have the Benham Rise, now referred to as the Philippine Rise. This area is said to be rich in natural resources. Of course, we also have the Pacific Ocean.

South

Land: To the south we have Borneo, divided into Malaysia and Indonesia.  

Water: We have the Celebes Sea  towards the south

West

Land: The Philippine EEZ or Exclusive Economic Zone. These are small islands, islets, rocks and other natural formations that are scattered wherein we have sovereign rights. It is an area of dispute with other countries such as China, Viet Nam and Indonesia.  

Water: The West Philippine Sea surrounds the western seaboard. Also in here is the South China Sea which as mentioned above is a point of disagreement among various Asian nations.

Implications

Because we do not share land borders with other nations, and the oceans have always been treated as a shared resource, Filipinos typically do not have the concept of protecting its land boundaries--compared with Tibet for example, where they are strongly standing against China as the bigger country attempts to encroach on its lands.
