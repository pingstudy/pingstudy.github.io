12. Bears have poor eyesight, but how do they hunt their food?


Answer:Bears have several senses that help them hunt their food, despite their poor eyesight. One of their primary senses is their excellent sense of smell. They have an acute sense of smell that can detect food from long distances, even up to 20 miles away. This sense of smell helps them locate potential prey, such as fish, rodents, and insects.

In addition to their sense of smell, bears also have excellent hearing. Their large ears are capable of picking up even the slightest sounds, which can help them locate prey, especially small animals that may be hiding.

Finally, bears are also very strong and fast, despite their bulky size. They are capable of running at high speeds, which allows them to chase and capture prey, especially larger animals like deer. Their sharp claws and teeth also aid in hunting and killing prey.

Overall, while bears may have poor eyesight, they rely on their other senses, strength, and hunting instincts to survive in the wild.
