15. 1. Which of the following tend/s to cause physical or mechanical weathering? I. Animals that burrow in the ground II. Roots of plants growing into cracks in the rocks III. Water that seeps into pores and cracks of rocks IV. Oxygen which combines with some minerals present in the rocks I only C. III and IV only II and III only d. I, II, and Ill only a. b. proded?pls​


Answer:

D

Explanation:

The answer is: d. I, II, and III only

Physical or mechanical weathering is the breakdown of rocks into smaller pieces without changing their chemical composition. Animals that burrow in the ground, roots of plants growing into cracks in rocks, and water that seeps into pores and cracks of rocks are all examples of physical or mechanical weathering. Oxygen, on the other hand, can cause chemical weathering by combining with some minerals present in rocks to form new compounds. Therefore, option IV is not correct.

27. What causes the cracking of soils in most of the rice fields?


Too much drought or lack of irrigation can cause cracking of soils in rice fields which could affect the growth of plants or crops planted in there. The water molecules are withdrawn from inter-structural spaces as these clays dry out, and the clays shrink. If this shrinking occurs in a volume of soil in millions of clay structural units, the shrinking can serve to create large cracks in the soil.

Another reason is nutrient loss. When the same crop is cultivated spontaneously, the nutrient present in soil is used which affects the quality of soil, therefore leads to soil cracking.

That's why proper irrigation, used of organic matter and crop rotation should be practiced to avoid soil cracking. In addition, crop rotations can improve the soil structure and organic matter, thus reducing erosion and increasing the resilience of the farm system.
