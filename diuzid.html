15. 63.9 grams of carbon dioxide=____ molecules of carbon dioxide​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of CO₂.

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 2)

molar mass = 44.01 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of molecules of CO₂.

[tex]\text{number of CO₂ molecules = 63.9 g CO₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol CO₂}}{\text{44.01 g CO₂}} × \frac{\text{6.022 × 10²³ CO₂ molecules}}{\text{1 mol CO₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{number of CO₂ molecules = 8.74 × 10²³ molecules}}[/tex]

28. 12. Which of the following contributes to acid rain?A. carbon dioxide and carbon monoxideB. sulfur dioxide and carbon monoxideC. sulfur dioxide and nitrogen dioxideD. sulfur dioxide and nitrous oxide​


Answer:

Acid rain results when sulfur dioxide (SO2) and nitrogen oxides (NOX) are emitted into the atmosphere and transported by wind and air currents. The SO2 and NOX react with water, oxygen and other chemicals to form sulfuric and nitric acids. These then mix with water and other materials before falling to the ground.
