9. how does cavitations happens in the hydraulic system?​


Explanation:

The product of excessive vacuum conditions created at the hydraulic pump's inlet (supply side), cavitation is the formation, and collapse of vapors within a hydraulic pump. High vacuum creates vapor bubbles within the oil, which are carried to the discharge (pressure) side. These bubbles then collapse, thus cavitation.

Hydraulic lifts work on a basic principle: to go up, a pump pushes oil into the cylinder, pushing the piston (which pushes the lift car) up. To go down, the valve opens and oil is allowed to flow back into the reservoir, and is pushed back using the gravitational force of the lift car.

A hydraulic jacking system uses a fluid, usually oil, to transfer power from one point to another. The system consists of a hydraulic pump that applies pressure to the oil, which is then sent through a series of hoses to a hydraulic cylinder or piston. The ram is connected to a platform or hoist that needs to be raised.

When the pump applies pressure to the oil, the ram moves up and raises the platform. The power transmitted through the system depends on the size of the pump and the cylinder. The system can be controlled using valves that regulate the flow of oil through the system.

Hydraulic lifting systems are widely used in auto repair shops, manufacturing plants, and on construction sites to lift heavy equipment or machinery. They are also used in elevators, where they provide a fluid and efficient way to move people and goods between floors.

In general, the hydraulic lifting system works on the principle of power transmission through a fluid, which makes it a powerful and versatile tool for lifting heavy loads.

23. why supply elements is a part of pneumatic system? ​


Answer:

The supply elements of a pneumatic system provide and regulate the compressed air that powers the system's actuators and controls. These elements, such as air compressors, filters, regulators, and lubricators, ensure that the system operates efficiently and effectively. Without a proper air supply, a pneumatic system cannot function properly.

Answer:

Supply elements are a part of pneumatic systems because they are responsible for supplying compressed air to the system. This compressed air is the energy source that powers the pneumatic actuators and components. The supply elements, such as compressors, air receivers, and filters, play a crucial role in ensuring that the system has a consistent and clean source of air. Without the supply elements, the pneumatic system would not be able to operate efficiently and effectively.
