8. 17. There are 12 standbys who hope to get on your flight to Hawaii, but only 6 seats are available on the plane. How many different ways can the 6 people are selected?​


Answer:

924 ways

nine twenty four

Answer:

924

Step-by-step explanation:

This is a combination problem since the order in which the 6 people are selected does not matter.

We can use the formula for combinations:

nCr = n! / (r! * (n-r)!)

where n is the total number of items, r is the number of items being selected, and ! represents the factorial function.

In this case, n = 12 (the total number of standbys) and r = 6 (the number of available seats on the plane).

So the number of ways to select 6 people from 12 is:

12C6 = 12! / (6! * (12-6)!) = 12! / (6! * 6!) = (121110987) / (654321) = 924

Therefore, there are 924 different ways to select 6 people from the 12 standbys.
