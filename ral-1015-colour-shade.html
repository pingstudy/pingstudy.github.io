5. Differentiate Colour Gamut from Complementary Colours​


Answer:

Complementary colors are color combinations that, when combined or mixed, cancel each other out (lose hue) and produce a grayscale color such as white or black. They create the greatest contrast for those two hues when put adjacent to each other. Complementary colors are often known as "opposite colors." While the color gamut of a device is defined as the range of colors it can create or record. On the chromaticity diagram, it is typically represented by an enclosed area of the device's primary colors. Monitors, for example, have primary hues of red, green, and blue.
