Answer:

A manpower/recruitment agency must be 75% Filipino-owned and have a P5,000,0000 minimum paid-up capital. You will be required to maintain a balance in a local bank of P500,000 and have a P1,000,000.00 escrow agreement with POEA (Philippine Overseas Employment Administration), as well as a P100,000 bond.

Just what is a business opportunity? That question has plagued a great many people trying to decide whether to buy a current independent business, a franchise, or what we'll refer to in this text as a business opportunity. To allay the confusion, we offer a simple analogy. Think back to elementary school when your teacher was explaining the difference between a rectangle and a square. A square is also a rectangle, but a rectangle isn't necessarily a square. The same relationship exists between business opportunities, independent businesses for sale and franchises. All franchises and independent businesses for sale are business opportunities, but not all business opportunities meet the requirement of being a franchise nor are they in the strictest sense of the word independent businesses for sale.

A. Physical therapy

B. Nutrition service

C. Center for cancer control

D. Health education and manpower services

Explanation:

Health Centers provide jobs for of doctors, public health nurses, nutritionists, medical technicians, rural health midwives, barangay health workers, dentists, among others. They are also responsible for dissemination of health information to their respective areas.

The Department of Health (DOH) is the central health agency in our country. DOH is responsible for ensuring that Filipinos have access to basic public health. One of the most important parts of this goal is providing Barangay Health Centers.  

Health Centers are community-based organizations for patients who may have difficulty accessing private health care due to financial limitations. Thus, the government (DOH and Local Government Units) directed their efforts to making sure that every Filipino will have access to comprehensive primary care.

Preferably, there must be one Barangay Health Center per barangay or district. But this is not yet possible because some remote areas still  have difficulty accessing basic health care. Moreover, some brangays or districts share Health Centers because of their nearness or their lack of manpower.

Some of the services that are provided by Barangay Heath Centers are the following:

Education preventive medicine treatment of the most common diseases and injuries
