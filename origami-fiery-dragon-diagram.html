4. Is it necessary for us to make a pattern or diagram in making origami project? ​


Is it necessary for us to make a pattern or diagram in making origami project?  

Of course Yes, we really need a pattern in making an origami project. First is so that we can do it properly or correctly. The second is so that we no longer get confused in doing so.  

Origami does not have to be complicated and time-consuming. There are various figures or patterns that are quick and pleasant to create. Simply obey the step-by-step instructions or pattern, and you'll be able to fold something in no time.

What is origami? Origami is the art of folding paper. You can make about something out of a sheet of paper and your fingertips: birds, vessels, and boxes. It doesn't end there; you can even build cats, vehicles, and containers. Flowers, fans, and fish come to mind. Can you see what I mean? The options are limitless. Who does origami? Origami can be done by someone with a pinch of persistence and a dash of dexterity. Children under the age of five can struggles with origami, but with your assistance and encouragement, they may make basic models. Otherwise, origami can be done by anybody – even blind people have been able to fold origami. Why do people do origami? People do origami for a variety of reasons, the most important is that it is relaxing and helps them to construct beautiful objects. Some people love folding origami because it makes them relax. Some people use origami to exercise their fingers and minds (therapy). Some people sell their origami, while others use it to teach math and geometry. Where and when can you do origami? Origami can be done anywhere there is a speck of room to build on. Some people do origami while traveling by plane, rail, or bus. You can do origami while drinking tea with a friend or while watching television. Origami can be done anytime and wherever you choose.
