i've got a car with an internal volume of 12,000 L. If i drive my car into the river and it implodes, what will be the volume of the gas when the pressure goes from 1.0 atm to 1.4 atm?​

Given: V1 = 12000 L

           P1 = 1.0 atm

           P2 = 1.4 atm

Required: V2

Solution:

Use Boyle's law

(P1) × (V1) = (P2) × (V2)

V2 = [(P1) × (V1)] / (P2)

V2 = (1.0 atm × 12000L) / (1.4 atm)

V2 = 8571.43 L

Problem:

I've got a car with an internal volume of 12,000 L. If i drive my car into the river and it implodes, what will be the volume of the gas when the pressure goes from 1.0 atm to 1.4 atm?​

Given Data:

V1 (initital volume) = 12,000 L         V2 (final volume)  =  ? unknown

P1 (initial pressure)  = 1.0 atm        P2 (final pressure)  = 1.4 atm

Solution:

This problem can be solve using Boyle's Law which state that volume is inversely proportional to pressure when the amount and temperature of gas are constant. Formula of Boyle's Law is P1V1 = P2V2

To solve for the V2, we can use V2 = P1V1 / P2

V2 = P1V1 / P2

      = 1.0 atm × 12,000 L / 1.4 atm     (cancel the unit atm)

      = 12,000 L / 1.4 atm

      = 8571.43 L

Answer:

The new volume is 8571.43 L
