9. what is administrative management theory​


Administrative management theory attempts to find a rational way to design an organization as a whole. The theory generally calls for a formalized administrative structure, a clear division of labor, and delegation of power and authority to administrators relevant to their areas of responsibilities.

Administrative Management Theorists

-Henri Fayol (1841-1925): Fayol was born in France, where he worked for a coal-mining business. He developed 14 administrative principles for organizational structure and management.

-James D. Mooney (1884-1957): Mooney studied mechanical engineering and eventually became a key member of General Motors' top management team. In 1931, he wrote Onward Industry! The book is considered by many scholars to be a significant contribution to administrative management theory.

-Luther H. Gulick (1892-1993): Gulick is often considered the 'Dean of Public Administration.' He applied administrative management theory principles to government.

16. What is administrative management theory


Answer:

The Administrative Management Theory by Henry Fayol is based on the idea of departmentalization which means that the organization should identify the activities to be performed and classify this according to different groups or departments so the task will be accomplished effectively and efficiently.

The administrative theorist Henry Fayol believes that emphasis should be focused on the management of organization and also the human and behavioral factors in the management. Thus, the main focus is the structure of the organization and how competent the individuals are in accomplishing the tasks given to them.

Explanation:

This administrative management theory focuses on improving the efficiency of the management through standardization of processes then proceeds to the operational level where each staff needs to learn and implement the changes in their routine job.

Henry Fayol comprehensively studied the organization's problems from the manager's viewpoint and classified the organization's activities as Technical, Financial, Commercial, Security, Accounting and Managerial.

Henry Fayol’s 14 Principles of Management

1. Division of Work: The tasks are divided among individuals based on their area of expertise or specializations so as to make sure that there is full focus on the task at hand and thus leads to completion of the task assigned to them.

2. Authority and Responsibility: This means that when the person is given a task, he has the authority to obey and be held accountable if anything goes wrong with it.

3. Discipline: The staff are well-disciplined which means there is obedience, and respect towards each other.

4. Unity of Command: This means that the orders should come from only one supervisor and that individual should report only to his or her assigned supervisor to avoid conflict as to whose instructions to be followed.

5. Unity of Direction: All the individuals with different assigned tasks must have the same or common objective.

6. Subordination of Individual to General Interest: This means that the interest of the individual and the organization should coincide with each other so the task will be accomplished.

7. Remuneration of Personnel: The compensation and the method of payment should be the same and fair to all so everyone will be satisfied.

8. Centralization: Focusing on the importance of the subordinate's role in organization.

9. Scalar Chain: The organization should follow a proper hierarchy so there will be a smooth flow of authority and communication. Each individual must know to whom he shall receive instructions and to whom he is accountable.  The communication can either flow from top to bottom or bottom to top but must pass through level of authority.

10. Order: This means everything should be in their proper places, there is a systematic arrangement of things and of people. Every person should be assigned in the right job.

11. Equity: Everyone in the organization should be treated equally with respect, kindness, and justice.

12. Stability of Tenure: There is retention of employees in the organization.

13. Initiative: The manager must motivate the staff to take actions to implement the plan.

14. Esprit de Corps: This means "unity is strength". Thus, everyone must cooperate with each other and work in synergy and get along with each other.
