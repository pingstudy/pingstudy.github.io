1. Write in the box at least 5 testing procedures of termination and connections of electrical wiring and electronic circuits.TESTING PROCEDURES OF TERMINATION AND CONNECTION OFELECTRICAL WIRING AND ELECTRONIC CIRCUITS


Answer:

Explanation:

Continuity testing: This test checks that there is an unbroken path between two points in a circuit. It can be used to verify that a wire has been properly connected to a terminal or that a component has been soldered correctly.

Insulation resistance testing: This test measures the resistance of the insulation material surrounding a wire or component. It can help detect any breaks or faults in the insulation that may lead to short circuits or other problems.

Polarity testing: This test checks that the positive and negative connections of a circuit have been correctly identified and connected. It can prevent damage to sensitive components and ensure proper functioning of the circuit.

Voltage testing: This test checks that the voltage levels in a circuit are within the expected range. It can help identify any problems with power supply, voltage regulators, or other components.

Current testing: This test measures the current flowing through a circuit. It can help identify any issues with the resistance of the wires, connections, or components, and ensure that the circuit is functioning correctly.
