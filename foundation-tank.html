7. a cylindried tank can be hold 197.86 cubicseter of water. If the radius of the tank is 3meters, how high is the tank? ​


Answer:

7.04 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

We can use the formula for the volume of a cylinder, which is:

V = πr²h

where V is the volume, r is the radius, and h is the height.

We know that the volume of the tank is 197.86 cubic meters, and the radius is 3 meters. So we can substitute these values into the formula and solve for h:

197.86 = π(3²)h

197.86 = 9πh

h = 197.86 / (9π)

h ≈ 7.04 meters

Therefore, the height of the tank is approximately 7.04 meters.

17. difference between shallow foundation and deep Foundation​


Answer:

Difference between Shallow and Deep Foundation For a Shallow foundation, the depth of foundation is equal to or less than its width. Whereas for Deep foundation, the depth of footing is equal to or greater than its depth. A shallow foundation is located at very low depth, whereas a deep foundation is constructed at more depth.
