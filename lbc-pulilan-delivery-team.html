2. why do people calibrate the pulilan carabao festival (pulilan bulacan)​


Answer:

Celebration of the Pulilan Carabao Festival

People from Pulilan, Bulacan celebrate the Kneeling Carabao Festival every May 14 and 15 because they wanted to give thanks to the patron saint of the town, San Isidro Labrador. He is also the patron saint of the farmers who are using the carabaos for their work in the field.

Explanation:

Every May 14 and 15, the townspeople of Pulilan, Bulacan line up the streets to witness the annual Kneeling Carabao Festival. Many tourists from all over the Philippines also come to the town to see what the celebration is all about. The parade starts at 2 PM, in Barangay Dampol 1st, and the carabaos, along with the dancers and the musicians, would have to walk more than 1.5 kilometers just to reach the San Isidro Labrador Parish Church in Barangay Poblacion.

11. halimbawa ng kwentong bayan sa pulilan?


Answer:

Kwentong Bayan sa Pulilan, Bulacan

Ang aking ikukwento ay ang alamat ng bayan ng Pulilan. Noong unang panahon, wala pa ang bayan ng Pulilan. Ang itsura ng bayan noon ay isang malawak na lupain. Halos wala ngang nakatira sa lupain ito dahil sa ito ay liblib pa noon. Nang sakupin ng mga Kastila ang Pilipnas, at naitatag ang lalawigan ng Bulacan noong 1578, nagsimula nang dumaan ang mga Kastila sa lugar na ito. May isang paring Kastila ang nakapansin sa itsura ng topograpiya ng lugar – ang isang malaking ilog (na ngayon ay Ilog Angat) ay dumadaloy sa kalagitnaan ng lupaing ito. Sa laki ng ilog, nakakabuo ito ng mga maliliit na isla o pulo sa Tagalog. Naisip ngayon ng pari na tawagin ang bayan na “Pulilan”, na nagmula sa mga salitang “pulo ng iilan”.

25. History of robinson pulilan bulacan​


Answer:

History of Robinsons Pulilan

Robinsons is one of the largest mall operators in the Philippines. In 2010, they decided to open a branch in Pulilan, Bulacan, referred to as Robinsons Supermarket. A few years later, Robinsons decided to change the name of the mall into Robinsons Townville Pulilan. This building can be found in Barangay Cutcot, Pulilan, Bulacan, just in front of the public market.

Explanation:

Before the construction of Robinsons Townville Pulilan, the land where the building currently is used to be a parking space for public jeepneys. During Christmas time, or when the Kneeling Carabao Festival is fast approaching, this giant plot of land becomes a carnival, visited by the children.
