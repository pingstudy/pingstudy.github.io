2. You have 3 packs of candies Some packs contain 50 pcs and some have 30 pcs . There are 110 pcs of candies altogether. What is the first equation of a number 50 pieces and 30 pieces packs of candies are expressed in x and y, respectively?​


Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

Packs of candies according to quatity: x and yTotal packs of candies: 3 packs

Equation (1):

x + y = 3

Total number of candies:  110 piecesNumber of candies per pack:50x and 30y

Equation (2):

50x + 30y = 110

To find the number of packs for 50 pieces (x) and 20 pieces (y):

Equation (1):

x + y = 3

y = 3-x

Substitute to y in Equation (2):

50x + 30y = 110

50x + 30(3-x) = 110

50x + 90 - 30x = 110

50x - 30x = 110 - 90

20x/20 = 20/20

x = 1

1 pack contains 50 pieces

y = 3 - x

y = 3-1

y = 2

2 packs contain 30 pieces

Quantity of 2 packs = 2 × (30 pieces) = 60 pieces

Check:

Equation (1):

x + y = 3

1 + 2 = 3

3 = 3  (True)

Equation (2):

50x + 30y = 110

50(1) + 30(2) = 110

50 + 60 = 110

110 = 110  (True)

Ano ang tawag sa Laguna noong 1500-1600?

Saysaybagin (Nobyembre 14, 2014)

Ayon sa pinakaunang diksiyonaryo (1613 Vocabulario de Lengua Tagala), ang tawag sa Laguna ay “Pulilan” :

“Laguna : Pulilan pp : M. de Bay en respeto de Manila, namumulilan .3.ac. ir a contratar de Manila y sus contornos a la laguna de Bay, ypinamumulilan .3.P. lo q llenan, aco, y, namumulilan, voy a la laguna a tratar, tagapulilan ca, eres natural de la laguna? preguntan en Manila “ (pahina 383)

(“Namumulilan” ang tawag kung pupunta sa Laguna mula Maynila. “Taga-Pulilan” ang tawag sa mga taga-Laguna ng mga taga-Maynila).

“Doongan” naman ang tawag sa Laguna ng mga Tinguianes:

“Laguna : Doongan pp : respeto de los del Bondo y Tinguianes, tutugpa aco sa doongan, boy a la laguna doon sasang doongan man, aunque sea alla en toda la laguna.” (pahina 383).

Hindi po, Pulilan ang tawag sa Laguna noon

25. the price of a certain lot in Laguna increase from 4,000 per square meter is 6,000 per square meter. Express the increase in percent.


Answer:

The percent increase in price of the lot is 50%.

Step-by-step explanation:

Amount of increase in price:

6,000 - 4,000 = 2,000

Increase in percent:

2,000/4,000 = 1/2 or 0.5  

0.5 × 100 = 50%

The percent increase in price of the lot is 50%.
