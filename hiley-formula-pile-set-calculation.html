6. A set of cards contains cards numbered 1 – 8. Mrs. Jacob’s class is conducting an experiment in which a card is drawn from the pile, the number is recorded, and then the card is returned to the set. The class will conduct 1,000 trials. Based on the theoretical probability, which is the best prediction for the number of times a 4 will be drawn from the pile?


Answer:

125 times

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Find the probability of drawing a 4 in any one time:

Total number of cards = 8

Number of cards with a 4 = 1

P(Getting a 4) = 1/8

Step 2: Find the number of times a 4 will be drawn in 1000 trials:

P(Getting a 4) = 1/8

Number of times a 4 will be drawn = 1/8 x 1000 = 125

Answer: There will be 125 cards with the number "4" drawn.


7. 1. Six people entered a block building contest. Lynda built her pile of blocks twice as high as Selwyn's. Michelle created a pile that was three times higher than Lynda's pile. Warren built his pile one block higher than Michelle's. Jane's pile of blocks was six higher than Warren's. If Adrian piled up 27 blocks, which was two blocks higher than Jane's pile, how high was each person's pile? How many blocks would be needed altogether?​


Answer:

98 blocks

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's start by assigning variables to represent the height of each person's block pile:

Selwyn's pile = s

Lynda's pile = 2s (since her pile is twice as high as Selwyn's)

Michelle's pile = 3(2s) = 6s (since her pile is three times higher than Lynda's pile)

Warren's pile = 6s + 1 (since his pile is one block higher than Michelle's)

Jane's pile = 6s + 7 (since her pile is six higher than Warren's)

Adrian's pile = 6s + 9 (since his pile is two higher than Jane's)

We know that Adrian's pile is 27 blocks high, so we can set up an equation:

6s + 9 = 27

Solving for s, we get:

s = 3

So Selwyn's pile is 3 blocks high, Lynda's pile is 2s = 6 blocks high, Michelle's pile is 6s = 18 blocks high, Warren's pile is 6s + 1 = 19 blocks high, Jane's pile is 6s + 7 = 25 blocks high, and Adrian's pile is 6s + 9 = 27 blocks high.

To find the total number of blocks used, we can add up all the heights:

3 + 6 + 18 + 19 + 25 + 27 = 98

So a total of 98 blocks were used in the contest.

18. 3 thousand exercise books are arranged into 3 piles. The first pile has 10 more books than the second pile. The number of books in the second pile is twice the numbers of books in the third pile. How many books are there in the third pile?


let 'x' be the number of books on the first pile
     'y' be the number of books on the second pile
     'z' be the number of books on the third pile
x + y + z = 3000    -----equation 1
x = y + 10   -----equation 2
y = 2z     -----equation 3
from equation 3
y = 2z
z = y/2   -----equation 3'
substitute equations 3' and 2 to equation 1
x + y + z =3000
(y+10) + y + (y/2) = 3000
y + 10 + y + y/2 = 3000
2y + y/2 = 3000-10
(4y+y)/2 = 2990
5y = 2990(2)
5y = 5980
y=1196
substitute y=1196 to equation 2
x = y + 10
x = 1196 +10
x = 1206
substitute y=1196 to equation 3'
z = y/2
z = 1196/2
z = 598
therefore there are 598 books in the third pile.

Answer:

a. Speed of travel

Explanation:

Traveling incorrectly is a typical mishap. Sometimes the only condition that a welder needs to adjust is the travel speed. When you travel too fast, the puddle freezes too rapidly. As a result, contaminants and gases can become trapped, narrowing the bead face and causing pointing ripple. It is probable that partial penetration will occur due to the loss of the keyhole in the root pass. Traveling too slowly will result in a huge puddle with a pile-up and a straight ripple pattern. Slow travel speeds might cause the puddle to seep out the joint while welding out of position.
