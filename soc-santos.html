The significance of having an international body to provide global governance among state members is to ensure stability, peace, and cooperation among nations in addressing global issues that affect the entire international community. Concrete examples of international bodies providing global governance are the United Nations (UN), the World Trade Organization (WTO), and the International Criminal Court (ICC). The UN is tasked with maintaining international peace and security, promoting human rights, and promoting cooperation among nations in addressing economic, social, and environmental issues. The WTO is responsible for promoting free trade and resolving disputes between member states. The ICC, on the other hand, is responsible for investigating and prosecuting individuals for war crimes, crimes against humanity, and genocide.

These international bodies play a critical role in shaping the global political and economic landscape, and in promoting international cooperation and stability. They help to create a more just and equitable world by promoting the rule of law, and by ensuring that the voices and interests of all nations are heard and respected.
