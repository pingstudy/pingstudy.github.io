4. what is the weight of a steel plate 15 ft. long, 4 ft. wide, and 5/8 inch thick, If steel weighs 475 lb./ft.^3 ?


Given: 
l = 15 ft
w = 4 ft
h = 5/8 in ----> thick is the height of steel plate.
density = 475 lb/ft³

Solution:

Convert 5/8 in to ft.

= 5/8 * 1/12  
= (5/8)/12 
h = 5/96 ft

Find the volume of the plate by using the formula V = lwh.

V = lwh
= (15)(4)(5/96)
= 300/96
= 3.125 ft³  

3.125 ft³ is the volume of the plate.

To find the weight of steel plate, use the formula weight = volume * density.

475 * 3.125 = 1484.375 lbs.

The weight of a steel plate is 1484.375 lbs.

5x + 7x = 24

Combining like terms, we get:

12x = 24

Dividing both sides by 12, we get:

x = 2

Now that we have the value of x, we can find the lengths of each portion by multiplying 5x and 7x by 5 and 7, respectively:

Length of first portion = 5x = 5(2) = 10 feet

Length of second portion = 7x = 7(2) = 14 feet

Therefore, the lengths of the two portions of the steel bar are 10 feet and 14 feet, respectively, when the bar is cut in a ratio of 5:7.

To find the lengths of the two portions of the steel bar, we need to divide the bar into two parts in the ratio of 5:7.

First, we need to determine the total number of parts that make up the ratio, which is 5+7 = 12.

Then, we can find the length of each part by dividing the total length of the steel bar (24 feet) by the total number of parts in the ratio (12):

Length of each part = 24 feet ÷ 12 = 2 feet

Now we can determine the lengths of the two portions of the steel bar by multiplying the length of each part by the corresponding ratio:

Length of first portion = 2 feet x 5 = 10 feet
