Since we don't have information about R2 and L2, let's assume they are in parallel with each other and with R1 and L1. In this case, the equivalent resistance of the parallel combination of R2 and L2 is:

R_parallel = 1 / (1/R2 + 1/R3)

where R3 is the total resistance of the series combination of R1 and L1:

R3 = R1 + XL1 = R1 + 2*pi*f*L1

where XL1 is the inductive reactance of L1 and f is the frequency of the circuit.

X_parallel = 1 / (1/XL2 + 1/XL3)

Finally, the total impedance of the circuit is:

Z_total = sqrt((R1 + 1 / (1/R2 + 1/R3))^2 + (XL1 + 1 / (1/XL2 + 1/XL3))^2)
