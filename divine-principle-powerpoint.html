1.) Divine command theory is a framework or principle that holds that an action is morally right or wrong based on whether it is commanded or forbidden by a deity. This means that the morality of an action is not determined by its consequences or the intentions of the actor, but rather by the will of God.

2.) If I were the Batman and given the chance to kill the Joker, I would not do so according to the divine command theory. This is because the deity I believe in (whether that be God, Allah, Yahweh, or another) has likely not explicitly commanded or forbidden the act of killing the Joker. Therefore, I cannot determine the morality of the act based on divine command theory. Instead, I would have to consider other moral frameworks or principles, such as the principle of respect for human life, to determine whether killing the Joker would be morally right or wrong.
