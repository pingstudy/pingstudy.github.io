Linear Pair Of Angles

When two lines intersect at a single point, a linear pair of angles is formed. If the angles are adjacent to each other after the two lines intersect, they are said to be linear. The sum of the angles of a linear pair is always 180°. These angles are also referred to as supplementary angles. The adjacent angles are those that share a vertex. As a result, the linear angles have a common vertex here as well. There will also be a common arm that represents both angles. A ladder placed against a wall forms linear angles at the ground, which is an example of a linear pair in real life.

A linear pair of angles is always supplementary because the non-adjacent sides of a linear pair form a line. However, the fact that two angles are supplementary does not imply that they form a linear pair. ABC and DBE are supplementary in the diagram below because 30°+150°=180°, but they do not form a linear pair because they are not adjacent.

5. if mQU = 2x- 8cm and mUS = x+3cm then what is mQS?​


To find mQS, we need to use the fact that the sum of the measures of the angles in a triangle is 180 degrees. Since mQU, mUS, and mQS form a triangle, we can write:

mQU + mUS + mQS = 180

Substituting the given values, we get:

(2x - 8) + (x + 3) + mQS = 180

Simplifying the equation, we get:

3x - 5 + mQS = 180

To solve for mQS, we can isolate it on one side of the equation by subtracting 3x - 5 from both sides:

mQS = 180 - (3x - 5)

Simplifying this expression, we get:

mQS = 185 - 3x

Therefore, the measure of angle mQS is 185 - 3x degrees.

To do this, we can use the fact that triangle ∆MQR is similar to triangle ∆SNP, since they are both right triangles with the same angle at Q and P. This means that the ratios of their corresponding side lengths are equal:

MQ/QS = SN/SP

15/QR = 17/31

17QR = 15 × 31

QR = (15 × 31)/17

QR = 27.94 (rounded to two decimal places)

Perimeter = QR + QS + RS

Perimeter = 27.94 + 17 + NP (since RS = NP)

We are not given the length of NP, so we cannot find the exact perimeter. However, we can write the perimeter in terms of NP:

Perimeter = 44.94 + NP

Therefore, the perimeter of triangle ∆QRS is 44.94 plus the length of NP.

30. factor the following expressions by grouping.mp+mq+np+nq​

Step-by-step explanation:

Hello there! Here is the solution for your problem:

= mp + mq + np + nq

= (mp + mq) + (np + nq)       [Group similar terms m and n]

= m (p + q) + n (p + q)          [Factor out m and n]

= (m + n) (p + q)                   [Final Answer]
