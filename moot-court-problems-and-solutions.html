21. What are some possible problems that might ​ result from the lack of a national court system? ​


A national court system is the backbone of a country's judiciary. Without a national court system, judgments on situations that are considered of national importance would not be resolved fairly in an impartial trial that takes into account all of the factors of the situation. A lack of a national court system also leads to instability and decentralization as there would be no enforcement of legal principles, and disputes between the parties involved, the people, and the government would also be put in jeopardy.

22. similarities and differences of Higher Court (Review Court) and Lower Court (Trial Court) ​


Answer:

A lower court is a court from which an appeal may be taken. In relation to an appeal from one court to another, the lower court is the court whose decision is being reviewed, which may be the original trial court or appellate court lower in rank than the superior court which is hearing the appeal.

1. Movement:
