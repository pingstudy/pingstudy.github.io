13. Why is the Philippine eagle endangered?


Answer:

The Philippine eagle (Pithecophaga jefferyi) is an endangered species primarily due to habitat loss caused by human activity. The Philippine eagle is found only in the Philippines, and is considered a national symbol.

The Philippines has experienced significant deforestation over the past century, primarily due to logging, mining, and land conversion for agriculture and urbanization. This has led to the destruction of the Philippine eagle's natural habitat, leaving only small fragments of its original range. As a result, the eagle's population has become fragmented and isolated, making it difficult for them to find mates and reproduce.

Another major factor that contributes to the decline of Philippine eagles is hunting. The bird is hunted for its feathers, which are used for traditional ceremonial dress, and for its meat, which is considered a delicacy.

The Philippine eagle is also vulnerable to other human-related activities such as poisoning, accidental capture and electrocution, and also persecution by farmers who sometimes see them as pests.

Conservation efforts have been ongoing, including captive breeding programs and habitat restoration and protection, education, and awareness campaigns. International conservation groups such as the Philippine Eagle Foundation, Haribon Foundation and the World Wildlife Fund are actively working to save the species and its habitat.

30. Opical fishes .7. Massive production of coral may endanger the marine life.​


Answer:

Human threats to coral reefs

Overfishing and the use of destructive fishing methods have decimated fish populations on reefs and destroyed their habitats as well. Additionally, elevated surface temperatures caused by worldwide climate change lead to coral bleaching more frequently.

How does coral affect marine life?

Thousands of marine animals depend on coral reefs for survival, including some species of sea turtles, fish, crabs, shrimp, jellyfish, sea birds, starfish, and more. Coral reefs provide shelter, spawning grounds, and protection from predators. They also support organisms at the base of ocean food chains.

What causes the lives of corals to be endangered?

Coral reefs are endangered by a variety of factors, including: natural phenomena such as hurricanes, El Niño, and diseases; local threats such as overfishing, destructive fishing techniques, coastal development, pollution, and careless tourism; and the global effects of climate change—warming seas and increasing levels
