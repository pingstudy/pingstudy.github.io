12. Academic texts are based on researches. It is best to check the year of publication of the materials you are using because?​


Answer:

Academic texts such as those found in G Scholar are great sources of information since these text were peer-reviewed and checked by referees before their publication. Peer review means a panel of experts on the field of study of the academic paper read and determined that the paper is statistically, academically, and theoretically sound.

The downside of using indexed academic texts is that while the content, methods used, and the study method used were relevant, correct, and factful at the time of its publication, new and emerging studies may come to refute or provide better answers. As an example, in academic texts and publications before 2006, Pluto was a planet. But after a deliberation made by  International Astronomical Union (IAU) in 2006, Pluto was deemed not a planet anymore but instead categorized as plutoid or a minor planet. If astronomy researchers still use the pre-2006 description of Pluto in their 2021 academic text, the study will be deemed irrelevant and inaccurate.
