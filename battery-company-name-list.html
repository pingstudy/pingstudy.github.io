1. Batteries produced by a certain company have a mean lifetime of 25 hours which is the mean time between failures is 25 hours. The electronic testing machine for the batteries was assigned by the computer analyst who is the owner of the company. The purchasing officer of the customer company purchased 1000 of these batteries. What is the probability that a single battery will last more than 30 hours? How many of these batteries will last at most 20 hours?


Answer:

The probability that a single battery will last more than 30 hours can be calculated using the cumulative distribution function (CDF) of the exponential distribution with mean 25. The answer is approximately 0.303 or 30.3%.The number of these batteries that will last at most 20 hours follows a Poisson distribution with mean λ = 1000*(1 - e^(-20/25)) ≈ 284. Thus, approximately 284 of these batteries will last at most 20 hours.

8. 4. Rechargeable batteries are called nickel-cadmium batteries a. b. d. primary batteries secondary batteries storage batteries​


Answer:

Secondary batteries

Explanation:

Rechargeable batteries, sometimes known as secondary batteries, are intended to be recharged and reused several times. They differ from primary batteries, which are intended to be used once and then destroyed, and storage batteries, which are often huge and used to generate electricity in off-grid applications. Nickel-cadmium (NiCd) batteries are a type of secondary battery.
