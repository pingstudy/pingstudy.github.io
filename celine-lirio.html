1. kahulugan ng lirio. ​


ENGLISH DEFINITION:

Lirio is a lily plant, with subglobose bulbs up to 10 centimeters in diameter. Leaves are many, oblong-linear, 30 to 100 centimeters long, 5 to 8 centimeters wide. Scape is inserted on neck of the bulb, about as long as the leaves, stout and tinged with purple. Flowers are about 10 in each umbel, fragrant, about 8 centimeters long. Perianth tube is curved, 10 to 12 centimeters long. Segments are oblong-lanceolate, 7.5 to 10 centimeters long, 2.5 to 3 centimeters wide, white but flushed with pale purple in the median part. Filaments are white. Fruit is somewhat rounded, 4 to 5 centimeters in diameter. The cultivated hybrid forms give a variety of colors, some with white stripes running from the center of red segments.

FILIPINO (Tagalog):

Ang Lirio ay isang halaman ng liryo, na may mga bombilya ng subglobose hanggang sa 10 sentimetro ang lapad. Ang mga dahon ay marami, pahaba-guhit, 30 hanggang 100 sent sentimo ang haba, 5 hanggang 8 sent sentimo ang lapad. Ang scape ay ipinasok sa leeg ng bombilya, kasing haba ng mga dahon, mataba at may kulay na lila. Ang mga bulaklak ay humigit-kumulang 10 sa bawat umbel, mahalimuyak, mga 8 sent sentimo ang haba. Ang Perianth tube ay hubog, 10 hanggang 12 sent sentimo ang haba. Ang mga segment ay oblong-lanceolate, 7.5 hanggang 10 sent sentimo ang haba, 2.5 hanggang 3 sent sentimo ang lapad, maputi ngunit namula ng maputlang lila sa bahagi ng panggitna. Puti ang mga filament. Ang prutas ay medyo bilugan, 4 hanggang 5 sentimetro ang lapad. Ang mga nilinang hybrid form ay nagbibigay ng iba't ibang mga kulay, ang ilan ay may puting guhitan na tumatakbo mula sa gitna ng mga pulang segment.

10. the prayer celine dion melody​


"The Prayer" is a song by Canadian singer Celine Dion and Italian tenor Andrea Bocelli. It was written by David Foster, Carole Bayer Sager, Alberto Testa and Tony Renis. "The Prayer" was originally recorded in two solo versions for the 1998 film Quest for Camelot, in English by Dion and in Italian by Bocelli. A duet between Dion and Bocelli later appeared on their respective studio albums, These Are Special Times (1998) and Sogno (1999), and was released as a promotional single on 1 March 1999. "The Prayer" won a Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song and was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Original Song in 1999 and a Grammy Award for Best Pop Collaboration with Vocals in 2000. It received positive reviews from music critics and entered adult contemporary charts in Canada and the United States in 1999. In 2008, Dion released a live version of "The Prayer" as a duet with Josh Groban. This music download entered the Canadian Hot 100 at number 37 and the Billboard Hot 100 at number 70.

Musically, this change in attitude is signaled through the form, melody, harmony, tone color, dynamics, texture, tempo, and rhythm.The form of this song is a modification of a standard verse-chorus form. The chorus appears immediately after the verse and features a different melodic line that is heard twice with two different texts. After a bridge, we hear another verse (same melody, different text) and another statement of the chorus (same melody, same text). Another bridge leads into a final statement of the chorus which ends the song.

This final chorus is remarkable in that the text is different from the two preceding choruses. The use of the same chorus melody for a different text highlights the different standpoints of the speaker from the beginning of the song to the end.Two distinct melodies are used in this song, one for the verses and one for the chorus. 26. what does ca celin represent


How my Brother Leon Brought Home a Wife was a short story written by Manuel E. Arguilla in 1940, it was archived as part of the American colonial literature. The story talks about a countryside guy who had a brother named Leon, who brought home a wife from the city. The wife, named Maria, arrives in a carretela, a vehicle with wheels that is drawn by one or two horses. They pick her up and bring her to their home, to introduce to their family, for the most part, the story revolves on them talking on their way back home. Discussing on how Maria would handle and adjust to the countryside life, knowing, that she was born and raised in the city. The main character, named Baldo, also wondered if she can do it, but her brother believed that she can and she was willing to learn and adjust. En route, Leon, noticed something weird, they weren't taking the usual route, instead they took the longer one and he questioned Baldo about it. As it turns out, Baldo was ordered by his father to take this route for reasons unknown. It was then that Maria started to think that Leon's father would not like her, but Leon reassured her, until they arrived.

Upon arrival, Baldo was called by his father. He asked him what he saw, what he thought about her and Baldo stated that she was good, capable, and he liked her. He also seemingly asked, if he would be a suitable wife to his brother, in which he seemed to agree to. His father, then asks him to give Labang, his bull, which they rode on for the most of the story some water. As he exits the room, he could not help but look at Maria and admire her from a distance.

To answer the question. What does ca celin represent? Ca celin represents Baldo or rather Baldo's feelings for Maria. In the beginning of the story, Maria stepped down from the carretela of Ca celin, later in the story Maria stepped down from Baldo's cart. Also, before they were about to leave for their house, Ca celin is said to be looking at Maria so intently that he could not keep his eyes off of her, which then happened to Baldo at the end of the story. The story is an example of an oblivious love. He was struck by her by the moment he saw her, but he could not bring himself to because she was his brother's wife and he was not fully aware of it.
