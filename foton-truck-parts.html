Explanation"Jingle truck" origin

The term jingle truck comes from the United States military slang, coined by servicemen in Afghanistan, although it may date to the British colonial period. The term came to be because of the "jingle" sound that the trucks make due to the chains hanging from the bumpers of the vehicles.[4]

Practice

Many trucks and buses are highly customized and decorated by their owners. External truck decoration can cost thousands of dollars.[5] The decoration often contains elements that remind the truck drivers of home, since they may be away from home for months at a time.[6] Art is a mode of expression for truck drivers.[7] Decoration may include structural changes, paintings, calligraphy, and ornamental-decor-like mirror work on the front and back of vehicles and wooden carvings on the truck doors. Depictions of various historical scenes and poetic verses are also common.[8] Outfitting is often completed at a coaching workshop.[9] Chains and pendants often dangle off the front bumper.[10]

Artists

Phool Ji an artist named Mr. Syed Phool Badshah is a well-known truck artist who is best known for his unique style of doing Fine Arts with Truck art.[11]

Nafees Ahmad Khan, a truck art artist in Indore, is well known throughout India and has been designing one truck every day for over thirty-two years.[7]

One of the most prominent truck artists is Haider Ali. Trained by his father from his youth, he first came to international attention in 2002 when he painted a Pakistani truck as part of the Smithsonian Folklife Festival.[12]

Regional styles

Many local buses in Pakistan are decorated with truck art.

In Pakistan, Karachi is a major city center for truck art, though there are other hubs in Rawalpindi, Swat, Peshawar, Quetta, and Lahore. Trucks from Balochistan and Peshawar are often heavily trimmed with wood, while trucks from Rawalpindi and Islamabad often feature plastic work. Camel bone ornamentation and predominance of red colors are commonly seen on trucks decorated in Sindh.[8]

In India, the Delhi-based artist Tilak Raj Dhir states that the slogans he adds to his truck art, which is prevalent throughout the National Capital Region, often change with the socio-political atmosphere.[13] Truck art in Hindi and Urdu is sometimes called Phool Patti.[14][15]

Influence

Truck art decorates the façade of business in Peshawar, Pakistan.

Truck art has extended beyond the decoration and ornamentation of trucks into other forms and media.

Cars

Though cars are not traditionally decorated in South Asia, there are examples of cars embellished in a truck art style. In 2009, The Foxy Shahzadi, a 1974 VW Beetle decorated in a truck art style, traveled from Pakistan to France in a 25-day journey.[16][17] In the Indian city of Mumbai, some drivers decorate their taxis in a truck art style.[18]

Fashion

The lively colors of Pakistani trucks have inspired some fashion designers.[19] The Italian fashion company Dolce & Gabbana used truck art-inspired displays in a 2015 campaign.[20] Although used more often in women's fashion, some men's clothing has been inspired by South Asian truck art.[21]:

What is the mass of the second truck?2. A Blue Truck and Green Truck are identical truck that are on a flat, horizontal smooth surface. The blue truck is motionless, while the green truck is moving 3.5m/s to the left and collides to blue truck. the toy trucks have a mass of 25g. After the collision, the blue truck is moving at 3m/s to the left. What is the final velocity of the green truck?================================NONSENSE/INCOMPLETE = REPORTCORRECT ANSWER = BRAINLEIST================================​


1.) Given:

m1 = 28,000 kgs.

v1 = 8 m/s

v2 = 0 m/s (stationary)

Vt = 5 m/s

Solution:

[tex] (mv)_{1} + (mv)_{2} = (mv)_{t} [/tex]

[tex](28000 \times 8) + ( m_{2} \times 0) = (28000 + m_{2})(5) [/tex]

[tex]224000 + 0 = 140000 + 5 m_{2}[/tex]

[tex] m_{2} = \frac{224000 - 140000}{5} [/tex]

[tex] m_{2} = 16800 \: kgs.[/tex]

2.) Given:

m1 & m2 = 25 kgs.

v1 = 0 m/s (motionless)

v2 = 3.5 m/s (to the left)

vt1 = 3 m/s (to the left)

Solution:

[tex] (mv)_{1} + (mv)_{2} = (mv)_{t1} + (mv)_{t2}[/tex]

[tex](25 \times 0) + (25)( - 3.5) = (25)( - 3) + (25) (v)_{t2}[/tex]

[tex]0 - 87.5 = - 75 + 25 v_{t2}[/tex]

[tex] v_{t2} = \frac{ - 87.5 + 75}{25} [/tex]

[tex] v_{t2} = - 0.5 \: \frac{m}{s} \: [/tex]

or

[tex] v_{t2} = 0.5 \: \frac{m}{s} (to \: the \: left)[/tex]

29. A truck was 7/8 filled with grocery items for delivery. The driver delivered 2/3 of this to supermarket. What part of the truckload of grocery items was delivered? What part of the truck load was mot delivered?(serious answer po :)) ​


Linear Equation and It's Application in Real Life

In the given problem, it is an application of linear equation and solving word problem about unknown variables. It is a combination of general mathematics and algebra.

The given problem is "A truck was 7/8 filled with grocery items for delivery. The driver delivered 2/3 of this to supermarket. What part of the truckload of grocery items was delivered? What part of the truck load was mot delivered?"

Step-by-step Solution and DiscussionStep 1: List down all the given data of the problem

7/8 filled with grocery items for delivery

delivered 2/3 of this to supermarket

Step 2: Identify what is being asked

What part of the truckload of grocery items was delivered? What part of the truck load was not delivered?.

Step 3: Identify what operation to be used

The operation to be used are subtraction and multiplication

Step 4: Create mathematical sentence

Let x is the part of truckload of grocery items was delivered

x = 2/3 of 7/8 → x = (2/3)(7/8)

Let y is the part of truckload of grocery items was not delivered.

1 - 2/3 = represent as fraction part of truckload of grocery item was not delivered, thus

y = (1-2/3)(7/8)

Step 5: Solve the problem

x = (2/3)(7/8), in multiplying fraction, just multiply numerator to numerator, and then denominator to denominator.

x = (2/3)(7/8)

x = 14/24, simplify to its lowest term (use GCF).

The GCF of 14 and 24 is 2.

14/24 ÷ 2/2 = 7/12

Thus, 7/12 of the truckload of grocery items was delivered.

y = (1-2/3)(7/8)

Note, 1 is equal to 3/3.

y = (3/3 - 2/3)(7/8), in subtracting fraction, subtract the numerator then copy the denominator.

y = (1/3)(7/8)

y = 7/24

There is also other solution, it was 7/8 - 7/12 = 7/24.

Thus, 7/24 of the truckload of grocery items was not delivered.
