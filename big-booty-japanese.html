9. A Cambodia instrument which is a two big drums and similar to Japanese instrument​


Answer:

Skor thom

Explanation:

The Skor Thom are Cambodia 2-headed barrel drums played with a pair of wooden drumsticks. They typically have skin heads made from oxen, cows or buffalos and are played in pairs; the drums are tuned such that one will give a "tighter and louder" sound when struck, while the other gives a "loose and more flatter tone.


10. it is an instrument having two big drums similar to Japanese drums​


Answer:

Tsuzumi, any of a family of Japanese two-headed drums with hourglass-shaped (waisted) bodies. The two most commonly used tsuzumi are the ko-tsuzumi and the ō-tsuzumi, found in the music of Noh and Kabuki theatres. The tsuzumi is related to the Korean changgo, a large hourglass-shaped, two-headed drum.
