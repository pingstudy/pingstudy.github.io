Likewise, Is there a face on a sphere?

A face is a flat surface on a solid, and edges are the lines at which faces meet, and a vertex is the point at which when three or more edges meet. A sphere has no flat surfaces, so it has no faces. Since it doesn’t have faces, it can’t have edges, or vertices.

Also, Does sphere have face?

A sphere has no faces, a cone has one circular face, and a cylinder has two circular faces. Therefore, the number of faces increases by one from one figure to the next.

Spirituality and Religion:

Answers:

d. a quest for faith b. religiousness b. sacred space c. worldview b. true

Explanation:

Spirituality:

One does not need to attain a quest for faith in order to attain spirituality. This comes from the fact that spirituality means differently for different people. There are experiences that are spiritual in nature but are categorized as non – religious such as meditation, quiet reflection, private prayer, time with nature, and yoga.  

Higher levels of spirituality have been linked to improved self-esteem, increased compassion, and strengthened relationships. Spirituality is an extensive concept that involves many ideas. In general, it includes a sense of connection to something bigger than oneselves, and it typically involves a search for a meaningful life.

Definition of Spirituality: https://brainly.ph/question/1826263

Religiousness:

Religiousness refers to the process of developing a set of criteria for defining religion and spirituality. Religiousness is a peripheral tool through which individuals can access their spirituality and relationship to the divine.

Definition of Religiousness: https://brainly.ph/question/365864

Sacred Space:

A sacred space is considered an element of religion. Sacred space includes a wide variety of very different kinds of places. It includes places that are built for religious purposes including temples or temenoi, and places that are religiously interpreted including mountains or rivers. It includes spaces that can be entered physically, as the outer geography of a holy land, inspirationally, as the inner geography of the body in Tantric yoga, or visually, as the space of a mandala.

This view of sacred space as a lens for meaning implies that places are sacred because they perform a religious function, not because they have peculiar physical or aesthetic qualities. Three roles of sacred space are especially significant, for they are widely attested in religious systems and fundamental to their purposes. First, sacred space is a means of communication with the gods and about the gods. Second, it is a place of divine power. And third, it serves as a visible icon of the world and thereby imparts a form to it and an organization to its inhabitants.

Worldview:

Worldview refers to the particular way of ordering the realities of one’s world. Worldview is more than culture as it extends to perceptions of time and space, of happiness and of well-being. In fact, the beliefs, values, and behaviors of a culture stem from its worldviews. Derived from the German term ‘weltanschauung,’ the term worldview refers to the series of beliefs an individual have regarding the most significant concepts of life such as God, the cosmos (universe), and humanity. These beliefs, which may or may not be true, form a general picture, a broad-spectrum outlook, or a grand perspective on life and the world.

Definition of Worldview: https://brainly.ph/question/1974680

Multidimensionality of Religion and Spirituality:

Both religion and spirituality are multidimensional. Religion is a multidimensional event. One’s acceptance of and position towards a supernatural being, towards an ultimate reality and its manifestations involve a multidimensional process that includes attitudes, beliefs, emotions, experiences, rituals, and community and belonging. Spirituality is multidimensional since it is compose of a search for a meaningful life, an encounter with transcendence, a sense of community, a search for ultimate truth or highest value, a respect and appreciation for the mystery of creation, and a personal transformation.
