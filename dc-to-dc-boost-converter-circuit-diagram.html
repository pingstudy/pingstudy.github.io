24. ​​​​​​​A transformer is a device which step-up or step-down the level of AC current or voltage. Can a transformer be used in DC circuits? Why or why not? Differentiate AC from a DC circuit.


The basic principle of a transformer actually depends upon induction. So it is clear that when we apply a voltage across the primary coil of the transformer, it magnetizes the core of it and due to this magnetic field, there comes an EMF across the secondary coil following the Faraday’s Law. Now, if we apply the DC voltage across the primary, the magnitude of voltage is uniform with respect to time. So there will be no magnetic field in the core and the transformer will not work. But due to constant voltage supply to the iron core, the core will turn into a electromagnetic rather producing a magnetic field across it. Thus, the answer is no. You cannot use DC cicrcuits in transformers. Why not? As transformers are based on mutual inductance property, DC doesn’t have this property because of non varying flux produced by it. Therefore no EMF would be generated in the secondary winding of transformer. Hence, no transformation of power from primary winding to secondary winding.

The difference between AC and DC lies in the direction in which the electrons flow. In DC, the electrons flow steadily in a single direction, or "forward." In AC, electrons keep switching directions, sometimes going "forward" and then going "backward." It describes the flow of charge that changes direction periodically. As a result, the voltage level also reverses along with the current.

30. Explain how ac converted to dc


AC (alternating current) can be converted to DC (direct current) using a device called a rectifier. A rectifier allows current to flow in only one direction by using a diode, which is an electronic component that acts as a one-way valve for electric current. When AC is applied to the diode, it allows current to flow in one direction during the positive half cycle of the AC signal, and blocks the flow of current during the negative half cycle. This effectively converts the AC signal into a pulsating DC signal. To smooth out this pulsating DC signal into a steady DC voltage, a filter circuit can be used, which uses capacitors and/or inductors to smooth out the waveform. The resulting output is a stable DC voltage that can be used to power electronic devices that require a DC power source.
