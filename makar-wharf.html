RELATED RATES

Problem:

A man on a wharf is pulling a rope tied to a raft at a rate of 0.6 m/s. if the hands of the rope are 3.66 m above the water, how fast is the raft approaching the wharf when there are 6.10 m of rope out?

Solution:

In order to solve this problem involving application of differential calculus, specifically related rates, we need to list down first the given data:

When we try to illustrate the scenario, we can see that a right triangle is formed so we use the Pythagorean theorem:

            [tex]x^2 + y^2 = z^2[/tex]

0.6 meter per second refers to the velocity of the rope being pulled. The length of the rope can be represented by "z". So, 0.6 m/s is dz/dt.3.66 m above the water can be represented by "y".6.10 m of rope out is the value of "z".

What we need to solve for now is the value of x. Using the Pythagorean theorem:

         [tex]x^2 + y^2 = z^2\\x = \sqrt{z^2 - y^2} \\x = \sqrt{6.10^2 - 3.66^2} \\x = 4.88[/tex]

Now, what we are looking for is how fast the raft is approaching to the wharf as it is being pulled by a man which can be represented by "dx/dt". Let us do partial differentiation to the Pythagorean theorem.

      [tex]x^2 + y^2 = z^2\\2x \frac{dx}{dt} + 0 = 2z \frac{dz}{dt} \\x \frac{dx}{dt} = z \frac{dz}{dt} \\[/tex]

Since y is a constant, it becomes zero after it was derived. As we continue, we substitute the values we listed above to solve for dx/dt:

     [tex]x \frac{dx}{dt} = z \frac{dz}{dt} \\4.88 \frac{dx}{dt} = 6.10(0.6)\\\frac {dx}{dt} = 0.75[/tex]

Therefore, the raft is approaching to the wharf at a speed of 0.75 m/s.
