6. How do you perform the four operations of integers?(Don't ask me bad stuff)​


Hello~ （￣︶￣）Addition

when the signs are the same you add normally and keep the sign.

when the signs are different you subtract and keep the sign of the larger number.

examples:

(-7) + 4 = ( -7 ) - 4

       -3 = -3

the signs are different here so you subtract and keep the sign of the bigger number ( -7 ) which leads to your answer, -3.

there's a song you can sing if you forget the rules ( by the tune of row your boat ) :

same signs add and keep

different signs subtract

keep the sign of the higher number

and it'll be exact !!

Subtraction

for subtraction we use a term called COCHOP !

COCHOP basically means, COpy, CHange, OPposite

you copy the first number, change the operation from subtraction to addition, and make the sign of the next number opposite.

examples:

( -7 ) - 4 =

( -7 ) + ( -4 ) = -11

in here, we kept the sign of -7, change the operation to addition, and switched the sign of 4 from positive to negative which leads to our answer, -11

Multiplication and Division

multiplication and division have the same rules so bear with me here ~

when you multiply/divide integers with the same signs it will be positive, when the signs are different it will be negative.

it's very simple !!

-7 • -4 = 24 ( positive )

-8 ÷ 4 = -2 ( negative )
