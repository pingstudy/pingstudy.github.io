2. Suppose a cinema has 42 rows of seats and there are 20 seats in the first row.


Answer:

2562

Step-by-step explanation:

F = a+(n-1)*d

a=20 n=42 d=2

F = 20+(42–1)*2 = 20+41*2=20+82=102

The last row has 102 seats

S = (n/2)*(a+F)

S = (42/2)*(20+102) = 21*122 = 2562

There are 2562 seats in the cinema

4. Suppose a cinema has 42 rows of seats and there are 20 seats in the firstrow. Each row after the first row has two more seats that the row that itprecedes. How many seats are in the cinema? with solution​


PROBLEM

Suppose a cinema has 42 rows of seats and there are 20 seats in the first row. Each row after the first row has two more seats that the row that it precedes. How many seats are in the cinema?

ANSWERThere are a total of 2,562 seats in the cinema

SOLUTION

The problem is the same as an Arithmetic Sequence with a common difference of 2 and a total of 42 terms. But since it is asking for the sum of all the seats, we'll use the Arithmetic Series formula.

[tex]S_{n} = \frac{n}{2}[2a_{1} + (n - 1)(d)] \\ S_{42} = \frac{42}{2}[2(20) + (42 - 1)(2)] \\ S_{42} = 21[40 + (41)(2)] \\ S_{42} = 21(40 + 82) \\ S_{42} = 21(122) \\\boxed{S_{42} = 2562}[/tex]
