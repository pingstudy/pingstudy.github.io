3. The Mariel Tub Company manufactures two lines of bathtubs called model A and model B. Every tub requires blending a certain amount of steel and zinc; the company has available a total of 2500kg of steel and 600kg of zinc. Each model A bathtub requires a mixture of 12.5kg of steel and 2kg of zinc wherein each yields a profit of 9000php. Each model B tub produced can be sold for a profit of 7000php; it in turn requires 10kg of steel and 3kg of zinc. Find the best production mix of bathtubs.


Answer:

The best production mix of bathtubs between the two models is model B.

Step-by-step explanation:

We have steps to follow in answering word problems.

Analyze the Problem

Let us analyze the given problem first. Based on the problem, the company manufactures two bathtubs which requires different amount of steel and zinc for each production. We need to know which production mix of bathtubs is best.

2. What are the given?

Model A - requires 12.5 kg of steel and 2 kg of zinc with a profit of 9000 php

Model B - requires 10 kg of steel and 3 kg of zinc with a profit of 7000 php

3. Solution

Model A

(steel) 2500 ÷ 12.5 = 200

(zinc) 600 ÷ 2 = 300

* For this amount of steel and zinc, the company can produce 200 bathtubs with 9000 php profit.

Model B

(steel) 2500 ÷ 10 = 250

(zinc) 600 ÷ 3 = 200

* The company can also produce 200 bathtubs with a profit of 7000 php.

4. Give the Final Answer

Based on our answers, we can clearly tell that the best production of bathtubs is Model B, in terms of the amount of steel and zinc needed for the production with a lesser or cheaper profit needed.

29. Two thousand kilogram of steel containing 8% of nickel is to be made by mixing steel containing 14% nickel with another steel containing 6% nickel. How much of the steel containing 14% nickel is needed? ​


Let the quantity of the steel containing 14% nickel be a and the one containing 6% be b.

1. a + b = 2,000

Multiply each by their percentage.

(14%)a + (6%)b = (8%)2000

2. 14a + 6b = 16,000

Use system of equations to find a. Multiply equation #1 to 6 so we can cancel out b.

14a + 6b =  16,000

-6a  - 6b = -12,000

8a           =   4,000

a = 500

You need 500 kg of steel containing 14% nickel.
