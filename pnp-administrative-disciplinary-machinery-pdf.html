11. refers to the administrative machinery of the state​


The term "administrative machinery of the state" refers to the system of government agencies and officials that are responsible for carrying out the day-to-day functions of the state. This includes the executive branch, the legislative branch, and the judicial branch.

The executive branch is responsible for implementing the laws passed by the legislature. It is headed by the president or prime minister, who is assisted by a cabinet of ministers. The executive branch also includes the civil service, which is responsible for carrying out the day-to-day work of the government.

The legislative branch is responsible for making laws. It is made up of a parliament or congress, which is elected by the people. The legislative branch also has the power to approve or reject the president or prime minister, and to declare war.

The judicial branch is responsible for interpreting the laws and ensuring that they are applied fairly. It is headed by a supreme court, which is made up of judges who are appointed by the president or prime minister. The judicial branch also has the power to strike down laws that it deems unconstitutional.

The administrative machinery of the state is essential for the smooth functioning of the government. It is responsible for carrying out the laws, providing services to the people, and protecting the country from harm.

30. explain the composition of the disciplinary board ​


Answer:

The Disciplinary Board is comprised of 12 members (10 attorneys, 2 non-attorneys) appointed by the Supreme Court for a term of six years, unless otherwise ordered. The Supreme Court designates one member as the Board Chair and one member as the Board Vice Chair
