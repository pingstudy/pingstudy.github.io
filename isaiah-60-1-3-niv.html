2. Write a reflection about: Isaiah 60: 1-6 John 1:29-34 1 Corinthians 1: 1-3


Answer:

Isaiah 60: 1-6: These passages emphasize the significance of light in our lives, both physically and spiritually. Isaiah 60:1-6 speaks of the glory of God shining upon us, and how we should rise and shine as his people. This highlights the idea that God's light can bring us hope and guidance, even in the darkest of times.

John 1:29-34 tells us that Jesus is the light of the world, and that he came to bring salvation to all people. This passage emphasizes the importance of accepting Jesus as our Lord and Savior, as it is through him that we can find true light and guidance.

Corinthians 1:1-3 highlights the role of the Holy Spirit in guiding us towards a deeper understanding of God's love and grace. This passage reminds us that, as believers, we are called to be witnesses of God's love to the world and to live our lives in a way that brings honor to his name.

Attitude

Jesus uses the healing of the man with the withered hand to point out that the Sabbath was not just a time not to work. The Pharisees watched Jesus like a cat watches a mouse hole. They stood ready to pounce on Him for every little thing that violated their legalistic beliefs. The Jews permitted healing on the Sabbath Day only if there was danger that the person might die before the next day. Unless this was the case, healing on the Sabbath, to them, was working. Jesus had broken these regulations and they were genuinely convinced that He was a bad man. This is sort of like a person who believes that true Christianity consists of going to church, reading the Bible, saying grace at meals etc., but is deaf to the needs of people. Jesus was grieved because of the hardness of their hearts and angry at the callous, cruel attitude of the Pharisees toward human suffering and need.

To refuse to do good is to choose to do evil (James 4:17). We cannot truly love God unless we hate sin. Instead of rejoicing about the man being healed the Pharisees left the synagogue to hold counsel with the Herodians as to how they might destroy Jesus. These Herodians were supporters of Roman rule. Ordinarily the Pharisees would have nothing to do with them, but now sought their cooperation in order to fulfill their purpose. It’s easy for us as sinners to develop bad attitudes. My reflection about Matthew 20:28 is centered on Jesus' teachings about "servant leadership" and what it means to be a leader.

"For Jesus came into this world not to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many."

This came when some of his disciples asked who is the greatest among them. They normally think that to be the greatest is tantamount to being the leader of the twelve. But to their surprise, Jesus' concept of leadership, and of being great is not in congruence with theirs.

For Jesus, to be great is to be the least of all, to be a leader is to be the servant of all. This is so becasue for Jesus, the true essence of leadership is "servant leadership".

Thus, he told his disciples, "if anyone who wish to be the leader of the twelve, he must be the servant of all, if anyone wish to be great, must make himself the least of all".

Jesus, showed them the essence of servant leadership in his ministry. He, being the son of God, exhibited the value of humility by serving his disciples.

In a single instance, Jesus was seen cleansing and washing the feet of all his disciples. He chose to be the least of them, he chose to be a leader by serving them.

Jesus' ministry is centered on serving the the people of his time. He served the possessed by driving away the demons inside them, he served the sick by healing them, he served the sinners by teaching them the value of repentance. and lastly, he served the world by offering His life for our sins. This is the reason why Jesus came into this world, to serve, and not to be served.

This belongs to a larger section on true versus false fasting. These notes may fill in some holes of understanding for you:

"58:6 fast that I choose. This verse presents a different idea of fasting: to rescue the oppressed and provide for the needy (Matt. 25:34–40)."

58:7 own flesh. Relatives, and secondarily people from the same tribe or area (1 Tim. 5:8).

58:8 Then. This word connects human responsibility and the coming of God’s kingdom.

light . . . glory. The glorious kingdom of God ( Luke 1:78, 79) will dawn with God’s blessing and protection, and with His healing. It is the establishment of a new order , with the presence of the God of glory.

rear guard. Like the pillar of cloud and fire in the wilderness (4:5, 6; Ex. 13:21; 14:20).

58:9 call . . . cry. Then God will answer prayer.

Here I am. Typically this is a courteous answer given by a person who is ready to hear a message or follow instructions. (ex. Here I am, your servant Lord.) (e.g., Gen. 22:1, 7; Ex. 3:4; 1 Sam. 3:4).

yoke . . . speaking wickedness. These expressions represent oppression, contempt, and improper speech.
