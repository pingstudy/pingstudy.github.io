[tex]sn = \frac{n(a1 + an)}{2} [/tex]

Substitute the given values to the formula.

[tex]s20 = \frac{20(9 + 47)}{2} [/tex]

Then simplify.

[tex]s20 = \frac{20(56)}{2} \\ \\ s20 = \frac{1120}{2} \: \: \: \: \\ \\ s20 = 560 \: \: \: \: \: [/tex]

[tex]a_n=a_1+(n-1)d[/tex]

[tex]a_{20}=2+(20-1)5[/tex]

[tex]a_{20}=2+(19)5[/tex]

[tex]a_{20}=2+95[/tex]

[tex]a_{20}=97[/tex]

23. find the general term of an arithmetic sequence with a1 = -5 and d = 4. afterwards, find a10 and a20.given, a1 = 5, d = 4, an =? a10 = ? a20 = ?​


Answer:

General term: an=-5+(n-1)4

Solve for a10:

a10=5+(10-1)4

a10=5+(9)4

a10=5+36

a10=41

Solve for a20:

a20=5+(20-1)4

a20=5+(19)4

a20=5+76

a20=81

EXPLANATION:

The general term of an arithmetic sequence is an=a1+(n-1)d wherein an is the last nth term, a1 is the first term, n is the number of terms in the sequence and d is the common difference.

In solving or looking for a term, just substitute the given values then solve.

In the given, the missing is the 10th term and 20th term.

Steps:

1. Substitute the given values:

an=a1+(n-1)d

a10=5+(10-1)4

2. Subtract the 1 from the n inside the parenthesis which is (10-1)

a10=5+(9)4

3. Multiply the answer from step 2 to the common difference which is 4

a10=5+36

4. Add the 1st term or a1 to the answer from step 3 to get the missing term (a10)

a10=41
