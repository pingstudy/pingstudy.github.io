10. what is the molar mass of Vanadium (V) phosphate (HPO4). *V2(HPO4)5​


Answer:

To calculate the molar mass of V2(HPO4)5, we need to add the atomic masses of all the atoms in the compound, taking into account the subscripts and coefficients.

The atomic mass of vanadium (V) is 50.94 g/mol.

The atomic mass of phosphorus (P) is 30.97 g/mol.

The atomic mass of oxygen (O) is 15.99 g/mol.

The atomic mass of hydrogen (H) is 1.01 g/mol.

For V2(HPO4)5, we have:

2 vanadium atoms, each with a mass of 50.94 g/mol

5 HPO4 groups, each with a total mass of:

1 phosphorus atom (30.97 g/mol) + 4 oxygen atoms (15.99 g/mol each) + 1 hydrogen atom (1.01 g/mol)

= 98.00 g/mol

Therefore, the molar mass of V2(HPO4)5 is:

2(50.94 g/mol) + 5(98.00 g/mol) = 1,109.7 g/mol

Hence, the molar mass of Vanadium (V) phosphate (HPO4). V2(HPO4)5 is 1,109.7 g/mol.

21. Use the combined gas law to solve the following problem. If P1 = 5 atm, V, = 1 L, T, = 300 K, P2 = 10 atm and T2 = 600 K, what is the volume V2? 0 V2 = 10.02 O V2 = 2.0L O V2 = 1.0L O V2 = 0.5 L


COMBINED GAS LAW

[tex]\normalsize{\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad}}}[/tex]

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

We know that the combined gas law is:

[tex]\boxed{\sf{\dfrac{P_{1} V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \dfrac{P_{2} V_{2}}{T_{2}} }}[/tex]

Where:

P₁ = initial pressure.V₁ = initial volume.T₁ = initial temperature.P₂ = final pressure.V₂ = final volume.T₂ = final temperature.

Temperature must be in Kelvin. To convert Celsius to Kelvin, add 273.15.

So our givens are:

P₁ = 5 atmV₁ = 1 LT₁ = 300 KP₂ = 10 atmT₂ = 600 K

Rearrange the equation to isolate V₂:

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = \dfrac{P_{1} V_{1} T_{2}}{P_{2} T_{1}} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = \dfrac{5 \times 1 \times 600}{10 \times 300} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = \dfrac{5 \times 600}{10 \times 300} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = \dfrac{3,000}{3,000} }[/tex]

[tex]\sf{\implies V_{2} = 1.0L}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of V₂ is 1.0 L.

29. The velocity vector of the boat has two components: v1 is the velocity of the water, and v2 is the velocity of the boat relative to the water. If v1 = 3 Km/h and v2 = 5 Km/h, determine the velocity vector of the boat.


Answer:

The velocity vector of the boat is 8 km/h

Explanation:

In this problem, we will be using the concept of relative velocity to solve the vector velocity of the boat. To do this, we will use the formula

V₂ = Vb - V₁

where

V₂     is the velocity of the boat relative to the water according to the

        problem statement

Vb    is the velocity of the boat, the given unit is in km/h

V₁     is the velocity of the water, the given unit is also in km/h

Given information

V₁ = 3 km/h

V₂ = 5 km/h

Vb = ?

Solving the problem

Now, let us solve for the velocity of the boat using the given formula for relative velocity

V₂ = Vb - V₁

Then substitute the given data

5 km/h = Vb - 3 km/h

Transpose 3 km/h to the left side of the equation to solve for Vb, therefore

Vb = 5 km/h + 3 km/h

Vb = 8 km/h

Therefore, the velocity vector of the boat is 8 km/h
