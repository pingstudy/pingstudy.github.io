24. LESSON 2 TREADING THE ROAD TO MASTERY Directions: list down the step-by-step process when troubleshooting the following: PRINTER WI-FI SIGNAL MONITOR


Answer:

TROUBLESHOOTING PRINTER:

1.Check if the printer is turned on and properly connected to the power source.

2.Ensure that the printer is properly connected to the computer and that the cable is working.

3.Check if there are any error messages displayed on the printer's LCD or LED screen.

4.Verify that the printer driver is properly installed on the computer.

5.Check if there is enough ink or toner in the printer and that the cartridges are correctly installed.

6.Run a printer diagnostic test or reset the printer to its default settings.

7.If none of the above works, contact the manufacturer or a technician for further assistance.

TROUBLESHOOTING WI-FI SIGNAL:

1.Make sure that the device is in range of the Wi-Fi signal.

2.Check if the Wi-Fi signal is turned on and that the device is connected to the right network.

3.Verify that the router is working properly and that the signal strength is strong enough.

4.Restart the device and router, and try connecting again.

5.Ensure that the device's Wi-Fi settings are correctly configured and that the password is entered correctly.

6.If there are still issues, consider resetting the router or contacting the service provider for assistance.

TROUBLESHOOTING MONITOR:

1.Check if the monitor is properly connected to the computer or other device and that the cable is working.

2.Verify that the monitor is turned on and that the power supply is working.

3.Check if the monitor settings are configured correctly, such as resolution and refresh rate.

4.Inspect the monitor for any physical damage or dead pixels.

5.Try connecting the monitor to another device or using a different cable.

6.If none of the above works, contact the manufacturer or a technician for further assistance.
