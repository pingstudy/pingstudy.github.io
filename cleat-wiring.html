21. ScienceExplain the presence of gravity and friction in the following:Athletes used shoes with cleats​


Answer:

The presence of gravity and friction can be explained in the context of athletes using shoes with cleats as follows:

Gravity is a natural force that pulls objects towards each other, such as the Earth and the athletes wearing the shoes with cleats. As the athletes run, jump, or change direction, their weight and movement create a force that acts against gravity. This force can cause slipping or sliding of the athletes' shoes, making it difficult for them to maintain balance and control.

Friction is the force that resists motion between two surfaces in contact. When athletes wear shoes with cleats, the cleats provide more surface area and protrusions that can create greater friction between the shoes and the ground. This increased friction helps the athletes maintain their footing, improve their traction and grip, and change direction more easily. Without the presence of friction, athletes wearing smooth-soled shoes could slip or slide on the playing surface, making it difficult for them to perform at their best.

Therefore, the use of shoes with cleats by athletes is an example of how gravity and friction work together to help athletes perform their best by keeping them grounded and providing traction for better performance.
