9. Calculate kinetic energy of a shotgun bullet that has a mass of 25 grams with a velocity of 3600km /hour


To calculate the kinetic energy of a bullet, we can use the formula:

KE = 1/2 * m * v^2

where:

KE = kinetic energy (in joules)

m = mass of the bullet (in kilograms)

v = velocity of the bullet (in meters per second)

First, let's convert the mass of the bullet from grams to kilograms:

m = 25 grams / 1000 = 0.025 kg

Next, let's convert the velocity of the bullet from kilometers per hour to meters per second:

v = 3600 km/hour * (1000 meters/1 kilometer) * (1 hour/3600 seconds) = 1000 m/s

Now we can plug in the values into the formula:

KE = 1/2 * 0.025 kg * (1000 m/s)^2

KE = 1/2 * 0.025 kg * 1,000,000 m^2/s^2

KE = 1/2 * 25,000 J

KE = 12,500 J

Therefore, the kinetic energy of the shotgun bullet is 12,500 joules.

12. 3. In medias res. This word means "into the middle of things.” Another related story is narrated inside the main plot line. It is narrating from the opening with a dramatic action rather than the exposition. For example, in the animated film Ratatouille, the main character starts looking at a house from afar with the muffled sounds of what turns out to be shotgun blasts. Then, with Remy jumping through a plate glass window while carrying a book larger than himself, he quickly jumps back to explain how the incident happened.​


Answer:

A narrative work beginning in medias res (Classical Latin: [ɪn ˈmɛdɪ.aːs ˈreːs], lit. "into the middle of things") opens in the midst of the plot (cf. ab ovo, ab initio).[1] Often, exposition is bypassed and filled in gradually, through dialogue, flashbacks or description of past events. For example, Hamlet begins after the death of Hamlet's father. Characters make reference to King Hamlet's death without the plot's first establishment of said fact. Since the play is about Hamlet and the revenge more so than the motivation, Shakespeare uses in medias res to bypass superfluous exposition.

Works that employ in medias res often later use flashback and nonlinear narrative for exposition to fill in the backstory. In Homer's Odyssey, the reader first learns about Odysseus's journey when he is held captive on Calypso's island. The reader then finds out, in Books IX through XII, that the greater part of Odysseus's journey precedes that moment in the narrative. In Homer's Iliad there are fewer flashbacks, although it opens in the thick of the Trojan War.

Answer:

1. Scientific Management: The theory focuses on maximizing efficiency through the optimization of task processes and the implementation of specialized training for workers.

2. General Administrative Theory: This theory emphasizes the importance of understanding the structure, functions and relationships of an organization and its environment in order to manage it effectively.

3. TOM philosophy: This approach assumes that organizations are composed of interconnected and interdependent parts that must be managed holistically in order to achieve success.
