11. A pure inductance of l= 0.20 h passes a current i = 28cos (800t) a. what is the voltage across the element?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & L = \text{0.20 H} \\ & i = 28\text{cos} \: (800t) \: \text{A} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Express the given current as a function of sine.

We add π/2 to the angle of cosine function to become sine function.

[tex]i = 28\text{sin} \: \left(800t + \dfrac{\pi}{2}\right) \: \text{A}[/tex]

Step 3: Determine the voltage across the element by using the formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} v & = L\frac{di}{dt} \\ & = (\text{0.20 H})\left(\frac{d\left[28\text{sin} \: \left(800t + \dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)\right]}{dt}\right) \: \frac{\text{A}}{\text{s}} \\ & = (\text{0.20 H})\left[28(800)\text{cos} \: \left(800t + \dfrac{\pi}{2}\right)\right] \\ & = \boxed{4480\text{cos} \: \left(800t + \dfrac{\pi}{2}\right) \: \text{V}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the voltage across the element is 4480cos(800 + π/2) V.

BALLAST

» In a fluorescent lighting system, the ballast regulates the current to the lamps and provides sufficient voltage to start the lamps.

» Without a ballast to limit its current, a fluorescent lamp connected directly to a high voltage power source would rapidly and uncontrollably increase its current draw.

» To achieve full rated light output and rated lamp life from a fluorescent lighting system, a ballast’s output characteristics must precisely match the electrical requirements of the lamps it operates.

» Traditionally, ballasts are designed to operate a specific number (usually one to four) and type of lamp (such as a four-foot T8 lamp) at a specific voltage (in North America either 120, 277, or 347 volts).

23. Transformers are used to raise the voltage along substations and lower it for residential consumption. What kind of transformers is used to raise the voltage?a. Step-upb. Step-downc. Neither of the two.d. Both.


Answer:

The kind of transformer used to raise up voltage is a step-up transformer.

Explanation:

A transformer is used for voltage conversions. Like in the case of other countries, there are low voltages in generating equipment, household and distribution to industrial power. Other countries uses different standard of generating power and to use their equipment, we need a transformer to convert the necessary voltage to operate it. The same way in voltage distribution to households. The voltage from the main source is high and need a transformer to distribute them to residential.

A transformer consists of two coils: the primary and the secondary coil. The first winding or coil is where the power is supplied while the secondary winding is where the power is delivered. In a step-up transformer, the primary winding supply a low input voltage that will yield to high delivery of voltage to the secondary winding. This happens when an alternating current sets up an alternating flux in the soft core and induces and electromagnetive force or emf in each winding. There, the energy is transferred from the current in one winding to the other through the core flux and its induced emfs.

27. where the power transformer lowers the voltage​


Answer:

All transformers have both a primary and a secondary coil. A transformer whose primary coil has more turns than its secondary coil is called a step-down transformer because it reduces voltage — that is, the voltage at the secondary coil is less than the voltage at the primary coil.
