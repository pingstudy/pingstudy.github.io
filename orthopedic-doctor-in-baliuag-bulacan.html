9. On a hospital staff, there are 4 dermatologists, 7 surgeons, 5 general practitioners, 3 psychiatrists, and 3 orthopedic specialists. If a doctor is selected at random, find the probability that the doctor isa. A psychiatrist, surgeon, or dermatologistb. A general practitioner or surgeonc. An orthopedic specialist, a surgeon, or a dermatologistd. A surgeon or dermatologist​


Answer:

a. The probability that the doctor is a psychiatrist, surgeon, or dermatologist selected at random is [tex]\frac{7}{11}[/tex].

b. The probability that the doctor is a general practitioner or surgeon selected at random is [tex]\frac{6}{11}[/tex].

c. The probability that the doctor is an orthopedic specialist, a surgeon, or a dermatologist selected at random is [tex]\frac{7}{11}[/tex].

d. The probability that the doctor is a surgeon or dermatologist​ selected at random is [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex].

Step-by-step explanation:

The meaning of probability is technically the percentage of an outcome to likely happen. It is the studying the chance of an event to occur.

The "Or" and "And" ProblemIn probability, when we say "or", it means to add the respective probabilitiesWhen we say "and", it means to multiply the respective probabilities

Note that the probability of an event to happen is given by the formula,

[tex]P(E)=\frac{\text{number of considered possible outcome}}{\text{total number of possible outcome}}\\[/tex].

Solutions

a. Find the probability that the doctor is a psychiatrist, surgeon, or dermatologist.

         The total number of doctors in the hospital is 22.

         The probability of selecting a psychiatrist is [tex]\frac{3}{22}[/tex].

         The probability of selecting a surgeon is [tex]\frac{7}{22}[/tex].

         The probability of selecting a dermatologist is [tex]\frac{4}{22}[/tex].

         So, the probability of selecting a psychiatrist, surgeon, or dermatologist at random is the sum of the individual probabilities.

                [tex]\begin{aligned}P(\text{Psychiatrist})+P(\text{Surgeon})+P(\text{Dermatologist})&=\frac{3}{22}+\frac{7}{22}+\frac{4}{22}\\&=\frac{14}{22}\\&=\frac{7}{11}\\&\text{or}\\&\approx{64\%}\end{aligned}[/tex]

b. Find the probability that the doctor is a general practitioner or surgeon.

         The probability of selecting a general practitioner is [tex]\frac{5}{22}[/tex].

         The probability of selecting a surgeon is [tex]\frac{7}{22}[/tex].

         So, the probability of selecting a general practitioner or a surgeon at random is given by,  

                [tex]\begin{aligned}P(\text{General Practitioner})+P(\text{Surgeon})&=\frac{5}{22}+\frac{7}{22}\\&=\frac{12}{22}\\&=\frac{6}{11}\\&\text{or}\\&\approx{55\%}\end{aligned}[/tex]

c. Find the probability that the doctor is an orthopedic specialist, surgeon, or dermatologist.

         The probability of selecting an orthopedic specialist is [tex]\frac{3}{22}[/tex].

         The probability of selecting a surgeon is [tex]\frac{7}{22}[/tex].

         The probability of selecting a dermatologist is [tex]\frac{4}{22}[/tex].

         So, the probability of selecting an orthopedic specialist, surgeon, or dermatologist at random is given by,  

       [tex]\begin{aligned}P(\text{Orthopedic Specialist})+P(\text{Surgeon})+P(\text{Dermatologist})&=\frac{3}{22}+\frac{7}{22}+\frac{4}{22}\\&=\frac{14}{22}\\&=\frac{7}{11}\\&\text{or}\\&\approx{}64\%\end{aligned}\\[/tex]

d. Find the probability that the doctor is a surgeon or dermatologist.

         The probability  of selecting a surgeon is [tex]\frac{7}{22}[/tex].

         The probability of selecting a dermatologist is [tex]\frac{4}{22}[/tex].

         So, the probability of selecting a surgeon, or dermatologist at random is given by,  

           [tex]\begin{aligned}P(\text{Surgeon})+P(\text{Dermatologist})&=\frac{7}{22}+\frac{4}{22}\\&=\frac{11}{22}\\&=\frac{1}{2}\\&\text{or}\\&=50\%\end{aligned}[/tex]
