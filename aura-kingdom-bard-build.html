16. why did the old kingdom succeed in building a glorious glory? ​


Why did the old kingdom succeed in building a glorious glory?

The Old Kingdom of Egypt was a successful civilization that built a glorious legacy due to various factors. One of the key reasons for their success was the establishment of a strong centralized government led by pharaohs who maintained a stable administration through a powerful bureaucracy and military. Additionally, the ancient Egyptians developed advanced agricultural techniques, such as irrigation, which allowed them to cultivate crops in the fertile Nile River Valley. The construction of impressive architectural structures, such as the pyramids, also served as symbols of the pharaoh's power and authority, showcasing the wealth and resources of the kingdom. The Old Kingdom had a thriving economy based on trade, agriculture, and mining, which contributed to their success. Lastly, the Old Kingdom's strong belief in an afterlife and the use of funerary rituals and monuments contributed to the construction of impressive tombs and temples.

Answer:
The Cannon-Bard theory states that the lower part of the brain, also called the thalamus, controls your experience of emotion. At the same time, the higher part of the brain, also called the cortex, controls the expression of emotion. It is believed that these two parts of the brain react simultaneously.
