28. The molar mass of gas A is four times that of gas B. Gas A is monatomic, while gas B is diatomic. What is the ratio of the speed of sound in gas A to the speed of sound in gas B at the same temperature?​


Answer:

The speed of sound in a gas is proportional to the square root of the gas's molar mass. Since the molar mass of gas A is four times that of gas B, the speed of sound in gas A will be twice that of gas B. The ratio of the speed of sound in gas A to the speed of sound in gas B will therefore be 2:1.
