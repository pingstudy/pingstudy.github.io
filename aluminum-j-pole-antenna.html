Q = mc∆T  

Q = heat, J  

m = mass, g  

c = specific heat, joules / g ° C  

∆T = temperature difference, ° C / K  

The specific heat for aluminum is 0.900 J/g·°c

mass = 3.8 g

∆T = 25 - 450 =-425 ° C  

the heat released (negative sign=exothermic):

[tex]\tt Q=m.c.\Delta T\\\\Q=3.8\times 0.9\times -425\\\\Q=-1.453\times 10^3~J\\\\Q\approx -1.5~kJ[/tex]

17. How many joules of heat are needed to raise the temperature of 40.0 g of aluminum from 67°C to 89°C, if the specific heat of aluminum is 0.89 J/g°C?


Answer:

783.2 Joules

Step-by-step explanation:

SPECIFIC HEAT

Specific heat is the amount of heat required by a substance to change its unit mass by one degree Celcius. Specific heat is measured in J/kg C or J/g C or J/kg K and represented by Cp. The specific heat can be calculated by the formula of heat:

Q = mCpΔT

where Q is the heat generated, m is the mass of the object, Cp is the specific heat and ΔT is the change in temperature.

In this problem, we are given 40.0 grams of aluminum, specific heat of 0.89 J/g °C, and the change in temperature from 67 °C to 89  °C. We need to calculate the heat.

Substitute the given values to the formula.

Q = mCp( [tex]T_{final}[/tex] - [tex]T_{initial}[/tex])

Q = 40.0 grams ( 0.89 J/g °C) ( 89  °C - 67 °C )

Q = 783.2 Joules

30. The specific heat of aluminum is 600 J/kg.K. How much heat is needed to change the temperature of a 2.0 kg object made of aluminum from 25°C to 35°C?- need Solution​


Answer:

The heat needed to change the temperature of an object can be calculated using the formula Q = m * c * ΔT, where Q is the heat, m is the mass of the object, c is the specific heat of the material, and ΔT is the change in temperature. In this case, the heat needed to change the temperature of the aluminum object from 25°C to 35°C is 2.0 kg * 600 J/kg.K * (35°C - 25°C) = 12,000 J. This means that 12,000 joules of heat are needed to change the temperature of the object from 25°C to 35°C.
