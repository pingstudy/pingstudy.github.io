26. what makes manual materials handling hazardous


Manual materials handling can be hazardous due to several factors, including:

Heavy or awkward loads: Lifting, pushing, or pulling heavy or awkward loads can put a strain on the body and lead to musculoskeletal injuries.
Repetitive motions: Repeating the same motions over and over again can cause repetitive stress injuries like tendinitis and carpal tunnel syndrome.
Awkward postures: Bending, twisting, and reaching can put stress on the back, shoulders, and neck, and increase the risk of injury.
Poor ergonomics: Improper lifting techniques or using poorly designed tools and equipment can make manual materials handling tasks more difficult and increase the risk of injury.
Lack of training: Workers who are not trained in proper lifting techniques and safe handling practices may be more likely to suffer injuries.
Environmental factors: Slippery or uneven floors, poor lighting, and other environmental factors can increase the risk of falls and other accidents when handling materials manually.
It is important to identify and address these hazards in order to prevent injuries and promote safe manual materials handling practices in the workplace.
