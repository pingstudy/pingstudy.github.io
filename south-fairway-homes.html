2. A sprinkler on a golf course fairway is set to spray water over a distance of 70 feet and rotates through an angle of 120°. Find the area of the fairway watered by the sprinkler. A. 5131ft2 B. 4200ft2 C. 73.304ft2 D. 294000ft2


Answer:

A. 5131ft^2

Step-by-step explanation:

Area of a sector = 1/2(r^2)θ

Given: r= 70ft, θ=120°

Required: A=?

Equation: A= 1/2(r^2)θ

Solution:

*θ must be in radians

120 ° *( π/180 °) =  2 π/3

A= 1/2(70ft^2)(2π/3)

A= 4900 π/3 ≈ 5131ft2


3. 16. A sprinkler on a golf course fairway sprays water over a distance of 50 feet and rotates through an angle of 90°. What is the area of the fairway watered by the sprinkler? a. 1963 sq. ft. b. 1936 sq. ft. c.1933 sq. ft. d.1639 sq. ft. explain how to get answer pls.


The area of the fairway watered by the sprinkler can be found by using the formula for the area of a sector of a circle: (angle/360) * π * r^2, where angle is measured in degrees, and r is the radius of the circle.

In this case, the radius of the circle is the distance that the sprinkler sprays water, which is 50 feet. The angle is 90°.

So, the formula to find the area of the fairway watered by the sprinkler is (90/360) * π * 50^2 = (1/4) * π * 2500 = 625π sq. ft.

625π is approximately 1963 sq.ft, hence the answer is A. 1963 sq. ft.





4. 4. A golfer hits his tee shot along a flat fairway at 30° to the horizontal with an initialspeed of 60 m/s. What is the range of the ball? What is the maximum height towhich the ball rises?​


Solution: (Range)

vi = 60 m/s

θ = 30°

R = vi² sin 2θ / g

R = (60 m/s)²[sin (2 × 30°)] / 9.81 m/s²

R = 317.81 m

Note: If the value of g used is 9.8 m/s², the answer is 318.13 m.

--------------------------------------------------------

Solution: (Maximum Height)

y max = (vi sin θ)² / (2g)

y max = (60 m/s sin 30°)² / (2 × 9.81 m/s²)

y max = 45.87 m

Note: If the value of g used is 9.8 m/s², the answer is 45.92 m.

9. A golf ball accelerates off a tee at 15 m/s2, changing its velocity from 0 m/s down the fairway. How long did it take the golf ball to accelerate?


Answer:

3.3 s

Explanation:

v = u+ at

Acceleration a = 15 m/s²

initial velocity u = 0 m/s

Final velocity v = 50 m/s

50 = 0 + 15 × t

50 = 0 + 15t

15t = 50

t = 50/15

t = 3.3 s

14. A golfer hits his tee shot along a flat fairway at 40 degrees to the horizontal with an initial speed of 50m/s. What is the range of the ball? What is the maximum height to which the ball rises?


Answer:

a. The range of the ball is 251.23 meters

b. The maximum height of the ball is 52.7 meters

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the concept of projectile motion to solve the following requirements.

Formula to use

R = V₀² sin(2θ)/g

H = V₀² sin²θ/2g

where

R     is the range, the unit is in meter

V₀   is the initial velocity, the unit is m/s

θ     is the angle at the horizontal

g     is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/s²

Given information

V₀ = 50 m/s

θ = 40°

a. R = ?

b. H = ?

Solving the problem

a. Let us solve the range of the ball using the first given formula

R = V₀² sin(2θ)/g

Then, substitute the given data

R = (50 m/s)² sin[2(40°)] / 9.8 m/s²

R = 50² sin 80° / 9.8

R = 251.23 meters

b. Now, let us solve for the maximum height of the ball using the second given formula

H = V₀² sin²θ/2g

Then, substitute the given data

H = (50)² (sin 40°)² / 2(9.8)

H = 52.7 meters

Therefore, the ball rises to a maximum height of 52.7 meters and a maximum horizontal distance or range of 251.23 meters.
