You can read this about pitch name:

Each pitch is referred to as one of the first seven characters in the Latin / Roman / English / etc. alphabet - namely A, B, C, D, E, F, and G - in one of the most prevalent pitch-naming schemes. The frequency of the pitch "A" is the lowest, while the frequency of the pitch "G" is the greatest. These letters are assigned to the white keys on a piano keyboard, as shown below. Because there are 52 white keys on a conventional piano, the drawing below only shows a section of the keyboard.

Flats and Sharps

There is a black key in the middle of some of the white keys, but not all of them. The frequency of the black keys is slightly higher than that of the white key on their immediate left, and slightly lower than that of the white key on their immediate right. In fact, the black keys are referred to as "black keys." The term "sharp" in music refers to a change in frequency, while "flat" refers to a change in frequency. A-sharp or B-flat are the two names for the black key that sits between the white keys A and B. Sharp and flat are represented by a single character in music, and they are represented by the letters and, respectively.
