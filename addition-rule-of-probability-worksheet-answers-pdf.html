20. divisibility rules of 4,8,11,12 and worksheets


Answer:

Divisibility by 4 :

a number is said to be divisible by 4 if the number formed by the last two digits is a multiple of divisible by 4.divide the last 2 digits by 4.

Examples :

516 → 16 ÷ 4 = 4 624 → 24 ÷ 4 = 6

Checking :

516 ÷ 4 = 129 624 ÷ 4 = 156

Divisibility by 8 :

a number is said to be divisible by 8 if its last three digits are zeros or if the number formed by the last three-digits is divisible by 8.

Examples :

95 000 ÷ 8 = 11 8755 288 → 288 ÷ 8 = 36

Checking :

5 288 ÷ 8 = 661

Divisibility by 11 :

a number is said to be divisible by 11 when the difference between the sum of the odd positioned digits and the sum of the even positioned digits is zero (0) or eleven (11).

Example :

5 775The sum of odd positioned digit :

5 + 7 = 12

The sum of even positioned digit:

5 + 7 = 12

Their difference :

12 - 12 = 0

Checking :

5 775 ÷ 11 = 525

Divisibility by 12 :

a number is said to be divisible by 12 if the number is divisible by both 3 and 4.

Divisibility by 3 :

a number is said to be divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is a multiple or divisible by 3.

Example :

672 → 6 + 7 + 2 = 15 is divisible by 3672 → 72 ÷ 4 = 18

Which means 672 is divisible by 12

Checking :

672 ÷ 12 = 56
