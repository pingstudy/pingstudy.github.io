7. A silver coin is 28 years older than a bronze coin. In 6 years, the silver coin will be twice as old as the bronze coin. Find the present age of each coin.​


Let X be the present age of the bronze coin.

The present age of the silver coin is X+28.

In 6 years, the bronze coin will be X+6 years old.

The silver coin will be (X+28)+6=X+34 years old.

We are told that X+34=2(X+6).

It simplifies to X+34=2X+12.

Solving for X, we find that X=22.

The present age of the bronze coin is 22 years.

The present age of the silver coin is 22+28=50 years.

Thus, the present age of the bronze coin is 22 years, and the current generation of the silver coin is 50 years.

The silver coin is currently 34 years old, and the bronze coin is currently 6 years old.

Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

Analyzing word problem

The given are a total of 50 coins consisting of 20-peso and 5-peso coins and a total amount of 790.00. We need to know the quantity of each type of coin.

Step 1: Determine the working equation of the problem. We are given two mathematical statements to translate into working equations. Let x be the 20-peso coin and let y be the 5-peso coin.

Statement 1: 50 coins consisting of 20-peso and 5-peso coins

Equation 1: x + y = 50

Statement 2: Total amount of 790.00

Equation 2: 20x + 5y = 790.00

Step 2: Rearrange equation one in terms of x and substitute in equation two. Simplify the resulting equation and solve for y.

x + y = 50

x = 50 - y : Equation 3

20x + 5y = 790.00

20 ( 50 - y) + 5y = 790.00

1000 - 20y + 5y = 790.00

1000 - 790.00 = 20y -5y

210 = 15y

210/15 = 15y/15

y = 14

Step 3: Substitute the resulting value of y to equation 3.

x = 50 - y

x = 50-14

x = 36

Therefore, there are 36 pieces of 20-peso coin and 14 pieces of 5-peso coin.

29. A 1pesos-coin into 16 coins consisting of just 5g-coins and 10g-coins how many coins of each kind where in the change​


Answer:

Let's assume that there are x 5g-coins and y 10g-coins in the change.

The total number of coins is given as 16, so we can write:

x + y = 16

The total weight of the coins is 1 peso, which is equal to 100g. We know that each 5g-coin and 10g-coin weighs 5g and 10g, respectively. Therefore, we can write:

5x + 10y = 100

Now we can solve for x and y by using the two equations:

x + y = 16

5x + 10y = 100

Multiplying the first equation by 5, we get:

5x + 5y = 80

Subtracting this equation from the second equation, we get:

5y = 20

y = 4

Substituting this value of y in the first equation, we get:

x + 4 = 16

x = 12

Therefore, there are 12 5g-coins and 4 10g-coins in the change
