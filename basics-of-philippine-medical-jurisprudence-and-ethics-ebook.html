24. Elements to prove negligence medical ethics


Medical negligence is a condition in which a doctor or medical personnel deviates from the medical code of ethics, doctor professional standards and standard operating procedures (SOP) when performing medical actions against their patients, resulting in losses suffered by patients as a result of medical actions. The elements of proof of medical ethics negligence are:

1. Medical action consent/informed consent

The consent given by the patient or his family on the basis of a detailed explanation complete information about the medical/medical actions that will be carried out to the patient. Therapeutic Transactions are agreements between doctors and patients, in the form of legal relationship that gives birth to rights and obligations for both parties party. The object of the agreement is an effort or therapy to patient healing.

2. Doctor's certificate

A doctor's certificate has the binding force and can be used as evidence in the form of writing, the contents of the doctor's certificate are the truth that must be accounted for by doctors to whoever, wherever, and whenever, doctor's certificate only contains the truth and not others are just the truth. If the contents of the doctor's certificate do not match the patient's condition, the doctor has committed an unlawful act.
