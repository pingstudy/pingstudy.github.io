5. What is TAC TTT AAA CGC ACT of mRNA (codom)​


Assuming that the given sequence "TAC TTT AAA CGC ACT" represents the template DNA strand, the corresponding mRNA sequence (codons) can be obtained by replacing T with A, A with U, C with G, and G with C.

So, the mRNA sequence would be AUG AAA UUU GCG UGA.

Note that AUG is the start codon and UGA is a stop codon, while the remaining codons code for specific amino acids in the protein synthesis process.

Complementary base pairing of DNA - RNA

                       DNA                     RNA

                    A  adenine      -     U  uracil

                    G  guanine      -     C  cytosine

                   C   cytosine      -    G guanine

                    T   thymine      -    A  adenine

Uracil can be found in RNA but not in DNA and  Thymine is present in DNA but not in RNA.

DNA   -     G G T  G A T  C G C   A T A

                 ↓  ↓ ↓   ↓ ↓ ↓   ↓ ↓ ↓    ↓ ↓ ↓

mRNA  -    C C A  C U A  G C G  U A U
