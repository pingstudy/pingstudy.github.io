Answer:

3. Compare and contrast front bodice pattern with back bodice pattern.​


Answer:

Virtually all pattern drafting systems assume a B cup size which results in a front pattern that is wider than the back by 1/2" on each side. As you increase the bust cup size, the front pattern width increases accordingly so for a D cup you may find that your front pattern is wider by 1" on each side.

14. procedure in drafting the front bodice pattern​


Answer:

Front Bodice

1. 2-8F = ½ back neck or 6.5 to 7.5 cm.

2. 2-9F = ½ shoulder. Square down 9F.

3. 9F – 10F = 4.5 cm. connect 8F to 10F.

4. 4-11F = ½ shoulder minus ¾ inch (2 cm).

5. 8 – 12F = ¼ bust + 1 inch (2.5 cm). Connect 10F,

6. 1F, and 12F with a French curve.

7. 5-13F = ½ bust point width.

8. 7-14F = ½ bust point width. ...

9. 7-15F = ¼ waist + 4cm.
