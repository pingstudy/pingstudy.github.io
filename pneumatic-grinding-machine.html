2. How do pneumatics machine work?​


Answer:

A pneumatics system works by increasing and decreasing air flows using valves with ports. Ports allow for air to simultaneously flow in different directions. When the machine turns on, these valves open; the compressed air turns cylinders and tubes within the machine, which in turn power up the engine.

10. *Define what is Hand Tools and its classifications. *Define what is Machine/Power Tools*Define what is Pneumatic Tools​


Answer:

Hand Tools are tools that are powered by hand rather than by electricity or other external sources of power. These tools are generally simple and easy to use and are used for a wide range of applications, including woodworking, metalworking, and automotive repair. Some common examples of hand tools include hammers, pliers, screwdrivers, wrenches, and hand saws.

Hand tools can be classified into several categories based on their intended use, such as cutting tools, gripping tools, fastening tools, measuring tools, and striking tools.

Machine/Power Tools are tools that are powered by electricity, gasoline, or compressed air, among other sources of power. These tools are generally more complex and sophisticated than hand tools, and they are used for a wide range of applications, including manufacturing, construction, and woodworking. Some common examples of power tools include drills, saws, grinders, and sanders.

Power tools can be classified into several categories based on their intended use, such as drilling machines, cutting machines, grinding machines, and sanding machines.

Pneumatic Tools are tools that are powered by compressed air, and they are commonly used in manufacturing and construction applications. Pneumatic tools are typically more powerful and efficient than hand tools, and they are able to perform tasks that would be difficult or impossible for human workers to accomplish manually. Some common examples of pneumatic tools include pneumatic hammers, pneumatic drills, and pneumatic screwdrivers. Pneumatic tools can be classified into several categories based on their intended use, such as impact wrenches, pneumatic sanders, and air ratchets.

20. 2. A man is engaged in buy and sell. He sold agrinding machine for 88,500. If his profit is15% of the purchased cost, find thepurchased cost of the machine.​


Answer:

The purchased cost of the machine is 76,956.

Step-by-step explanation:

Purchased cost of Machine: x

Profit: 15%(x) or 0.15x

Selling price:  88,500

Find the purchased cost, x:

x + 0.15x = 88,500

1.15x = 88,500

1.15x/1.15 = 88,500/1.15

x ≈ 76,956

The purchased cost of the machine is 76,956.

28. waht is pneumatic symbols​


Answer:

Directional air control valves are the building blocks of pneumatic control. Pneumatic circuit symbols representing these valves provide detailed information about the valve they represent. Symbols show the methods of actuation, the number of positions, the flow paths and the number of ports.


29. What is pneumatics?​


Answer:

Pneumatics is a branch of engineering that makes use of gas or pressurized air. Pneumatic systems used in industry are commonly powered by compressed air or compressed inert gases. A centrally located and electrically powered compressor powers cylinders, air motors, and other pneumatic devices.
