16. explain why friction is advantageous while driving on a wet ,slippery road​


Answer:

As a general rule, friction is more effective on rough surfaces. These rougher surfaces provide more grip for your vehicle, resulting in a more secure ride. If there is water on the road, this creates a layer between the road surface and the tyre of the car itself.


17. 1. Why is walking and driving a car friction?2. Why is there friction?3. 2 examples of undesirable effects of friction​


Answer:

1.) Friction is a force that arises when things rub together. The frictional force between the road and tire is what allows the tire to "push" off the road, thus moving the car forward (Newton's third law — the action is the pushing frictional force, the reaction is the forward movement of the car).

2.) Friction occurs because no surface is perfectly smooth. Rougher surfaces have more friction between them. Heavier objects also have more friction because they press together with greater force. Friction produces heat because it causes the molecules on rubbing surfaces to move faster and have more energy.

3.)Friction is Undesirable: A friction is undesirable during ice skating or when a block is lifted or put down on the truck with the help of some inclined plane. If the friction is more between the block and inclined surface then a large force is required to push the block on the plane.
