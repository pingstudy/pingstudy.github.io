4. magsaliksik ukol sa hare hawes -cutting law​


Answer:

Ang Hare-Hawes-Cutting Act ay ipinasa sa mga may-akda ng Kongreso Butler B. Hare, Senador Harry B. Hawes at Senador Bronson M. Cutting. . Ito ay bunga ng OsRox Mission na pinangunahan nina Sergio Osmeña at Manuel Roxas. Ipinangako ng batas ang kalayaan ng Pilipinas makalipas ang 10 taon, ngunit nakalaan ang ilang base ng militar at naval para sa Estados Unidos, pati na rin ang ipinataw na mga taripa at quota sa mga import ng Pilipinas.

Pagsapit ng 1932, dalawang pangunahing grupo ang sumuporta sa isang batas na naglalarawan ng mga detalye ng kalayaan ng Pilipinas: Mahusay na Kaligtasan ng panahon-Amerikano na mga magsasaka na nakikipagkumpitensya laban sa walang asukal na asukal at langis ng niyog; at mga Pilipino na naghahanap ng kalayaan ng Pilipinas. Ang Hare-Hawes-Cutting Act ay isinulat ng South Carolina Representative Butler Hare, Missouri Senator Harry Bartow Hawes at New Mexico na Senador Bronson M. Cutting. Ito ay ipinasa ng Kongreso ng Estados Unidos noong Disyembre 1932, ngunit hinango ni Pangulong Herbert Hoover ng Estados Unidos. Kinontrata ng kongreso ang veto noong Enero 17, 1933. Kinakailangan ang Senado ng Pilipinas na kumpirmahin ang batas. Sa mga pinuno tulad ni Manuel L. Quezon na tumutol dito, tinanggihan ng Senado ng Pilipinas ang panukalang batas.

12. When a substance melts, the kinetic energyA. DecreaseB. Stay the sameC. IncreasesD. Decreases then increases​.


Answer:

Substances can change phase — often because of a temperature change. At low temperatures, most substances are solid; as the temperature increases, they become liquid; at higher temperatures still, they become gaseous.

The process of a solid becoming a liquid is called melting (an older term that you may see sometimes is fusion). The opposite process, a liquid becoming a solid, is called solidification. For any pure substance, the temperature at which melting occurs — known as the melting point — is a characteristic of that substance. It requires energy for a solid to melt into a liquid. Every pure substance has a certain amount of energy it needs to change from a solid to a liquid. This amount is called the enthalpy of fusion (or heat of fusion) of the substance, represented as ΔHfus. Some ΔHfus values are listed in Table 10.2 “Enthalpies of Fusion for Various Substances”; it is assumed that these values are for the melting point of the substance. Note that the unit of ΔHfus is kilojoules per mole, so we need to know the quantity of material to know how much energy is involved. The ΔHfus is always tabulated as a positive number. However, it can be used for both the melting and the solidification processes as long as you keep in mind that melting is always endothermic (so ΔH will be positive), while solidification is always exothermic (so ΔH will be negative).

Table 10.2 Enthalpies of Fusion for Various Substances

Substance (Melting Point) ΔHfus (kJ/mol)

Water (0°C) 6.01

Aluminum (660°C) 10.7

Benzene (5.5°C) 9.95

Ethanol (−114.3°C) 5.02

Mercury (−38.8°C) 2.29
