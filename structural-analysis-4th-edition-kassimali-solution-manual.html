4. Thematic analysis- Generate themes based on data gathered?use manual coding to analyze a data?described data analysis procedure?pahelp po ty:)​


1.     Thematic analysis is a common qualitative research method used to analyze data and identify patterns or themes that emerge from the data. To generate themes based on the data gathered, you can follow the steps below:

Familiarize yourself with the data: Read and re-read the data to get a sense of the content and identify any patterns or trends that may be present.

Generate initial codes: Start by coding the data into smaller units of meaning, such as phrases or sentences, that capture the essence of what is being said. These codes can be descriptive or interpretive.

Identify potential themes: Look for patterns or clusters of codes that suggest common themes or topics. These themes should be relevant to the research question and capture important aspects of the data.

Review and refine themes: Go back through the data to ensure that each code is assigned to the appropriate theme. Refine the themes as needed, merging or splitting them based on the data.

Define and name the themes: Write a brief description of each theme and give it a clear and concise name that captures its essence.

Write up the analysis: Write a summary of the themes and the data that support them, and provide quotes or examples from the data to illustrate each theme.

2.     Regarding the use of manual coding, it is a common approach in thematic analysis and involves reading and re-reading the data to identify patterns and themes. It involves the researcher using their own judgement to identify and interpret patterns, which can be a more flexible and nuanced approach than using software.

3.     In terms of the data analysis procedure, the steps outlined above would generally be followed, but there may be some variation depending on the specific research question and data being analyzed. It is important to ensure that the analysis is systematic, transparent and replicable, and that the themes identified are grounded in the data.

23. arranging the components of the bibliographic entry using the Chicago Manual Style 17th edition​


Answer:

The Chicago Notes and Bibliography (NB) system is often used in the humanities to provide writers with a system for referencing their sources through the use of footnotes, endnotes, and through the use of a bibliography. This offers writers a flexible option for citation and provides an outlet for commenting on those sources, if needed. Proper use of the Notes and Bibliography system builds a writer’s credibility by demonstrating their accountability to source material. In addition, it can protect writers from accusations of plagiarism, which is the intentional or accidental uncredited use of source material created by others.
