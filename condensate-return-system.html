22. process by which a high percentage of precipitation is returned from the atmospherea. condensationb. rainfallc. runoffd. sublimation​


(A) Condensation is a process that returns a significant amount of precipitation to the atmosphere.

Condensation is the transformation of a substance from a less dense phase, like a gas (or vapor), to a denser phase, like a liquid. Commonly, condensation happens when a vapor is cooled to become a liquid.

The heat is carried by the water molecule. The atmosphere's temperature also slightly increases. The molecule typically has low kinetic energy in order to condense. The loss of a slow-moving particle has raised the average speed of the molecules in the atmosphere. Its temperature has therefore increased as well.

Clouds are created in the atmosphere when water vapor condenses. The temperature that an air parcel must reach before starting to condense is known as its dew point. Almost any surface, such as the inside or outside of your windows, drink cans, or shower screen, is susceptible to condensation.

These consist of:The indoor air's humidity - Condensation occurs when the inside air cannot hold the amount of moisture, as we briefly discussed earlier.Low temperature - The damp air interacts with the chilly interior surfaces.Insufficient ventilation - When there is nowhere for hot and cold air to go, humidity builds up within, increasing condensation.
