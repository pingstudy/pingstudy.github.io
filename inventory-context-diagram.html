1. To operationalize logistical integration within a supply chain context, the following are the operational objectives: EXCEPT ONE- a. responsiveness b. distribution c. variance reduction d. inventory reduction


Answer:

b. distribution

Explanation:

The objective of "distribution" is not typically considered an operational objective when operationalizing logistical integration within a supply chain context. Distribution typically refers to the process of physically delivering goods from one location to another, which is a part of logistics activities. However, when operationalizing logistical integration, the focus is usually on the coordination and integration of various logistics functions and processes across the supply chain, rather than solely on the distribution aspect.

The other three options - responsiveness, variance reduction, and inventory reduction - are commonly recognized as operational objectives in the context of logistical integration within a supply chain. Responsiveness refers to the ability to quickly and effectively respond to changes in customer demand or other supply chain disruptions. Variance reduction aims to minimize variability and uncertainty in the supply chain, such as reducing lead times, variability in transportation or production, and quality variability. Inventory reduction focuses on optimizing inventory levels and minimizing inventory costs while maintaining customer service levels. These objectives are often considered important in achieving efficient and effective logistical integration within a supply chain context.

12. 1. What is inventory? 2. Give at least 3 examples of inventories.​


Answer:

Manufacturers deal with three types of inventory. They are raw materials (which are waiting to be worked on), work-in-progress (which are being worked on), and finished goods (which are ready for shipping).

Answer:

1. "WHAT IS INVENTORY?"

-a complete list of items such as property, goods in stock, or the contents of a building.

2. "3 Examples of Inventories"

-Raw Materials:

Raw Materials:Raw materials are the materials a company uses to create and finish products. When the product is completed, the raw materials are typically unrecognizable from their original form, such as oil used to create shampoo.

-Components:

Components:Components are similar to raw materials in that they are the materials a company uses to create and finish products, except that they remain recognizable when the product is completed, such as a screw.

-Work In Progress (WIP):

Work In Progress (WIP):WIP inventory refers to items in production and includes raw materials or components, labor, overhead and even packing materials.

24. What is the difference between a physical inventory from a perpetual inventory.


Answer:

Perpetual inventory continuously tracks and records items as they are added to or subtracted from the inventory. And it keeps track of the cost of goods purchased and sold. Physical inventory uses a periodic schedule to manually count and record items and keep track of the cost of what's bought and sold.
