7. how can your promote, conserve, and give value to the different literary works, especially our Philippine literature?​(ANS ASAP)


Answer:

As a student, I know that I am not in the position to make big waves in the world of literature. However, there are still ways for me to promote, conserve, and value literary works.

For one thing, I believe that simply consuming these works of art it is already an act that supports the local literature industry. This is especially true if this involves buying a copy of a specific book or other published work since it will directly help the authors and publishing houses. Unfortunately, I have limited funds to do this. Apart from this, I try to use and borrow from libraries whenever possible. By doing so, it can push these public institutions to continue supporting local artists. It is also a good way to show interest in what they do, which will encourage even more support from others.

I also steer clear of piracy as much as possible. These acts are not helpful to the local industry, which is still a business that needs to make money. Piracy does not give the hard-working writers and publishers compensation for the work that they do. If piracy is rampant, it will discourage publishing houses from releasing new works. As you can imagine, this will be bad for the industry.

I can support legislators who want to improve the state of Philippine literature as well. Right now, I am not yet old enough to vote. Despite this, I can express my support for these politicians online and by discussing the topic with my friends and loved ones. I will also advocate the need for a translation industry, which is badly needed so that the books can be more accessible to people of all ages and economic backgrounds. By doing this, I am spreading the word and hopefully contributing to the promotion of our local literature.

It is also a good idea to share Philippine literature to friends, both locals and foreigners. Many people are still under the assumption that locally-made media is somehow inferior to ones made abroad. If I keep talking about it and introducing these works to others, I can showcase the number of talented people we have here. When interest in these works increase, it will allow the industry to flourish even more than it already has.

17. 1.how can unity and collaboration help resolve the social, moral and economic issues? 2.why do we have to understand and promote Philippine literature? ​


Answer:

Collaboration, coordination,cooperation and unity lie at the core of interorganizational activities. To address the confusion regarding the definitions of these three terms, recent works have proposed redefinitions. Although these proposals address an important concern, we believe that they might be premature because (1) they do not build on a systematic examination of how these terms have been used in the literature and (2) they seem to narrow the focus to a given theory and alliances only, which might unduly restrict the meaning of the terms defined. In this paper, we review the definitions of the three terms as they appear in nine top journals in the general management literature (1948-2017). By studying the definitions, we identify three interactional dimensions that are present to different extents in collaboration, coordination, and cooperation: attitude, behavior, and outcome. Our systematic review confirms the confusion and lack of parsimony in the extant definitions. The overlap in the content of these dimensions across the three terms does not provide a basis for distinctively defining collaboration, coordination, and cooperation. Thus, we further draw on our review to identify two discriminating dimensions that allow us to distinguish these three terms: the temporal stage and the type of goal. Our review contributes to theoretical development by offering a conceptual redefinition of the three terms that renders them distinct and thus facilitates knowledge accumulation and theory development. Moreover, the set of interactional and discriminating dimensions generates a host of managerially relevant research questions about a wide range of interorganizational relationships.
