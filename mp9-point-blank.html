12. Car 1 starts from point A and heads for point B at 60 mph. Fifteen minutes later, car 2 leaves the same point A and heads for point B at 75 mph. How long before car 2 overtakes car 1?


[tex] \huge \mathbb{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

[tex] \!\!\begin{array}{l} \bold{Given:}\: \begin{cases}\: \textsf{Speed of car 1 = 60 mph} \\ \: \textsf{Speed of car 2 = 75 mph} \end{cases} \\ \\ \bold{Required:}\: \textsf{Time t (in hour/s) for car 2 to} \\ \quad\qquad \qquad\textsf{overtake car 1} \\ \\ \textsf{Since both of them travel at the same direction,} \\ \textsf{at some point when car 2 overtakes car 1, the} \\ \textsf{distance they travelled will be equal.} \\ \\ \textsf{We know that }\bold{Distance} = \bold{Speed}\times \bold{Time}. \\ \\ \textsf{Car 1 travels 15 minutes or }\sf \frac{1}{4}\ hr\ longer\ so \\ \\ \bold{Equation:}\: \sf 75t = 60\left(t + \frac{1}{4}\right) \\ \\ \textsf{Solving for t,} \\ \\ \quad \sf 75t = 60t + 15 \\ \\ \quad \sf 75t - 60t = 15 \\ \\ \quad \sf 15t = 15 \\ \\ \:\:\therefore \boxed{\sf t = 1\ hour} \end{array} [/tex]
