13. 13. In the following image, observe the triangles ABC and AOC and the circle.Determine the measure of angle OCB.​


Answer:

Angle OCB is 20°.

Step-by-step explanation:

Consideration:

As it was not mentioned in the statement, consider the point O center of the circle.

Definitions: Central angle is the one whose vertex is at the center of the circle, so the angle AOB is central and is relative to the arc AB. An inscribed angle is one whose vertex is on the circle, so angle ACB is inscribed on the circle and is relative to the arc AB.Complementary angles are those whose measures add up to 90°.

Properties:Property of the central angle: (P₁) The measure of the central angle is equal to the measure of the arc relative to it.Property of the inscribed angle: (P₂) The measure of the inscribed angle is equal to half the measure of the arc relative to it.An isosceles triangle has two congruent sides and two congruent angles adjacent to its base.

Resolution:

If O is the center of the circle then:

OA, OB and OC are radii of the circle.AB is the diameter of the circle and therefore the arc AB (ncah) measures 180°.

If OA and OC are radii of the circle then they are congruent, the triangle AOC is isosceles and its base angles (AC) are congruent, so:

m(∠OAC) = m(∠ACO) = 70°

Note that angle ACB is inscribed in the circle and relative to arc AB (180°), so according to property P₂, the measure of angle ACB is half the measure of arc AB.

m(∠ACB) = 180 ÷ 2

m(∠ACB) = 90°

Determine the measure of angle OCB.

If angle ACB measures 90° then angles ACO and OCB are complementary:

m(∠ACO) + m(∠OCB) = 90° ⟹ Substitute the measurement of AĈO.

70 + m(∠OCB) = 90° ⟹ Subtract 70 from both sides.

Therefore, the answer is m(∠OCB) = 20°

Nomenclature:

ncah: counterclockwise cyclic notation.

m(∠OCB): measure of angle OCB.

We know that angle AOC is 140° and that OX divides it into two equal parts. This means that angle AOX and angle COX are congruent and each measure (1/2) × 140° = 70°. Therefore, angle AOX is 70°.

Let angle XOC be x°. Then we have:

angle AOX = 2x - 9°

We also know that angle AOC is 120°, so:

angle AOX + angle XOC = (1/2) × 120°

Substituting the first equation into the second equation, we get:

(2x - 9°) + x = 60°

Simplifying, we get:

3x - 9° = 60°

Adding 9° to both sides, we get:

3x = 69°

Dividing both sides by 3, we get:

x = 23°

Therefore, angle XOC is 23° and angle AOX is:

angle AOX = 2x - 9°
