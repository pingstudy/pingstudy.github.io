13. what is the difference of coop with a business enterprise?​


Answer:

A co-op business is a type of corporation. Instead of being owned by shareholders, a cooperative is owned by the people who use the company's services or by its employees. Nobody owns a larger portion of the business than anyone else, so all votes are weighted equally.

Explanation:

How do cooperatives differ from businesses?

Having more in common with a traditional business than, say, a nonprofit organization, a cooperative distinguishes itself by a member ownership, benefits, and control model which puts power in the hands of the customers rather than a single owner or small group of partners.

17. 9. How many committees can be formed by choosing 4 men from an organization of a membership of15 men?​


Answer

There are 15 ways to choose the first member, 14 ways to choose the second member, 13 for the third member, and 12 for the fourth member.

However, choosing A, then B, then C, then D is the same as choosing D, C, A, B, which is the same as choosing B, C, A, D, etc.

Therefore, we have counted each possible committee 24 times, since there are 4! = 24 ways of rearranging any four committee members.

To account for this, we will divide by 24 so we only count each possible committee once.

15⋅14⋅13⋅1224  

=1365  possible committees
