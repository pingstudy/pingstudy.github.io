17. A zip code contains 4 digits. How many different zip can be made with the digits 0-9 if no digit is used more than once and the first digit is not 0​


Solution:

There are 10 digits from 0 to 9.

First, there are 10 possible digits for the last digit.

_ _ _ 10

Second, there are 9 possible digits for the third digit because no digit is used more than once.

_ _ 9 10

Third, there are 8 possible digits for the second digit because no digit is used more than once.

_ 8 9 10

Fourth, there are 6 possible digits for the first digit because no digit is used more than once and the first digit is not zero.

6 8 9 10

Finally, we can now compute for the number of permutations of zip code.

P = 6 × 8 × 9 × 10

[tex]\boxed{\text{P = 4320 zip codes}}[/tex]
