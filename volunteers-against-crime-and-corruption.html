1. Ano ang Layunin o Adbokasiya ng "VOLUNTEERS AGAINST CRIME AND CORRUPTION (V.A.C.C) "?​


Answer:

SA paglipas ng taon, naging saksi ang Volunteers Against Crime and Corruption (VACC) sa samu’t saring pangyayari sa ating lipunan, lalo na sa mga bagay na kung saan ang buhay at kapakanan ng ating mga kababayan ay naaapektuhan.

Hindi na marahil kaila na ang pagkilala ng mga biktima ng krimen sa VACC bilang kaagapay ng mga ito sa pagsulong ng hustisya sa ating lipunan ay lubos na nagpapalakas sa diwa at layunin ng VACC na pag-ibayuhin pa ang laban para sa mga biktima ng krimen at katiwalian.

Ngunit sa paglipas ng mga taon hindi lamang pawang mga biktima ng krimen lamang ang naging pangunahing layunin ng VACC bilang isang pribadong samahan na nagsimula upang tulungan ang mga naaapi ng isang mahinangn sistema ng pangkatarungan.

Ginampanan na rin ng VACC ang tungkulin nito sa lipunan sa pagpuna sa mga pangyayaring humuhubog sa ating lipunan sa pamamagitan ng mga gawain ng ating pamahalaan at ang mga ahensiyang nakikipagtulungan dito upang itaguyod ang kapakanan ng taumbayan.

Ang kahinaan o criminal justice system, sa ating lipunan ay naging isa sa mga pangunahing dahilan kung bakit ang hustisya sa ating bansa ay mahirap makamtan ng ating mga napakararaming kapus-palad na kababayan, lalo na’t kapag ang kalaban ng mga ito sa kanilang mga kaso ay pawang mga mayayaman o maimpluwensiya

16. why was the crime of rape amended as a crime against persons instead of crime against chastity? explain.​


Answer:

The crime of rape has historically been classified as a crime against chastity, which implies that the primary harm caused by rape is the violation of the victim's sexual purity or morality. This classification has been criticized for trivializing the harm caused by rape and for perpetuating harmful gender stereotypes that blame the victim for the crime.

In recent years, many countries have amended their laws to classify rape as a crime against persons rather than a crime against chastity. This change reflects a growing recognition that rape is a violent crime that causes physical and psychological harm to the victim, regardless of their sexual history or morality.

The classification of rape as a crime against persons has several important implications. First, it acknowledges that the harm caused by rape is not limited to the victim's sexual purity or morality, but extends to their physical and mental well-being. This can help to reduce the stigma associated with being a victim of rape and encourage more victims to come forward and report the crime.

Second, classifying rape as a crime against persons emphasizes the importance of the victim's consent in sexual activity. This can help to shift the focus away from the victim's behavior and towards the perpetrator's actions, which is critical for holding perpetrators accountable for their crimes.

Finally, reclassifying rape as a crime against persons is an important step towards achieving gender equality and promoting a culture of respect for women's rights. By recognizing the harm caused by rape and taking steps to prevent and punish this crime, societies can work towards creating a safer and more equitable world for all.
