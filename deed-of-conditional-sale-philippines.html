1. 1. What is the essence of consent in the contract of sale? 2. What will happen to the contract of sale when the price is inadequate? Can there be a sale without a price? 3. Enumerate and define the kinds of contract of sale.4. Distinguish Contract of Sale and Contract to Sell. 5. Distinguish Contract to sell and conditional deed of sale.​


Answer:

1. The essence of consent in the contract of sale refers to the mutual agreement between the buyer and seller on the essential terms of the sale, including the subject matter, price, and conditions of delivery and payment.

2. If the price is inadequate, the contract of sale may still be valid, but the buyer may be able to seek a price reduction or cancel the contract. There cannot be a sale without a price, as the price is a necessary element of the agreement.

3.The kinds of contract of sale include:

Absolute sale: a sale where ownership of the goods is transferred immediately to the buyer.

Conditional sale: a sale where ownership of the goods is transferred to the buyer only upon the occurrence of a specified condition.

Sale on approval: a sale where the buyer has the right to inspect the goods and return them within a specified period if they are not satisfactory.

Sale or return: a sale where the buyer has the right to return the goods and receive a refund if they are not sold within a specified period.

Installment sale: a sale where payment is made in installments over a specified period.

4. The main difference between a contract of sale and a contract to sell is that in a contract of sale, ownership of the goods is transferred to the buyer immediately upon the agreement, while in a contract to sell, ownership remains with the seller until certain conditions are met, such as full payment by the buyer.

5. A contract to sell and a conditional deed of sale are similar in that ownership of the goods remains with the seller until certain conditions are met. The difference is that a contract to sell is an agreement to transfer ownership in the future, while a conditional deed of sale is a present transfer of ownership that is subject to certain conditions, such as payment or delivery.

4. Deed of transfer of rights on real property philippines


Philippine Deed Transferring Real Property Rights

A deed of allocated and transferring of rights is a legally binding document used when the assignor, or the party who originally agreed to the terms of the contract, transfers their rights thereunder to another party.

Make a list of and have ready the prerequisites for a title transfer.

Transportation Deed, copies of all signers to the deed's valid IDs, formal acknowledgment from the notary public of the notarization of the deed, Authenticated copy of the title (3 copies), most latest tax declaration, Certified True Copy, Tax Exemption, etc. should all be on hand, along with the original and at least two photocopies.

Send in all the paperwork required for tax calculation.

The Deed of Sale should be signed and stamped before it can be forwarded, along with other required documents, to the Regional Office of the Bureau of Internal Revenue for the jurisdiction that governs your property.

Pay Your Taxes When Due.

It's time to proceed to the Authorized Agent Bank (AAB) that has received BIR approval so that you can pay the necessary Documentary Stamp Tax, Value-Added Tax, and Capital Gains Tax.

Obtain an Authorization Certificate for Registration (CAR)

The National Internal Revenue Code mandates the submission of this certificate, often known as a Certificate of Authorizing Registration or CAR. It serves as confirmation and documentation that all taxes related to the sold property have been paid and resolved properly.

You must deliver the CAR to the treasurer's office in your community

You must now deliver your CAR to the Treasurer's Office in order to pay the Transfer Tax. You have 60 days after the conveyance deed's notarization to pay the transfer tax.

Bring your documents to the Registry of Deeds to register them

Nowadays, registering your property is a need. The following records may now be delivered to the Registry of Deeds

Get a New Tax Declaration from the Local Assessor's Office

Using the new title you obtained from the Registry of Deeds, you may now submit an application for a New Tax Declaration Certificate at the Assessor's Office. The new tax statement indicates that all assessment expenses should be paid by the new owner.

The significance of land titles:

To demonstrate that you are the legitimate owner of a piece of property, you must have a land title. In the Philippines, whether you're buying a house or just a piece of land, full ownership necessitates the transfer of the land title.
