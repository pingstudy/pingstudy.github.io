Answer:

Facts:

Andres Bonifacio was born in Tondo, Manila, on November 30, 1863.

He grew up in slums and had practical experience with the class struggle in society.

He was orphaned at a young age and interrupted his primary education to earn a living as a craftsman and clerk-messenger for foreign commercial firms in Manila.

He was influenced by the writings of José Rizal, Victor Hugo, and other authors.

He joined the Liga Filipina, a group that sought to unite and intensify the national movement for reforms.

When the Liga was dissolved, Bonifacio formed the Katipunan in 1892.

The Katipunan's initiation rites were based on Masonry, but its ideological principles were derived from the French Revolution.

The Katipunan exalted work as the source of all value and focused on the unjust class structure and colonial system in the Philippines.

The Katipunan was discovered in August 1896 and had around 30,000 members at the time.

Bonifacio was arrested, tried, and executed by other Filipino rebels on May 10, 1897.

Opinion:

Bonifacio's upbringing in the slums of Tondo gave him a unique perspective on the class struggle in society.

His early education was disrupted by the need to earn a living, but he was able to educate himself through reading.

The Liga Filipina and the Katipunan were important organizations that sought to unite and mobilize the Philippine people for reform and independence.

The ideology of the Katipunan was influenced by the ideals of the French Revolution and had a materialistic-historical orientation.

The discovery of the Katipunan led to the armed struggle for Philippine independence.

Bonifacio's execution was not solely the result of personal pride, but was also influenced by the class forces and the conflicting strategies of the rebels.
