24. Brenda is twice as old as cindy. If brenda’s age in ten yrs will be five times cindy’s age ten yrs ago, how old is brenda?


Let  B be the age of Brenda and C be the age of Cindy now. So, (B+10) is the age of Brenda 10 years from now, and (C-10) is the age of Cindy ten years ago. 

If Brenda is twice as old as Cindy, then  B=2C. This is our first equation. 

If Brenda's age in ten years will be five times Cindy's age ten years ago, then
(B+10)=5(C-10). This is our second equation.

Substitute the first equation into the second equation. We have

(2C+10)=5(C-10)

Then solve for C.

2C+10=5C-50
2C-5C=-50-10
-3C=-60
C=20

Then solve for B using the first equation.
B=2C
B=2(20)
B=40

Therefore, the age of Brenda now is 40 years.
