Answer:

A little overview :)

The difference between the two kinds of pronouns is intensive pronouns emphasize a preceding noun. It can be removed without changing the meaning the thought of the sentence. Whereas, reflexive pronouns refer back to the subject and is necessary for the sentence to have the same meaning.

Just remember, INTENSive= intense= strong and REFLEXive= reflect=bounce back.

Pronoun is underlined.

1. Early civilizations sprang from the ancient Near East itself.

-INTENSIVE

2. These civilizations were themselves strongly influenced by religion.

- INTENSIVE

3. Some of people of this ancient region devoted themselves to the worship of several gods.

-REFLEXIVE

4.Some engaged themselves in religious sacrifices and rituals.

-REFLEXIVE

5. It was region itself that defined much of the culture of the region.

-INTENSIVE

6. Any historical who involves himself in the study of ancient history will maruel ath the richness of near Eastern culture.

-REFLEXIVE

7. One might find herself engrossed in the scared text of the Hebrews.​

-REFLEXIVE

One tip is to remove the pronoun and identify whether it still has the same meaning or not. If it does, it is intensive and if it does not its reflexive.

Cambodia

In 1863 Cambodia's King Norodom asked France to make Cambodia a protectorate. In 1867 Thailand renounced sovereignty over Cambodia and the French protectorate was officially recognized in Cambodia. Then Cambodia was integrated into French Indochina in 1887 along with the protectorates of the French colonies in Vietnam (Cochinchina, Annam and Tonkin).

Laos

Laos was initially dominated by the Nanzhao Kingdom, which was continued in the 14th century by the local kingdom of Lan Xang which lasted until the 18th century, after Thailand took control of the kingdom. Then France took control of the region in the 19th century and incorporated it into French Indochina in 1893. After Japanese occupation during World War II, the country became independent in 1949 under the name Kingdom of Laos under King Sisavang Vong.

The rest of Thailand has never been colonized by any country, while Myanmar has been under British rule for 62 years.
