15. Using the triangle in inequality theorem 3, what inequality will you write to check whether segments with lenghts S1,S2,S3 form a triangle if S1 < S2 < S3 ?


Answer:

[tex]S_{3}-S_{1}<S_{2}<S_{1}+S_{3}[/tex] , [tex]S_{3}>S_{1}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

The triangle inequality states that:

Every side of a triangle is smaller than the sum of the other two.

This means that three inequalities must be true, like here for segments [tex]S_{1},S_{2} ,S_{3}[/tex] we have the following :

[tex]S_{1}<S_{2}+S_{3}[/tex]

[tex]S_{2}<S_{3}+S_{1}[/tex]

[tex]S_{3}<S_{1}+S_{2}[/tex]

The three statements above are equivalent to the next statement:

[tex]S_{3}-S_{1}<S_{2}<S_{1}+S_{3}[/tex]

where [tex]S_{3}-S_{1}>0[/tex] from the hypothesis that [tex]S_{1}<S_{3}[/tex]

Of course the same statement would be true if we switched [tex]S_{2}[/tex] in the middle for any other side of the triangle.

The triangle inequality is necessary for three segments to form a triangle.

In case that the equality [tex]S_{1}=S_{2}+S_{3}[/tex] is true, then there can be no triangle, but a segment which is the sum of the other two.
