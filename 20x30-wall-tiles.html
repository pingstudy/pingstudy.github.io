1. a rectangular room has square tiles on the floor. There are 14 tiles along one wall and 17 tiles along an adjacent wall. how many tiles cover the floor of the room?​


Answer:

238

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the number of floor tiles, find the area of a rectangle by using [tex]A=lw[/tex]. Since we cannot find which one is length and which one is height, just multiply both numbers. Since [tex]14 \times 17 = 238[/tex], there are 238 tiles covering.


2. An interior wall face with a height of 3.60 meters and a length of 4.80 meters is to be finished with wall tiles. How many wall tiles are needed if the dimension of each wall tile has a width of 0.20 meters, height of 1.20 meters and thickness of 1/2 inch.?


Area of Plane Figure Applied in Real Life Situation

Answer: 72 tiles

Explanation

The given problem is an application of solving area of the plane figure in real life situation. You will apply your basic knowledge in mathematics and geometry especially in solving area of rectangle and how many items can be occupied in the space provided.

The given problem, "An interior wall face with a height of 3.60 meters and a length of 4.80 meters is to be finished with wall tiles. How many wall tiles are needed if the dimension of each wall tile has a width of 0.20 meters, height of 1.20 meters and thickness of 1/2 inch.?"

Solution and Discussion

In mathematics, solving area of plane figure is commonly used in architectural and construction industry including floor covering, such as carpets and tiles. Calculation of dimensions and area measurements applied in wallpaper and paint. Solving area also applied in industry including machineries, clothing and other items that used length and width of the figure.

How to find the area of a rectangle? Area of a rectangle is the space occupied by the shape express in square units. Finding area is the product of length and width (A = lw)

Step-by-step Solution

Step 1: Identify all the given in the problem

Dimension of interior wall face

Height of 3.60 meters and a length of 4.80 meters

Dimension of tiles

Width of 0.20 meters, height of 1.20 meters and thickness of 1/2 inch

Step 2: Identify what is being ask

How many wall tiles are needed in the interior wall face?

Step 3: Solve the surface area of the interior wall

A = lw, where l = 3.60 meters and w = 4.80 meters

A = (3.60)(4.80)

A = 17.28 m²

Step 4: Solve the area of each tile

A = lw, where l = 0.20 meters and h = 1.20 meters

Note: Height of the tiles is equal to the width of the tiles and thickness is not needed in solving area.

A = (0.20)(1.20)

A = 0.24 m²

Step 5: Solve for the total number of tiles

No. of tiles = Area of wall ÷ area of each tile

= 17.28 ÷ 0.24

= 72 tiles

Thus, it requires 72 tiles to completely cover the interior wall face.

13. A wall facing, usually of wood, cut stone, or ceramic tile, that is carried only partway up a wall.


Wainscoting is about a wall facing that is only carried partially up a wall, typically made of wood, cut stone, or ceramic tile.

Main Types of Wainscoting:

Board and Batten Wainscoting - Wainscoting with vertical boards used to conceal the seams or styles is known as board and batten wainscoting. Distinctive in style thanks to its consistent pattern of broad vertical molding boards, or battens. These battens are typically positioned 6 to 10 inches apart and range in width from 4 to 6 inches. Additionally, battens were used to hide the joints between the boards; extra battens might be inserted between the joints for aesthetic purposes.

Raised Panel Wainscoting - In order to insulate homes, raised panel wainscoting was initially made popular in England in the 17th century. The formal dining and living rooms are the most typical locations for this sort of wainscoting, which is also the oldest and most prevalent.

Flat Panel Wainscoting - Panels in flat panel wainscoting are positioned behind stiles and rails. This form of wainscoting has a more modern appearance because it employs boards without beveled edges or molding, which makes them appear deeper than the stiles and rails.

Overlay Wainscoting - Combining flat panel and raised panel wainscoting styles results in overlay wainscoting. Prior to centering additional panels on top of flat panels, they are first installed. For added elegance, the lining of these extra panels can be made of molding.

Beadboard Wainscoting - The raised beads and long, continuous vertical grooves that define beadboard wainscoting are spaced around every inch to two inches apart. Thin, individual boards are arranged next to one another to create beadboard wainscoting. The tongue and groove system are used to connect these little boards, which are typically between 32 and 48 inches long and 4 inches wide.
