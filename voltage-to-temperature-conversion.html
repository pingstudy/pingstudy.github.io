7. It is required that the loading of 3KW be maintained in a heating element at an initial temperature of 20°C, a voltage of 220V is necessary for the purpose. After the element has settled down to steady state, it is found the voltage of 240V is necessary to maintain the 3KW loading; the element resistance temperature coefficient is 0.0006 per degree Centigrade at 20°C. Calculate the final temperature of the heating element​


The final temperature of the heating element​ : 336.85°C

Further explanation

Resistance increases with increasing temperature

Can be formulated :

[tex]\tt R=R_o[1+\alpha(T-T_o)]\\\\T=T_o+\dfrac{\dfrac{R}{R_o}-1 }{\alpha}[/tex]

R = conductor resistance at T

Ro = conductor resistance at temperature-To(20°C or 0°C)

T = final temperatur

To = reference temperature

α = the element resistance temperature coefficient

We can use Ohm's Law for basic calculation :

W(watts)=E(volts) x I(amperes)

E(volts) = I(amperes) x R(ohms)

Initial state

W=3KW=3000 watts

V=220 volts

Then the current (I) :

[tex]\tt I_o=\dfrac{W}{E}\\\\I_o=\dfrac{3000}{220}=13.636~A[/tex]

The resistance :

[tex]\tt R_o=\dfrac{E}{I}\\\\R_o=\dfrac{220}{13.636}=16.133~ohms[/tex]

Final state

[tex]\tt I=\dfrac{W}{E}\\\\I=\dfrac{3000}{240}=12.5~A[/tex]

[tex]\tt R=\dfrac{E}{I}\\\\R=\dfrac{240}{12.5}=19.2~ohms[/tex]

The final temperature

[tex]\tt T=20^oC+\dfrac{\dfrac{19.2}{16.133}-1 }{0.0006}\\\\T=20^oC+\dfrac{1.19011-1}{0.0006}\\\\T=20^oC+316.85\\\\T=\boxed{\bold{336.85^oC}}[/tex]
