Answer:

If a mineral has platy or micaceous cleavage, it means it breaks along one set of parallel planes into thin, flat pieces! Many minerals break into fragments bounded by two or more sets of intersecting planes which are parallel to a common axis. The remaining surfaces are irregular.

5. The measure to avoid breakage ang do's and don'ts of equipment handling mo​


Answer:

To avoid breakage and ensure proper equipment handling, here are some general do's and don'ts to follow:

Do's:

Read and follow the manufacturer's instructions for handling and using the equipment.

Use the equipment for its intended purpose only and avoid overloading or exceeding its capacity.

Use protective gear, such as gloves or goggles, when handling equipment with sharp or hazardous parts.

Keep the equipment clean and free of debris or contaminants that could damage it.

Inspect the equipment regularly for signs of wear and damage, and address any issues immediately.

Don'ts:

Don't use equipment that is damaged or malfunctioning, as this could cause injury or further damage.

Don't force equipment parts to move if they are stuck or jammed, as this could cause breakage or damage.

Don't handle equipment roughly or drop it, as this could cause breakage or damage.

Don't use equipment near water or other liquids unless it is specifically designed for such use.

Don't leave equipment unattended or in an unstable position, as it could fall or cause injury.

By following these guidelines, you can help ensure the safe and effective handling of equipment and avoid costly and dangerous breakages or damage.

24. weak machine troublesFirst week•Needle thread breakage•Skipped stitches•Seam pucker•Looper thread breakage•Fabric jam CausesSolutionsPlease pakisagot naman po ​


Answer:

Needle tread breakage

Causes– Improper threading, incorrect or damage needle, bad thread, old thread, upper tension is too tight, bobbin tension is too tight, burrs on the needle plate, burrs on the hook assembly, and more.Solution– Set the bottom tension to a minimum but still forms a consistent stitch.

Skipped Stitches

Causes– Skipped stitches are usually caused by an old or worn needle.Solution– make sure that the thread is in the tension disk and the spoll is placed correctly on the machine.

Seam Pucker

Causes– Tension pucker is caused while sewing with too much tension thereby causing a stretch in the thread.Solution– Reduce the tension applied to the thread while it is being wound onto the lock stitch bobbin.

Looper thread breakage

Causes– Tension too tight or bobbin over running.Solution– Adjust bobbin case tension. Insert a washer or spring to prevent over running.

Fabric Jam

Causes– Lack of tension on the upper thread.Solution– remove all the jammed thread.
