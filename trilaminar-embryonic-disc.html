30. what is the embryonic stem cell?​


What is the embryonic stem cell?​

Embryonic stem cells are pluripotent stem cells formed from the inner cell mass of a blastocyst, a pre-implantation embryo in the early stages of development. 4–5 days after conception, human embryos reach the blastocyst stage, which consists of 50–150 cells. Isolating the embryoblast, or inner cell mass (ICM), causes the blastocyst to be destroyed, raising ethical concerns about whether embryos in the pre-implantation stage of development have the same moral considerations as embryos in the post-implantation stage.

Many laboratories are actively focusing their efforts on the therapeutic potential of embryonic stem cells, with clinical usage as a goal. Diabetes and heart disease therapy are two possible applications. The cells are being researched for therapeutic medicines, genetic diseases models, and cellular/DNA repair. However, there have been reports of negative consequences in the research and clinical procedures, such as tumors and undesired immune reactions.
