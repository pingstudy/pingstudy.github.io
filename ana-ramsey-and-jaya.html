22. wade in the water by ramsey lewis meaning each stanza​


Answer:

"Wade in the Water" is a traditional African-American spiritual song that has been recorded and performed by many artists, including Ramsey Lewis. The lyrics of the song are open to interpretation and can hold different meanings for different listeners. However, here is one possible interpretation of the song's stanzas:

Stanza 1:

"Wade in the water

Wade in the water, children

Wade in the water

God's gonna trouble the water"

This stanza is believed to refer to the biblical story of the Israelites crossing the Red Sea to escape slavery in Egypt. The phrase "trouble the water" could mean that God is intervening to bring justice to the oppressed and to help the enslaved people escape their captors.

Stanza 2:

"See that band all dressed in white

God's gonna trouble the water

The leader looks like the Israelite

God's gonna trouble the water"

This stanza may refer to the Underground Railroad, a network of secret routes and safe houses that helped slaves escape to freedom. The band dressed in white could represent abolitionists or conductors of the Underground Railroad who guide the slaves to safety. The reference to the Israelite leader Moses, who led the Israelites out of Egypt, could suggest that the conductor is leading the slaves to freedom like Moses led the Israelites.

Stanza 3:

"Who's that young girl dressed in red?

God's gonna trouble the water

Must be the children that Moses led

God's gonna trouble the water"

This stanza may continue the reference to the Israelites and Moses, with the young girl in red representing Miriam, Moses' sister who led the Israelites in song and dance after they crossed the Red Sea. The phrase "the children that Moses led" could refer to the Israelites who followed Moses out of Egypt.

Overall, "Wade in the Water" is a song that speaks to the struggles of African-Americans and their quest for freedom and equality. The lyrics suggest that God is on the side of the oppressed and will intervene to bring justice and freedom to those who are enslaved or oppressed.

Is jaya soprano or alto

Jaya soprano is a soprano Voice. Here are the explanation.

Jaya is listed as a dramatic soprano, in particular. That makes it around low A (A3) to "big C" in a voice  range (C6). This voice typically has a lower tessitura and a deeper timbre than other sopranos. For further information: A soprano has the highest vocal range of all forms and is a type of classically female song voice. The style of soprano is typically split in soprano, soubrette, lyrical, spinto and dramatic.

30. what is the difference between solo model and ramsey model​


Answer:

The Ramsey–Cass–Koopmans model differs from the Solow–Swan model in that the choice of consumption is explicitly microfounded at a point in time and so endogenizes the savings rate. As a result, unlike in the Solow–Swan model, the saving rate may not be constant along the transition to the long run steady state
