3. Example of Simple triage

Example of Simple triage

▪︎The definition of triage is a medical process where patients are sorted according to their need for care and the likely benefit that care will provide in order to determine what order in which to treat them. 5. The clinic nurse is preparing to test the visual acuity of a client using a snellen chart. which of the following identifies the accurate procedure for this visual acuity test


Answer:

Visual acuity is assessed in one eye at a time, and then in both eyes together with the client comfortably standing or sitting. The right eye is tested with the left eye covered; then the left eye is tested with the right eye covered. Both eyes then are tested together. Visual acuity is measured with or without corrective lenses and the client stands at a distance of 20ft. from the chart.

28. Roles and responsibilities of Triage Nurses​


Answer:

Responsibilities of a Triage Nurse include :

● Perform patient assessment.

● Reassess patients who are waiting.

● Manage and communicate with patients in waiting room.

● Provide education to patients and families when necessary.

● Sort patients into priority groups according to guidelines.

Explanation:

HOPE IT HELPS ●□●Roles and responsibilities of Triage Nurses​

Here are the Roles and responsibilities of Triage Nurses​:

Triage nurses are responsible for a wide range of tasks. They must place a premium on patient care. They must also collect vital signs, assess patient status, obtain a medical history and personal information from the patient, and continuously monitor the patient's condition. This information assists them in administering the proper medications and therapy techniques for the patient. To coordinate these tactics, triage nurses must collaborate closely with other healthcare providers. Finally, triage nurses must record patient status, treatment, and prescriptions, as well as update electronic medical records as needed.

To survive, triage nurses must have a distinct skill set. It is crucial that they have time-sensitive clinical decision-making abilities, critical thinking talents, and the capacity to effectively diagnose patient issues – all while demonstrating the ability to manage in a high-stress setting.

29. how important is triaging in responding to emergency situation​


Answer:

The process of triage allows the respondents of disaster, who do not have enough resources to treat everyone, to prioritize care services, so that most services are provided to the greatest number of injured people, and this is essentially the philosophy of doing triage in disasters and mass casualty incidents


30. what are the 4 type of triage tape​


Answer:

This 4 pack of adhesive triage tape some with green (minor), yellow (delayed), red (immediate), and black (morgue). This tape is excellent for marking out triage areas and can also be used around the waist or arms of victims to indicate the extent of an injury.
