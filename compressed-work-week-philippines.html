4. guide questions:1. how refrigerator works?____________________2. what happened to the hot gas produced by compression?____________________3. when does the cycle repeat?____________________​


Answer:

1.HOW REFRIGERATOR WORKS?

Refrigerators work by causing the refrigerant circulating inside them to change from a liquid into a gas.

2.What happened to the hot gas produced by compression?

The hot gas produced by compression in the refrigeration cycle is transferred to the condenser. The condenser then dissipates the heat from the refrigerant to the outside air. What remains is the liquid. The refrigerant in effect turns the vapor into hot liquid as a result of the high pressure and reduction in temperature. More details can be found below.

3.When does the cycle repeat?

The refrigeration cycle begins by the heat being absorbed by the refrigerant, cooling the air inside the evaporator (low pressure). The cooled air is then transferred to the compressor (high pressure). When air is compressed, it heats up. The greater the heat, the more high pressure gas is produced. This is the gas that is described in the process above. Now, as refrigerant passes through high pressure to low pressure, the expansion valve acts a regulator to the process. With reduced pressure, the expansion valve allows the liquid refrigerant to be at its coolest. Remember that when pressure is added, the temperature increases and vice versa.

8. The work needed to compress a spring by 40 mm is 0.80 J. How will you compute for the force constant of the spring?


Answer:

The force constant of the spring is 1000 N/m

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the force constant of a spring using the concept of force and work on springs.

Formula to use

W = 1/2kx²

where

W     is the work, the unit is in Joules (J)

k       is the force constant of the spring, the unit is in Newton per meter

x       is the length of the spring, the unit is in meter

Given information

x = 40 mm = 0.04 m

W = 0.80 J

k = ?

Solving the problem

Let us solve the spring constant using the given formula

W = 1/2kx²

then, substitute the given data

0.80 J = 1/2k(0.04 m)²

Apply cross-multiplication

k = [tex]\frac{0.80J(2)}{(0.04m)^2}[/tex]

k = 1000 N/m

Therefore, the force constant of the spring is 1000 N/m

19. If a gas compresses with 79.3 J of work and releases gas of 34.50 J of heat, what is the change in energy of the system ?


Answer:

The change in energy of the system can be calculated using the First Law of Thermodynamics, which states that the change in internal energy of a system is equal to the heat added to the system minus the work done by the system:

ΔE = Q - W

where ΔE is the change in energy, Q is the heat added to the system, and W is the work done by the system.

Substituting the given values, we get:

ΔE = 34.50 J - 79.3 J

ΔE = -44.8 J

Therefore, the change in energy of the system is -44.8 J. The negative sign indicates that the system lost energy.

21. Datacompression to reduced and de compression​


In signal processing, data compression, source coding,[1] or bit-rate reduction is the process of encoding information using fewer bits than the original representation.[2] Any particular compression is either lossy or lossless. Lossless compression reduces bits by identifying and eliminating statistical redundancy. No information is lost in lossless compression. Lossy compression reduces bits by removing unnecessary or less important information.[3] Typically, a device that performs data compression is referred to as an encoder, and one that performs the reversal of the process (decompression) as a decoder.
