18. A 3-0.6 pole 50 Hz induction motor is running at 5% slip. What is the speed of motor? a. 950 rpm b. 850 rpm c. 900 rpm d. 1000 rpm​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The rotor speed of a motor is

[tex]\boxed{N_{r} = \frac{120f}{P}(1 - s)}[/tex]

where:

Nr = rotor speed

f = frequency

P = number of poles

s = slip

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Listing the given values

f = 50 Hz

P = 6

s = 5% = 0.05

Solving for Nr

[tex]N_{r} = \dfrac{120f}{P}(1 - s)[/tex]

[tex]N_{r} = \dfrac{120(50)}{6}(1 - 0.05)[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{N_{r} = \text{950 rpm}}[/tex]

Therefore, the rotor speed of the motor is 950 rpm

Answer:

Today, electromagnetic induction is used to power many electrical devices. One of the most widely known uses is in electrical generators (such as hydroelectric dams) where mechanical power is used to move a magnetic field past coils of wire to generate voltage
