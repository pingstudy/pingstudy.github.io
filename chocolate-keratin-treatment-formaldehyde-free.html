26. formaldehyde os a paint remover or not​


Answer:

Formaldehyde is an important indoor air pollutant. It is released mainly from building materials and consumer products. This contaminant has been recognized as having negative effect on humans. Recently, there are developed methods of reducing its emission as well as removal from indoor air. An example of the last approach is the functionalisation of interior wall paints. The influence of such paint on indoor air was preliminarily examined in this work. The obtained results show that, during intermittent exposures, responses of formaldehyde measurement device were lower in the room painted with functionalised paint compared with the room painted using traditional formula paint. This outcome was statistically significant in 67% of the examined cases at the significance level α=0.01. It was found that the serious problem associated with determining the influence of functionalised paint on indoor air, in real conditions, is the low level of formaldehyde concentrations, compared with the detection limit and accuracy of the available measurement devices.

28. Define the following terminologies 1. acetone 2. acetonitrile 3. butyl acetate 4. ethyl acetate 5. formaldehyde 6. phalanges 7. keratin 8. proximal folds 9. tissue 10. proximal part


Answer:

1. Acetone: a colorless, flammable liquid commonly used as a solvent in various industries.

2. Acetonitrile: a colorless, toxic liquid commonly used as a solvent in organic synthesis.

3. Butyl acetate: a colorless, flammable liquid commonly used as a solvent in various industries such as paint and cosmetic production.

4. Ethyl acetate: a colorless, flammable liquid commonly used as a solvent in various industries such as pharmaceuticals and food flavoring.

5. Formaldehyde: a colorless, flammable gas commonly used in the manufacturing of building materials and household products.

6. Phalanges: the bones that make up the fingers and toes of humans.

7. Keratin: a fibrous protein that makes up the structure of hair, nails, and skin.

8. Proximal folds: the folds of skin found at the base of the nail on the finger or toe.

9. Tissue: a group of cells that work together to perform a specific function in an organism.

10. Proximal part: the part of a structure or organ that is closest to the center of the body or point of attachment.
