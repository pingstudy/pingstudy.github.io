11. A force of 410 N is applied to a 25kg box. What is it's acceleration?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The force acting on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F = ma}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for a

F = ma

[tex]\dfrac{F}{m} = \dfrac{ma}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{F}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{410 N}}{\text{25 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{16.4 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration of the box is 16.4 m/s².

23. A force of 410 N is applied to a 25kg box. What is it's acceleration?


Answer:

The acceleration is 16.4 m/s^2.

Explanation:

Acceleration refers to the change in velocity at a given time. Force, on the other hand, is the push or pull. It affects the motion of an object thus affecting the acceleration of the object. Another factor affecting the amount of force to be exerted is the mass of the object. Force is directly proportional to mass and acceleration. It means that as you apply greater force, acceleration will be great also. For example, if you are applying different amounts of force to move two blocks of the same mass, we expect greater acceleration to the block where a greater amount of force was applied. Also, a greater amount of force is needed to change the motion of more massive objects. For example, if you intend to move two blocks of varying mass, that is 5 g and 10g. In order to move them, you need to apply a greater amount of force to a 10g block. Therefore, we can say that mass is the object's resistance to force.

Mathematically, we can express this with the formula F=ma. The problem provides the amount of force and the mass of the object and we are asked to get the acceleration. To get the formula of acceleration, we have to do formula transposition or rearrangement. We need to isolate on one side of the equation the desired quantity which is the acceleration. In order to do that, we have to transpose or transfer mass to the other side of the equation. In transferring, we do the opposite operation. Since it is initially multiplication, as we transfer we have to division making it a= F/m. Applying the formula, we get the answer 16.4 m/s^2. It means that when you apply 410 N to a 25-kg-box, it will accelerate at 16.4m/s^2.

It is important to note also that force is considered as a vector quantity. A vector quantity involves the value or magnitude and the direction.

When it comes to the units, F is expressed in N or kgms/s^2, mass in kg, and acceleration in m/s^2 in this problem. Dividing the force with mass will give us the unit for acceleration.

ACCELERATION

Problem:

A pushcart with a mass of 25kg is pushed on the floor with a force of 75 N.

Answer:The acceleration is 3 m/s².

Formula: [tex]\underline{ \boxed{ \tt \: a = \frac{F}{m} }}[/tex]

To calculate the acceleration, divide the given net Force by the mass. The quotient of it is the answer.

Solution:[tex]\tt \: a = \frac{F}{m} \\ \tt \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: = \frac{75 \: N}{25 \: kg} \\ \tt \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: = \green{3 \: m/s²}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration is 3 m/s².
