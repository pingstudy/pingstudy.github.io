16. two chords of a circle intersects each other. One chord, AB divides the other segment, CD into half while CD divides AB into 5cm and 20cm segments. How long is CD?​Diko po ma imagine yung circle nya


✒️CIRCLE

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

Two chords of a circle intersects each other. One chord, AB divides the other segment, CD into half while CD divides AB into 5cm and 20cm segments. How long is CD?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \:\rm{CD = 20 \, cm} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

Illustration:At the point of intersection, mark it as point E. Since CD is bisected or divided into two, then CE and ED are congruent and equal (represented as x).

Chord AB is divided into 2 parts, AE measuring 20cm and EB as 5cm.Solving:

» By applying chord-chord power theorem in the circle, we get the given as:

[tex] (CE)(CD) = (AE)(EB) [/tex]

» Substitute the given measurements to find the value of x.

[tex] (x)(x) = (20)(5) [/tex]

[tex] x^2 = 100 [/tex]

[tex] \sqrt{x^2} = \sqrt{100} [/tex]

[tex] x = 10 [/tex]

» The value of x or the measure of both CE and ED is 10cm. Find the length of chord CD which is the sum of CE and ED.

[tex] CD = CE + ED [/tex]

[tex] CD = 10cm + 10cm [/tex]

[tex] CD = 20cm [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The length of chord CD is 20 cm

21. What is the emf of a galvanic cell consisting of a Cd2+/Cd half-cell and a Pt/H+/H2 half-cell if [Cd2+] = 0.20 M, [H+] = 0.16 M, and PH2 = 0.80 atm?


The emf of a galvanic cell : 0.376 V

Further explanation  

Cell potential (E °) is the potential difference between the two electrodes in an electrochemical cell.  

The value of E cells uses a reference electrode which is used as a comparison called the Standard Electrode which is the hydrogen-platinum electrode  

The cell potential for nonstandard conditions we can use the Nernst equation  

[tex]\tt E=E^o-\dfrac{RT}{nF}ln~Q[/tex]

Given

[Cd2+] = 0.20 M, [H+] = 0.16 M, and P H₂ = 0.80 atm

Required

The emf

Solution

E° reduction

Cd²⁺ + 2e⁻ ⇒ Cd E° = -0.40 V

2H⁺ + 2e⁻ ⇒ H₂ E° = 0 V

Cd as anode(smaller E°), H⁺ as cathode

Cd ⇒Cd²⁺ + 2e⁻   E° = +0.40 V (oxidation)

2H⁺ + 2e⁻ ⇒ H₂ E° = 0 V (reduction)

Total reaction :

Cd(s) + 2H⁺(aq)⇒Cd²⁺(aq)+ H₂(g)

E° cell = E cathode - E anode

E° cell = 0.4 - 0 = 0.4 V

n = 2 (2 mol electron transferred)

If gases are involved in the cell reaction, their concentrations should be expressed in atm and solid/liquid not included in the calculation of Q

[tex]\tt Q=\dfrac{[Cd^{2+}]P~H_2}{[H^+]^2}[/tex]

Assuming standard conditions T = 298, then the Nernst equation becomes:

[tex]\tt E=E^o-\dfrac{0.0257~V}{n}ln~Q\\\\or\\\\E=E^o-\dfrac{0.0592~V}{n}log~Q[/tex]

Input the value :

[tex]\tt E=0.4-\dfrac{0.0257}{2}ln\dfrac{0.2\times 0.8~atm}{0.16^2}\\\\E=0.376~V[/tex]
