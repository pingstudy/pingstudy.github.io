21. formula of Charlie's law​


Charlie's law, also known as the law of Charles and Gay-Lussac, states that at a constant pressure, the volume of a gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature. It can be expressed mathematically as:

V = kT

where V is the volume of the gas, T is its absolute temperature (in Kelvin), and k is a constant of proportionality.

The equation can also be written as:

V1/T1 = V2/T2

where V1 and T1 are the initial volume and temperature of the gas, and V2 and T2 are the final volume and temperature of the gas, respectively.

Charlie's law is a special case of the ideal gas law, which relates the pressure (P), volume (V), absolute temperature (T), and number of moles (n) of a gas, and can be expressed as:

PV = nRT

where R is the universal gas constant.

A value proposition is a statement that highlights why a client should buy a company's product or use its service by identifying clear, measurable, and demonstrable benefits. Simply expressed, it is a promise to offer value to the client.
