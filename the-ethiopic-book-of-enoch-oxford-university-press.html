7. Which school was built before the aztec civilization?A. Yale UniversityB. Harvard UniversityC. University of OxfordD. None of the above​


University of Oxford is older than the aztecs

As early as 1096, teaching had already started in Oxford. By 1249, the University of Oxford had grown into a full-fledged university, replete with student housing at the school’s three original “halls of residence”—University, Balliol and Merton Colleges.

Oxford isn’t the oldest university, not by a long shot. India’s Nalanda University had already operated for hundreds of years and been burnt down by invaders before Oxford got its act together. But Oxford, as one of the oldest universities in continuous operation, doesn’t feel that old. It’s a product of our time. You can still enroll at Oxford. You can still go to Merton College.

The Aztec civilization of central Mexico, on the other hand, feels like ancient history. Archaeologists dig up Aztec ruins, museums put on Aztec exhibits. But the origination of the Aztec civilization, marked by the founding of the city of Tenochtitlán by the Mexica at Lake Texcoco, didn’t come until 1325. Tenochtitlán was captured by Spanish conquerors in 1521, just 196 years later. The White House has been standing longer than the Aztecs ruled Tenochtitlán.

None of this is intended to pit civilizations against each other. But it’s an interesting way to think about how skewed our understanding of history really is—we have these timelines in our heads that are distorted and compressed, and they don’t always agree with reality. To get a clearer picture of how the world really works, sometimes it helps to keep things in context.

24. theme: politicsExplain why it is the themeanalects of confusion The Chinese Classics, volume 1 (Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1893​


In the "Analects," politics is a significant theme because it addresses issues related to the role of rulers and the principles that should guide their actions. Confucius believed that rulers should serve as moral exemplars and lead by example, and he emphasized the importance of virtues such as benevolence, righteousness, and filial piety in building a just and harmonious society. The "Analects" contains numerous passages that discuss the responsibilities of rulers, the importance of good governance, and the need for rulers to set a moral example for their subjects. For example, in Book 2, Chapter 3 of the "Analects," Confucius says: "The ruler who governs by virtue is like the Pole Star, which remains in its place and all the other stars revolve around it." This passage suggests that a virtuous ruler is the foundation of a well-ordered society, and that other rulers should model their actions on this standard. Therefore, the theme of politics is central to the "Analects" because it addresses the principles and practices that should guide the behavior of rulers.

The correct APA citation format for a book is:

a. Jenkins, H. (2006). Fans, bloggers, and gamers: exploring participatory culture. New York: New York University Press.

Explanation:

In the APA citation format for a book, the author's name is listed first, followed by the publication year in parentheses, the title of the book, and the publication information (place of publication and publisher).

In the example you provided, "a. Jenkins, H. (2006)" is the correct format for listing the author and publication year. The title of the book, "Fans, bloggers, and gamers: exploring participatory culture," is correctly formatted, and the publication information, "New York: New York University Press," is also correct.

Therefore, "a. Jenkins, H. (2006). Fans, bloggers, and gamers: exploring participatory culture. New York: New York University Press." is the correct APA citation format for a book.
